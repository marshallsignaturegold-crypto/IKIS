## 1. Executive Summary

The global salt, gypsum, and mineral water markets represent a combined **$120-150 billion** opportunity (2024), driven by industrial demand, food processing, construction, agriculture, and premium consumer trends. Somaliland possesses significant untapped salt and gypsum deposits near the Berbera port, creating strategic advantages for the AISTA corridor through low-cost extraction, port access, and reduced transport costs to GCC and East African markets. The Corleone Products CPG subsidiary (16 SKUs, $33M Y5 revenue) and Corleone Halal Meats SPV ($12M Y5 revenue, mineral extraction arm) are positioned to capture value from these minerals. The AISTA corridor's port infrastructure, cold chain, and digital settlement systems create competitive advantages over established suppliers (Djibouti, Ethiopia, Egypt, India) through reduced logistics costs, preferential logistics access, and integrated certification. Key risks include extraction infrastructure investment, environmental compliance, and market price volatility.
