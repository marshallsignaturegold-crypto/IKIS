## 1. Executive Summary

This document benchmarks global, regional, and direct-to-consumer (DTC) CPG models against Corleone Products, the 16-SKU consumer packaged goods subsidiary of Corleone Enterprise. Corleone Products targets Year 5 revenue of $33M, with a portfolio split of 60% wholesale to franchise network, 30% DTC, and 10% retail. Manufacturing is co-located in India and UAE, with Somaliland mineral extraction providing a 33–40% below-market cost subsidy on key inputs. The analysis evaluates product portfolio, pricing strategy, distribution architecture, manufacturing footprint, brand equity, innovation pipeline, and margin structure to identify competitive positioning and capability gaps.

**Key Finding:** Corleone Products operates in a hybrid model that is uncommon in the CPG industry: primarily a captive supplier to the Corleone franchise network (60% wholesale), with a secondary DTC channel (30%) and emerging retail presence (10%). This model is structurally different from both global CPG giants (Nestle, Unilever) and pure-play DTC brands (HelloFresh, Blue Apron). The captive wholesale model provides demand stability and cross-subsidy leverage, but the DTC and retail channels require independent brand-building capabilities that are distinct from the B2B franchise supply business.

## 7. Conclusion & Recommendations

Corleone Products occupies a unique position in the CPG landscape: a hybrid B2B foodservice supplier and emerging DTC brand, with structural cost advantages from AISTA integration and Somaliland mineral subsidies. The 16-SKU portfolio is focused and manageable, but brand equity outside the franchise network is minimal. The path to $33M revenue requires: (1) defending the captive B2B franchise supply business (60% of revenue); (2) building the DTC channel (30% of revenue) with e-commerce infrastructure and brand marketing; (3) cautiously expanding retail (10% of revenue) with selective retail partnerships.

**Priority Actions:**

1. **DTC Platform Build (Q1–Q2 Y5):** Launch proprietary e-commerce platform (Shopify Plus or Magento) with subscription management, CRM, and analytics. Budget: $300K.
