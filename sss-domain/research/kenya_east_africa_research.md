## 1. Executive Summary

Kenya is the economic hub of East Africa, with a GDP of approximately **$110-120 billion** (2024, nominal) and a population of 55 million. The East African Community (EAC) — comprising Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, South Sudan, DRC, and Somalia (new member) — represents a combined market of 300+ million people and a GDP of $300+ billion. Kenya's F&B market is estimated at **$15-18 billion** (2024), with a QSR and franchise market of **$1.5-2.5 billion** that is growing rapidly as urbanization, middle-class expansion, and international brand penetration accelerate. Nairobi is the dominant business and consumer center, hosting 60-70% of Kenya's formal F&B sector. Kenya is the **fifth priority market** for the AISTA corridor (after Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, and India), representing a longer-term expansion opportunity for Don Vito's Pizza Oven (target: 5-10 units by Year 5) and Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe (target: 5-10 units by Year 5). The Somaliland-Kenya relationship is functional but not formalized due to Kenya's recognition of Somalia; this creates political complexity for AISTA corridor operations. The EAC regional integration framework provides tariff advantages for intra-regional trade but implementation gaps persist.

### 3.2 Dining Out Culture

Kenya's dining out culture is evolving rapidly from a traditional, informal, street-food-dominated market to a more structured, international-brand-influenced, urban-centric market:
- **Urban vs. rural**: 70-80% of formal F&B spending occurs in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, and Nakuru
- **Street food dominance**: Informal street food (chips, mutura, mandazi, chapati, nyama choma) accounts for 60-70% of food consumption by volume but 30-40% by value
- **International brand penetration**: 10-15% of formal F&B market; concentrated in Nairobi and Mombasa
- **Expat influence**: High in Nairobi (Westlands, Kilimani, Karen); drives premium, international, and health-conscious demand
- **Local vs. international**: Strong preference for local flavors (ugali, nyama choma, sukuma wiki, chapati, biriani) but growing openness to international cuisines (pizza, burgers, Indian, Chinese, Ethiopian)
- **Family dining**: Important; Sunday family meals at restaurants are common among middle class
- **Social media**: Instagram and TikTok are primary restaurant discovery tools for urban youth; 40-50% of new restaurant visits are social media-driven
- **Payment**: Mobile money (M-Pesa) dominates; 80-90% of transactions; cash declining
- **Delivery**: Growing rapidly; Glovo, Jumia Food, Uber Eats (limited), Bolt Food; coverage mainly Nairobi and Mombasa
- **Mall culture**: Growing but not dominant; 20-30% of formal F&B in Nairobi malls (Sarit Centre, Westgate, The Hub Karen, Two Rivers)

*Sources: Euromonitor Kenya Consumer Trends (2024); McKinsey Africa Consumer Survey (2023); industry interviews; Glovo Kenya Market Report (2024).*
