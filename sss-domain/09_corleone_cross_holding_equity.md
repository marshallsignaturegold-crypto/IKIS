## 2. TRANSFER PRICING FRAMEWORK

### 2.1 OECD Arm's-Length Compliance Strategy

All intercompany transactions are priced using the **Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP) method** with the following documentation:

| Transaction | CUP Source | Arm's-Length Range | Selected Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Halal meat (frozen, processed) | UAE halal meat import benchmarks (ESMA) | $3.80–$4.60/kg | $2.80/kg | Internal cost + 5% markup; justified by captive sourcing from own Somaliland facility |
| Mineral water (bottled) | Somaliland groundwater extraction + regional bottled water | $0.70–$1.00/bottle | $0.55/bottle | Internal cost + 5% markup; captive mineral extraction |
| Salt/gypsum (packaging raw) | Regional salt export prices (Djibouti, Ethiopia) | $100–$140/ton | $72/ton | Internal cost + 5% markup; captive mineral extraction |
| Pizza sauces/toppings | Indian CPG wholesale benchmarks | $10.00–$15.00/unit | $10.65/unit | Wholesale midpoint minus 15% volume discount for captive offtake |
| Bakery mixes/packaging | Indian CPG wholesale benchmarks | $6.50–$9.90/unit | $6.97/unit | Wholesale midpoint minus 15% volume discount for captive offtake |
| Marketing/content services | Regional F&B marketing agency rates | $1.50–$3.50/unit | $1.75/unit | Agency rate minus 30% for captive brand alignment |

### 2.2 Transfer Pricing Documentation Requirements

To comply with OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines (2022) and local tax authorities:

1. **Functional Analysis** (per transaction):
   - Functions performed by each entity
   - Assets employed (tangible and intangible)
   - Risks assumed (market, credit, operational, financial)

2. **Economic Analysis**:
   - Comparable uncontrolled transactions (external benchmarks)
   - Profitability analysis (residual profit split for brand IP)
   - Sensitivity analysis (commodity price, FX, volume variations)

3. **Master File** (Group-level):
   - Organizational structure
   - Business description
   - Intangible assets
   - Intercompany financial activities
   - Financial and tax positions

4. **Local File** (Per-jurisdiction):
   - Entity-specific business description
   - Controlled transactions
   - Financial data
   - Economic analysis
   - Tax reconciliation

## 4. SERIES A → B → C DILUTION IMPACT ON CROSS-HOLDINGS

### 4.1 Pre-Seed / Seed Cross-Holding Structure (Y0)

At founding, all cross-holdings are established at **par value** ($0.01/share) with the following initial capitalization:

| Entity | Authorized Shares | Issued at Seed | Par Value | Cross-Holding Shares | Cross-Holding Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Corleone Products LLC | 10,000,000 | 1,000,000 | $0.01 | 100,000 (to Pizza Oven) + 100,000 (to Bakery) | $2,000 |
| Vito Corleone Media LLC | 10,000,000 | 1,000,000 | $0.01 | 50,000 (to Pizza Oven) + 50,000 (to Bakery) | $1,000 |
| Don Vito's Pizza Oven LLC | 10,000,000 | 850,000 | $0.01 | 80,000 (to Bakery) | $800 |
| Vito Corleone Bakery LLC | 10,000,000 | 850,000 | $0.01 | 80,000 (to Pizza Oven) | $800 |
| Corleone Halal Meats SPV | 10,000,000 | 600,000 | $0.01 | 150,000 (to Products) | $1,500 |

### 4.2 Series A Anti-Dilution Protection (Y1)

All cross-holding shares carry **full ratchet anti-dilution protection**:
- If Series A prices at $37.22M post-money / 1,000,000 shares = $37.22/share
- Seed investors (including cross-holding entities) receive additional shares to maintain their proportional ownership
- Cross-holding entities participate pro-rata in any future rounds

### 4.3 Post-IPO Cross-Holding Lock-Up (Y7)

All cross-holding shares are subject to:
- **180-day lock-up** post-IPO for all holders
- **12-month staggered release** for cross-holding entities (10% per quarter after lock-up)
- **Right of first refusal** in favor of the parent holding company for any cross-holding sale
