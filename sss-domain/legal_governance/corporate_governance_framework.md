## 1. DEFINED TERMS

**"AISTA"** means the AISTA Corridor, the trade and investment corridor linking Somaliland, India, the UAE, and Saudi Arabia under the Sovereign SafeTrade Program (SSTP).

**"Board"** means the Board of Directors of Corleone Enterprise Holdings, a Delaware corporation (the **"Parent"**).

**"Committees"** means the standing committees of the Board as described herein and in the Board Charter.

**"Corleone Enterprise"** or **"Enterprise"** means the Parent and its direct and indirect subsidiaries, including Don Vito's Pizza Oven LLC, Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe LLC, Corleone Products LLC, Vito Corleone Media & Licensing LLC, and Corleone Halal Meats SPV (the **"SPV"**), collectively.

**"ESOP"** means the employee stock ownership plan applicable to the franchise subsidiaries, representing 15% of the equity of each franchise entity.

**"FIV"** means the Franchisee Investment Vehicle, a Delaware limited liability company described in the FIV Operating Agreement (`fiv_operating_agreement.md`).

**"Independent Director"** means a director who satisfies the independence standards under the DGCL, applicable stock exchange rules, and the Board Charter.

**"Related Party"** means any director, officer, 5%+ shareholder of the Parent or any subsidiary, or any immediate family member or entity controlled by any of the foregoing.

**"SSTP"** means the Sovereign SafeTrade Program.

**"SSS Firm"** means the Sovereign SafeTrade Structuring Firm (Pillar A).

## 2. GOVERNANCE PHILOSOPHY AND PILLARS

The governance of Corleone Enterprise is designed to support the three pillars of the SSTP ecosystem:
- **Pillar A (SSS Firm):** Strategic structuring, capital allocation, and risk governance.
- **Pillar B (AISTA Corridor):** Cross-border regulatory compliance, concession governance, and blended-finance oversight.
- **Pillar C (Corleone Enterprise):** Operating company governance, franchisee and minority protections, and ESOP administration.

This Framework applies to all entities within the Enterprise, subject to local law overrides.

## 3. DELAWARE PARENT GOVERNANCE (CORLEONE ENTERPRISE HOLDINGS)

### 3.1 Governing Law and Formation
The Parent is incorporated under the Delaware General Corporation Law (DGCL), Title 8, Chapter 1. Its Certificate of Incorporation and Bylaws are the primary constitutional documents. This Framework supplements those documents and prevails in the event of conflict only to the extent expressly permitted by DGCL Section 116 and related provisions.

### 3.2 Board Composition
The Board shall consist of [5] directors, composed as follows:
- [2] directors designated by the SSS Firm or founding shareholders (the **"SSS Directors"**);
- [1] independent director meeting the independence standards set forth herein and in the Board Charter;
- [1] franchisee representative elected by the FIV or the franchisee ESOP trustee; and
- [1] Somaliland partner representative designated by the Somaliland partners holding equity in the SPV.

The Board Charter (`board_charter_committee_structures.md`) sets forth the full composition, skills matrix, and election procedures.

### 3.3 Board Committees
The Board shall maintain the following standing committees:
- **Audit Committee** (3 members, all independent, with financial expertise);
- **Compensation Committee** (3 members, independent, administering executive compensation and ESOP);
- **Nominating Committee** (3 members, responsible for board composition and succession);
- **Risk Committee** (3 members, overseeing enterprise risk appetite and the risk register); and
- **Technology Committee** (3 members, advising on digital strategy, cybersecurity, and AISTA digital settlement infrastructure).

Each committee shall operate pursuant to a written charter approved by the Board. Charters are annexed to the Board Charter document.

### 3.4 Shareholder Rights
Subject to the Certificate of Incorporation and shareholder agreements, shareholders of the Parent shall have the following rights:
- **Voting:** One vote per share on all matters submitted to a vote of shareholders, except as otherwise provided by DGCL or the shareholder agreements.
- **Dividends:** Right to receive dividends when, as, and if declared by the Board, subject to DGCL solvency requirements and the dividend policies of any applicable shareholder agreements.
- **Inspection:** Right to inspect books and records pursuant to DGCL Section 220, subject to reasonable confidentiality restrictions.
- **Appraisal:** Right to appraisal and payment of fair value in connection with certain mergers and consolidations under DGCL Section 262.
- **Pre-emptive Rights:** As set forth in the applicable Shareholder Agreement (`shareholder_agreement_templates.md`), shareholders may have pre-emptive rights on new issuances of Common Stock.

### 3.5 Related Party Transaction Policy
All transactions between the Parent or any subsidiary and a Related Party (including intercompany transactions with the SSS Firm, SPV, or franchise entities) must be:
- Reviewed and approved by the Audit Committee or the full Board (if the transaction exceeds [$250,000] or involves a director's personal interest);
- Documented at fair market value, supported by a third-party valuation or benchmark where material; and
- Disclosed in the minutes of the meeting at which approval was obtained.

A Related Party Transaction Register shall be maintained by the Corporate Secretary and reviewed annually by the Audit Committee.

### 3.6 Insider Trading Policy
All directors, officers, employees, and consultants with access to material non-public information (MNPI) are prohibited from trading in the securities of the Parent or any publicly traded counterparty while in possession of MNPI. This policy applies to:
- Direct trading by covered persons;
- Tippee liability;
- Short-swing profits under Section 16(b) of the U.S. Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (as applicable post-IPO); and
- Blackout periods around quarterly earnings releases, financing rounds, and regulatory milestones (e.g., REG-004, REG-003, REG-006, REG-005, REG-002 deadlines).

The General Counsel shall administer a preclearance process for all covered transactions.

### 3.7 Code of Conduct and Ethics
The Enterprise adopts a unified Code of Conduct applicable to all directors, officers, employees, franchisees, distributors, and Somaliland partners. Key principles include:
- Compliance with all applicable laws, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act 2010, and local anti-corruption statutes;
- Conflicts of interest disclosure;
- Protection of confidential information and intellectual property;
- Respect for human rights, labor standards, and environmental obligations; and
- Reporting of violations through the whistleblower mechanism.

### 3.8 Whistleblower Policy
The Enterprise maintains a whistleblower hotline and web portal administered by an independent third-party provider. Reports may be submitted anonymously where permitted by law. The Audit Committee shall receive all reports involving financial misconduct, fraud, or accounting irregularities. Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited and shall result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 3.9 Anti-Corruption and Bribery Policy
The Enterprise prohibits bribery, kickbacks, and improper facilitation payments in all jurisdictions, including:
- **FCPA (U.S.):** Prohibition on payments to foreign government officials to obtain or retain business.
- **UK Bribery Act 2010:** Prohibition on bribery of foreign and domestic officials, as well as commercial bribery.
- **Local Equivalents:** Compliance with UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 20/2018 on Anti-Money Laundering, Saudi Anti-Bribery Law (Royal Decree M/36/1992), and Indian Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.

All third-party agents, distributors, and government liaison consultants must execute an anti-corruption certification prior to engagement. The Risk Committee shall conduct an annual anti-corruption risk assessment.

## 4. UAE SUBSIDIARY GOVERNANCE

### 4.1 Applicable Regime
UAE operations may be conducted through entities licensed in the Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC), Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC), or Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM). The selection of jurisdiction depends on the activity (e.g., DMCC for commodities and CPG trading; DIFC/ADGM for finance and holding functions).

### 4.2 Local Director Requirements
Each UAE entity must appoint at least one UAE-resident individual or corporate service provider as director or manager, as required by the relevant free zone authority. The Parent shall retain majority board control subject to local minima.

### 4.3 Economic Substance
All UAE entities must comply with the UAE Economic Substance Regulations (Cabinet Decision No. 57/2020 and Ministerial Decision No. 100/2020). Entities conducting relevant activities (holding company business, distribution, or logistics) must:
- Maintain adequate substance in the UAE (directed and managed in the UAE, core income-generating activities performed in the UAE, adequate employees, expenditure, and premises);
- File annual economic substance notifications and reports; and
- Maintain documentation evidencing substance compliance.

### 4.4 Cross-Border Governance Interface
UAE entities shall implement the Delaware Parent's governance policies to the extent consistent with UAE law and free zone regulations. The UAE entity board shall include at least one Parent-designated director.

## 5. SOMALILAND SPV GOVERNANCE (CORLEONE HALAL MEATS SPV)

### 5.1 Local Company Law and SPV Structure
The SPV is established under the Somaliland Company Law (as amended) and the Berbera Free Zone regulations. The SPV is a locally incorporated company or special purpose vehicle with the following governance features:
- **Board Structure:** A [5]-member board comprising [3] directors appointed by Corleone Enterprise Holdings and [2] directors appointed by the Somaliland partners (the **"Local Partners"**).
- **Concession Governance:** The SPV board shall establish a Concession Oversight Subcommittee responsible for monitoring compliance with the 25-year concession agreement (`somaliland_concession_agreement_draft.md`), including development milestones, environmental bonds, and production targets.
- **Partner Rights:** Local Partners shall have customary minority protections, including veto rights over: (a) amendment of the concession; (b) material asset sale outside the ordinary course; (c) incurrence of debt exceeding [$1,000,000]; and (d) change of control of the SPV.

### 5.2 Concession Governance
The SPV shall maintain a Concession Compliance Register tracking:
- Minimum $5M capex obligations in the first 3 years;
- Halal certification audits and recognition by UAE ESMA and Saudi SFDA;
- Mineral extraction volumes and environmental rehabilitation bond performance;
- Employment of 30% local Somaliland workforce and skills transfer programs; and
- Export quotas, preferential port access, and customs facilitation metrics.

### 5.3 Related Party Transactions (SPV)
All intercompany transactions between the SPV and Corleone Products LLC (including the 15% cross-holding and supply arrangements) must be documented at arm's length. Transfer pricing documentation shall be prepared in accordance with OECD guidelines and local Somaliland tax requirements, if and when enacted.

## 6. INDIA OPERATIONS GOVERNANCE

### 6.1 Companies Act 2013 Compliance
Indian subsidiaries (whether incorporated as private limited companies, liaison offices, or project offices) must comply with the Companies Act, 2013, including:
- Board meetings: at least one meeting in each calendar quarter and a minimum of [4] meetings per year;
- Annual general meetings and statutory filings (AOC-4, MGT-7);
- Maintenance of statutory registers and books of account;
- Compliance with the National Financial Reporting Authority (NFRA) audit standards; and
- Secretarial standards (SS-1 and SS-2).

### 6.2 FDI Regulations
Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in food processing, retail (franchise), and CPG distribution is permitted under the automatic route in most sectors, subject to sectoral caps and pricing guidelines. The Indian subsidiaries shall:
- File FDI reports in the FIRMS portal of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI);
- Ensure pricing of equity instruments complies with FEMA Pricing Guidelines (Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of Security by a Person Resident outside India) Regulations, 2017); and
- Maintain compliance with downstream investment rules if the Parent invests through an intermediate foreign entity.

### 6.3 Joint Venture Governance (India)
If the Enterprise forms an Indian joint venture (e.g., for co-manufacturing or Bihar BIBC operations), the JV shall be governed by:
- A shareholders' agreement consistent with this Framework;
- A local board with Parent-designated majority or equal representation depending on shareholding; and
- Reserved matters requiring supermajority or Parent consent, including: annual budget, capital expenditure, borrowing, related party transactions, and intellectual property licensing.

### 6.4 Environmental and Regulatory Compliance
The India operations must comply with:
- PARIVESH (REG-003) for environmental clearances, with a 9-day deadline from the date of application submission for Category B projects;
- SEBI EGR (REG-002) if any Indian entity issues securities to foreign portfolio investors, with a 35-day compliance window; and
- State-level pollution control board requirements for manufacturing operations.

## 7. SAUDI ARABIA OPERATIONS GOVERNANCE

### 7.1 SAGIA / MISA Requirements
All Saudi entities must be licensed by the Ministry of Investment of Saudi Arabia (MISA, formerly SAGIA) and comply with the Foreign Investment Law. Key requirements include:
- Commercial registration (CR) and Articles of Association (AoA) filed with MISA and the Ministry of Commerce (MoC);
- Appointment of a Saudi-resident manager (if required by the license type); and
- Compliance with the specific regulations governing the food & beverage and CPG sectors.

### 7.2 Saudization (Nitaqat)
The Saudi entities shall comply with the Nitaqat program, maintaining the required percentage of Saudi nationals in the workforce. Compliance plans shall be reviewed quarterly by the HR and Compliance functions.

### 7.3 Local Partner Requirements
Where Saudi law requires a local partner or sponsor (e.g., for certain retail or franchise structures), the Enterprise shall:
- Engage a local partner under a contractual arrangement that preserves Parent governance and economic rights to the maximum extent permitted;
- Document the partner's role, capital contribution, and profit-sharing in a side agreement; and
- Ensure the partner does not trigger Related Party Transaction thresholds without Audit Committee approval.

### 7.4 Regulatory Deadlines (Saudi)
- **REG-004 MEWA / SFDA:** Compliance with the Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture (MEWA) and Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA) requirements within 4 days of notice or inspection finding.
- **REG-005 ORIASC-HCB SFDA:** Compliance with ORIASC-HCB (Organic / Halal Certification Body) SFDA registration and renewal obligations within 19 days of notification.

### 7.5 Halal Certification Governance
The Saudi operations shall maintain halal certification from SFDA-recognized bodies. The SPV's halal certification (Berbera) must be recognized by SFDA and UAE ESMA to support cross-border trade within the AISTA Corridor.

## 8. KENYA OPERATIONS GOVERNANCE

### 8.1 Companies Act Compliance
Kenyan subsidiaries shall comply with the Companies Act, 2015 (as amended), including:
- Registration with the Registrar of Companies;
- Annual returns and financial statements;
- Board meeting requirements (minimum one meeting per year for private companies, but best practice requires quarterly meetings); and
- Maintenance of a registered office in Kenya.

### 8.2 Local Requirements
Kenyan entities shall comply with:
- Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) tax registration and filing (corporate income tax, VAT, PAYE, withholding tax on dividends);
- National Environment Management Authority (NEMA) requirements for manufacturing or processing facilities; and
- Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) for product quality and labeling.

### 8.3 Franchise and CPG Governance
Kenyan franchise operations shall be governed by master franchise agreements or direct franchise agreements consistent with the templates (`franchise_agreement_template_pizza.md`, `franchise_agreement_template_bakery.md`). CPG distribution shall be governed by the distribution agreement template (`cpg_distribution_agreement_template.md`).

## 9. MINORITY SHAREHOLDER PROTECTIONS

### 9.1 Franchisee ESOP (15%)
Each franchise entity (Don Vito's Pizza Oven and Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe) maintains a 15% ESOP pool. Minority protections include:
- Tag-along rights on any sale of Parent or franchisee equity by the majority;
- Information rights (quarterly financial statements, annual budget, material litigation);
- Pre-emptive rights on new issuances (to the extent not waived under shareholder agreements);
- Anti-dilution protection (full ratchet) as set forth in the Shareholder Agreements (`shareholder_agreement_templates.md`); and
- Fair market valuation on any squeeze-out or redemption, as determined by an independent appraiser.

### 9.2 Somaliland Local Partners (40%)
The Local Partners holding 40% of the SPV shall have:
- Board representation (2 of 5 directors);
- Veto rights over reserved matters (see Section 5.1);
- Pre-emptive rights on transfers of SPV equity;
- Tag-along rights on any sale by the Parent; and
- Put/Call options as described in the Joint Venture Agreement (`joint_venture_agreement_somaliland.md`).

### 9.3 Franchisee Cross-Holdings (8% Each)
Franchisees in the same brand cross-hold 8% equity interests. These cross-holdings are governed by the Franchise Agreement and the FIV Operating Agreement, including transfer restrictions and rights of first refusal.
