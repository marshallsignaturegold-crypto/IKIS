## 1. DEFINED TERMS

**"AISTA"** means the AISTA Corridor under the Sovereign SafeTrade Program (SSTP), linking Somaliland, India, the UAE, and Saudi Arabia.

**"Berbera Free Zone"** or **"BFZ"** means the Berbera Free Zone established under the Somaliland Free Zone Authority, encompassing the designated industrial, commercial, and logistics zones in the Berbera region.

**"Concessionaire"** means Corleone Halal Meats SPV, a special purpose vehicle organized under the laws of Somaliland, or its successor or permitted assign.

**"Concession Area"** means the land and related rights granted to the Concessionaire under this Agreement, specifically **Berbera Free Zone Zone 3**, comprising approximately **2.5 hectares** (25,000 square meters), as delineated on the site plan attached as **Annex A**.

**"Critical Minerals"** means the minerals and natural resources authorized for extraction under this Agreement, specifically: salt (sodium chloride), gypsum (calcium sulfate dihydrate), and groundwater (for industrial and agricultural use within the Concession Area), as further described in **Annex F**.

**"Development Obligations"** means the capital expenditure, construction, and production milestones set forth in Section 5 and **Annex C**.

**"Environmental Bond"** means the financial guarantee required to secure the Concessionaire's environmental rehabilitation obligations, as described in **Annex D**.

**"ESMA"** means the Emirates Authority for Standardization and Metrology of the UAE.

**"Force Majeure Event"** means any event beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemic, government action, port closures, trade embargoes, and political instability.

**"Grantor"** means the Somaliland Government, acting through the **Berbera Free Zone Authority** and the **Ministry of Investment and Economic Development** (or the relevant successor ministries), collectively.

**"Halal Certification"** means certification by a recognized halal certification body that the Concessionaire's processing, slaughtering, and handling practices comply with Islamic dietary laws (Shariah) and the standards of the relevant importing jurisdictions (UAE, Saudi Arabia, India, Kenya, etc.).

**"Local Employment Target"** means the requirement that at least **[30%]** of the Concessionaire's workforce shall be Somaliland nationals, as further described in Section 8.

**"Mineral Extraction Rights"** means the non-exclusive or exclusive (as specified) right to extract Critical Minerals within a **50-kilometer radius** of the Concession Area, as described in Section 6 and **Annex F**.

**"Operations Commencement Date"** means the date on which the Concessionaire's halal processing facility achieves commercial production (the processing of livestock for halal meat export), as certified by the Grantor and the Concessionaire's independent engineer.

**"SFDA"** means the Saudi Food and Drug Authority.

**"SSTP"** means the Sovereign SafeTrade Program.

**"SPV"** means the special purpose vehicle established by the Concessionaire for the purposes of this Agreement, as described in the Joint Venture Agreement (`joint_venture_agreement_somaliland.md`).

**"Term"** means the initial 25-year concession period, as described in Section 3, subject to renewal.

**"Transfer"** means any sale, assignment, pledge, encumbrance, or other disposition of this Agreement, the Concession Area, or any interest therein, including a change of control of the Concessionaire.

## 3. GRANT OF CONCESSION AND TERM

### 3.1 Grant of Concession
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Grantor hereby grants to the Concessionaire the exclusive right and concession to:
- (a) Develop, construct, operate, and maintain a halal meat processing and slaughterhouse facility within the Concession Area (the **"Facility"**);
- (b) Extract Critical Minerals within the Mineral Extraction Area, subject to the terms of Section 6 and the applicable mining and environmental regulations of Somaliland;
- (c) Use the Concession Area for ancillary purposes, including administrative offices, cold storage, warehousing, logistics, staff housing, and training facilities; and
- (d) Export halal meat products and Critical Minerals from the Berbera Free Zone through the Port of Berbera and other designated export channels.

### 3.2 Concession Area
The Concession Area consists of **Berbera Free Zone Zone 3**, comprising approximately **2.5 hectares** (25,000 square meters), as delineated on the site plan attached as **Annex A**. The Concession Area includes:
- (a) The land surface and subsurface rights necessary for the construction and operation of the Facility;
- (b) Rights of way for access roads, utilities, and pipelines within the Concession Area; and
- (c) Non-exclusive rights to use the common infrastructure of the Berbera Free Zone (roads, port access, security, telecommunications) in accordance with the BFZ regulations and applicable fees.

### 3.3 Mineral Extraction Area
The Concessionaire is granted the right to extract Critical Minerals within a **50-kilometer radius** of the Concession Area (the **"Mineral Extraction Area"**), as further described in **Annex F**. This right is:
- **[Exclusive / Non-exclusive]** for salt and gypsum extraction; and
- **[Non-exclusive]** for groundwater extraction, subject to the Grantor's right to grant similar rights to other concessionaires for non-competing uses.

The Concessionaire shall not extract any minerals other than the Critical Minerals listed in **Annex F** without the Grantor's prior written consent and the issuance of a separate mining license or concession.

### 3.4 Term
The initial term of this Agreement shall be **twenty-five (25) years** from the Operations Commencement Date (the **"Initial Term"**). If the Operations Commencement Date has not occurred within [3] years of the Effective Date due to the Concessionaire's default, the Grantor may terminate this Agreement. If the delay is due to Force Majeure or the Grantor's failure to provide necessary permits or infrastructure, the term shall be extended by the period of such delay.

### 3.5 Renewal
The Concessionaire shall have the right to renew this Agreement for **two (2) additional terms of ten (10) years each** (each a **"Renewal Term"**), provided that:
- (a) The Concessionaire is not in material breach of this Agreement at the time of the renewal request;
- (b) The Concessionaire has achieved all production milestones and environmental compliance obligations in the preceding term;
- (c) The Concessionaire submits a renewal application at least [18] months before the expiration of the then-current term;
- (d) The Concessionaire pays a renewal fee of **[$500,000]**; and
- (e) The Parties negotiate and execute a renewal agreement, which may include updated fees, environmental standards, and development obligations reflecting then-current conditions.

If the Parties fail to agree on renewal terms within [12] months of the renewal application, the Concessionaire may submit the dispute to arbitration under Section 16.

### 3.6 Early Occupation and Development Period
The Concessionaire shall have the right to enter the Concession Area for site preparation, surveying, and environmental baseline studies upon execution of this Agreement and payment of the initial land rent. The period between the Effective Date and the Operations Commencement Date is the **"Development Period."** During the Development Period, the Concessionaire shall be subject to the development obligations in Section 5 and the environmental obligations in Section 15.

## 4. DEVELOPMENT OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Minimum Capital Expenditure
The Concessionaire shall invest a minimum of **$5,000,000** in capital expenditure (capex) within the first **three (3) years** of the Development Period (the **"Minimum Capex"**). The Minimum Capex shall be allocated as follows:
- (a) Facility construction and equipment: **$3,500,000**;
- (b) Cold chain infrastructure (refrigeration, blast freezing, cold storage): **$800,000**;
- (c) Utility infrastructure (power, water, wastewater): **$400,000**;
- (d) Environmental and safety systems: **$200,000**; and
- (e) Administration, training, and community facilities: **$100,000**.

The Concessionaire shall provide the Grantor with quarterly capex reports and shall submit audited financial statements evidencing the Minimum Capex within [90] days of the end of the third year.

### 4.2 Facility Construction Timeline
The Concessionaire shall complete the construction of the Facility in accordance with the development schedule attached as **Annex C** (Development Schedule). Key milestones include:
| Milestone | Target Date | Penalty for Delay |
|---|---|---|
| Site preparation and environmental clearance | Month 6 | $50,000/month |
| Foundation and structural construction | Month 12 | $50,000/month |
| Equipment installation and cold chain | Month 18 | $75,000/month |
| Halal certification and trial operations | Month 24 | $75,000/month |
| Commercial operations commencement | Month 30 | $100,000/month |
| Full production capacity (500 head/day) | Month 36 | $100,000/month |

Delay penalties shall be capped at **[10%]** of the Minimum Capex. Delays due to Force Majeure or the Grantor's failure to issue permits or provide infrastructure shall not trigger penalties and shall extend the milestone dates by the period of delay.

### 4.3 Production Milestones
The Concessionaire shall achieve the following production milestones after the Operations Commencement Date:
| Milestone | Target Date | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| 25% of design capacity | Month 6 post-operations | 125 head/day |

Failure to achieve a production milestone within [6] months of the target date shall constitute a breach, subject to a [90]-day cure period. If the Concessionaire fails to cure, the Grantor may reduce the Concession Area, impose penalties, or terminate this Agreement.

### 4.4 Reporting and Monitoring
The Concessionaire shall submit monthly progress reports to the Grantor during the Development Period, including:
- Construction progress (with photographs and independent engineer certification);
- Capex expenditure (with invoices and receipts);
- Employment and local hiring statistics;
- Environmental compliance and monitoring data; and
- Any delays, challenges, or Force Majeure events.

The Grantor shall have the right to inspect the Concession Area and the Facility (with [48] hours' notice) no more than [4] times per year during the Development Period and [2] times per year during the Operations Period.

## 5. OPERATIONS

### 5.1 Halal Processing Standards
The Concessionaire shall operate the Facility in strict compliance with halal processing standards, including:
- (a) Islamic slaughter (Zabiha) by certified Muslim slaughtermen;
- (b) Stunning only if permitted by the certifying body and the importing jurisdiction's halal standards;
- (c) Complete bleeding, evisceration, and inspection by qualified veterinarians;
- (d) Segregation of halal and non-halal products at all stages;
- (e) Traceability and labeling of all halal products with batch numbers, slaughter dates, and certifying body marks; and
- (f) Compliance with the halal certification requirements of the UAE (ESMA), Saudi Arabia (SFDA), and other importing jurisdictions.

The Concessionaire shall maintain halal certification from a recognized certifying body (e.g., UAE ESMA, Saudi SFDA, or an equivalent Somaliland-authorized body) at all times. Failure to maintain valid halal certification shall constitute a material breach.

### 5.2 Daily Capacity and Cold Chain
The Facility shall be designed to process a minimum of **500 head of livestock per day** (cattle, sheep, goats, or camels, as approved by the certifying body and the Grantor). The Concessionaire shall maintain a continuous cold chain from slaughter to export, including:
- (a) Blast freezing to [-18°C] within [4] hours of slaughter;
- (b) Cold storage at [-18°C] or below;
- (c) Refrigerated transport from the Facility to the Port of Berbera;
- (d) Temperature monitoring and recording at all stages; and
- (e) Compliance with the cold chain standards of the importing jurisdictions (e.g., UAE, Saudi Arabia, India, Kenya).

### 5.3 Environmental Compliance
The Concessionaire shall comply with the environmental obligations set forth in Section 15 and **Annex D** (Environmental Bond and Rehabilitation Plan). The Concessionaire shall:
- (a) Treat all wastewater to meet discharge standards before release;
- (b) Dispose of solid waste (offal, bones, hides) in accordance with the Grantor's waste management regulations and, where possible, through recycling, rendering, or by-product processing;
- (c) Control odors, noise, and air emissions;
- (d) Monitor groundwater usage and prevent contamination; and
- (e) Implement an environmental management system (EMS) consistent with ISO 14001 or an equivalent standard.

### 5.4 Health and Safety
The Concessionaire shall maintain a safe and healthy workplace, complying with all applicable health and safety regulations, including:
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) for all workers;
- Hazardous materials handling and storage;
- Fire safety and emergency response plans;
- First aid and medical facilities on-site; and
- Regular health and safety training for all employees.

### 5.5 Record-Keeping and Traceability
The Concessionaire shall maintain detailed records of all operations, including:
- Livestock sourcing (supplier, origin, health certificates, vaccination records);
- Slaughter and processing data (dates, batch numbers, slaughtermen, veterinarians);
- Halal certification records (certificates, audits, inspections);
- Cold chain temperature logs;
- Export documentation (phytosanitary certificates, customs declarations, shipping manifests); and
- Employment and training records.

All records shall be retained for a minimum of **[5] years** and shall be made available to the Grantor and relevant regulatory authorities upon request.

## 6. MINERAL EXTRACTION

### 6.1 Authorized Minerals
The Concessionaire is authorized to extract the following Critical Minerals within the Mineral Extraction Area:
- (a) **Salt (Sodium Chloride):** For industrial use, food processing, and export, up to a maximum annual extraction volume of **[50,000] metric tons**;
- (b) **Gypsum (Calcium Sulfate Dihydrate):** For construction, industrial use, and export, up to a maximum annual extraction volume of **[30,000] metric tons**; and
- (c) **Groundwater:** For industrial, agricultural, and potable use within the Concession Area, up to a maximum annual extraction volume of **[500,000] cubic meters**.

The extraction volumes, methods, and environmental safeguards are further detailed in **Annex F** (Mineral Extraction Plan).

### 6.2 Extraction Methods and Environmental Safeguards
The Concessionaire shall extract Critical Minerals using methods that minimize environmental impact, including:
- (a) **Salt:** Solar evaporation of brine or controlled brine pumping, with reinjection of waste brine or disposal in evaporation ponds designed to prevent soil and groundwater contamination;
- (b) **Gypsum:** Surface quarrying or controlled underground extraction, with dust suppression, slope stabilization, and progressive rehabilitation of extraction sites; and
- (c) **Groundwater:** Sustainable pumping rates based on hydrogeological studies, with monitoring wells, recharge area protection, and prohibition of contamination from industrial waste.

The Concessionaire shall prepare and submit an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for the Mineral Extraction Area to the Grantor within [12] months of the Effective Date, and shall obtain all required environmental permits before commencing extraction.

### 6.3 Rehabilitation Bond
The Concessionaire shall post an environmental rehabilitation bond with the Grantor in the amount of **[$500,000]** (or as otherwise agreed based on the EIA), as detailed in **Annex D**. The bond shall be released in tranches upon completion of rehabilitation milestones. If the Concessionaire fails to complete rehabilitation, the Grantor may draw on the bond to fund rehabilitation works.

### 6.4 Mineral Royalties
The Concessionaire shall pay mineral extraction royalties to the Grantor as follows:
| Mineral | Royalty Rate | Minimum Annual Payment |
|---|---|---|
| Salt | 5% of gross sales value | $50,000 |
| Gypsum | 5% of gross sales value | $30,000 |
| Groundwater | $0.05 per cubic meter | $25,000 |

Royalty payments shall be due quarterly, within [30] days of the end of each quarter, accompanied by a production and sales report.

## 7. HALAL CERTIFICATION

### 7.1 Authority to Certify
The Concessionaire shall obtain and maintain halal certification from a recognized halal certification body. The Concessionaire may, with the Grantor's approval, establish an in-house halal certification program under the supervision of a recognized Shariah board and accredited auditors.

### 7.2 Standards and Audit Procedures
Halal certification shall comply with:
- (a) The UAE ESMA halal standards (UAE.S GSO 2055-1, 2055-2, and related standards);
- (b) The Saudi SFDA halal regulations and recognition requirements;
- (c) The importing jurisdiction's halal standards (e.g., India FSSAI, Kenya KEBS, Qatar QGOSM); and
- (d) The Grantor's local halal certification requirements, if any.

The Concessionaire shall undergo annual audits by the certifying body and shall submit audit reports to the Grantor. Unannounced inspections may be conducted at any time.

### 7.3 Recognition by UAE ESMA and Saudi SFDA
The Concessionaire shall ensure that its halal certification is recognized by the UAE ESMA and Saudi SFDA to facilitate export of halal meat products to the UAE and Saudi Arabia through the AISTA Corridor. The Concessionaire shall cooperate with the UAE and Saudi authorities to obtain recognition, including accreditation of the certifying body, submission of facility documentation, and participation in inspections.

## 8. EMPLOYMENT AND SKILLS TRANSFER

### 8.1 Local Employment Target
The Concessionaire shall ensure that at least **[30%]** of its total workforce (including management, technical, and administrative staff) are Somaliland nationals (the **"Local Employment Target"**). The Local Employment Target shall be measured annually and reported to the Grantor.

### 8.2 Training Programs
The Concessionaire shall implement training programs for Somaliland employees, including:
- (a) Vocational training in halal slaughtering, meat processing, and cold chain management;
- (b) Technical training in equipment operation, maintenance, and repair;
- (c) Administrative and management training in accounting, procurement, and human resources;
- (d) Health and safety training; and
- (e) Language training (English, Arabic) for export-facing roles.

The Concessionaire shall allocate at least **$100,000 per year** for training programs and shall collaborate with local vocational training institutions and the Grantor's workforce development programs.

### 8.3 Skills Transfer
The Concessionaire shall implement a skills transfer program to ensure that Somaliland nationals are progressively trained to assume senior technical and management roles. Within [10] years of the Operations Commencement Date, the Concessionaire shall achieve a target of **[50%]** Somaliland nationals in senior management and technical positions.

### 8.4 Labor Standards
The Concessionaire shall comply with all applicable labor laws and regulations, including:
- Minimum wage and overtime requirements;
- Safe working conditions and PPE;
- Non-discrimination and equal opportunity;
- Prohibition of child labor and forced labor;
- Freedom of association and collective bargaining (to the extent permitted by local law); and
- Fair disciplinary and grievance procedures.

## 9. EXPORT RIGHTS AND FACILITATION

### 9.1 Preferential Port Access
The Concessionaire shall have preferential access to the Port of Berbera for the export of halal meat products and Critical Minerals, including:
- (a) Priority berthing and loading for refrigerated vessels;
- (b) Expedited customs clearance and inspection;
- (c) Dedicated cold storage and staging areas at the port; and
- (d) Coordination with port authorities to minimize dwell time and ensure cold chain integrity.

The Grantor shall use its best efforts to ensure that the Port of Berbera maintains adequate cold chain infrastructure to support the Concessionaire's export volumes.

### 9.2 Customs Facilitation
The Grantor shall facilitate customs clearance for the Concessionaire's exports, including:
- (a) Pre-clearance procedures and electronic documentation;
- (b) Coordination with customs authorities to minimize inspection delays;
- (c) Exemption from or reduction of export duties and fees on halal meat products and Critical Minerals; and
- (d) Assistance in obtaining phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, and other export documentation required by the importing jurisdictions.

### 9.3 Export Quotas and Targets
The Concessionaire shall have the right to export all halal meat products and Critical Minerals produced in the Facility, subject to:
- (a) Compliance with the importing jurisdiction's import quotas, tariffs, and regulations;
- (b) The Grantor's right to impose temporary export restrictions in the event of national emergency, food security concerns, or international trade disputes; and
- (c) The Concessionaire's obligation to prioritize export to AISTA Corridor markets (UAE, Saudi Arabia, India, Kenya) before exporting to other markets, unless otherwise agreed by the Grantor.

### 9.4 Phytosanitary Certificates
The Concessionaire shall obtain all required phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, and origin certificates for its exports. The Grantor shall assist the Concessionaire in obtaining these certificates through the relevant ministries and veterinary authorities.

## 10. FEES AND ROYALTIES

### 10.1 Land Rent
The Concessionaire shall pay an annual land rent for the Concession Area as follows:
| Year | Annual Land Rent |
|---|---|
| Years 1–3 | $50,000 |
| Years 4–10 | $75,000 |
| Years 11–20 | $100,000 |
| Years 21–25 | $125,000 |

Land rent shall be due annually in advance, within [30] days of the anniversary of the Effective Date. The Grantor may adjust land rent based on an independent appraisal every [5] years, with a cap of [10%] increase per adjustment.

### 10.2 Production Royalty
The Concessionaire shall pay a production royalty to the Grantor on halal meat products, calculated as **[2%]** of the gross export value of halal meat products (FOB Port of Berbera). The production royalty shall be due quarterly, within [30] days of the end of each quarter, accompanied by a production and export report.

### 10.3 Mineral Extraction Royalties
As set forth in Section 6.4.

### 10.4 Port Fees
The Concessionaire shall pay standard port fees and charges for the use of the Port of Berbera, as published by the Port Authority from time to time. The Concessionaire shall be eligible for volume discounts and preferential rates based on its export volumes, as negotiated with the Port Authority.

### 10.5 Tax Incentives and Exemptions
The Grantor shall provide the Concessionaire with the following tax incentives and exemptions, as authorized under the Berbera Free Zone regulations and applicable Somaliland investment law:
- (a) **Corporate Income Tax:** Exemption from corporate income tax for the first **[10] years** of the Operations Period; **[50%]** reduction for the subsequent **[10] years**;
- (b) **Import Duty Exemptions:** Exemption from import duties on machinery, equipment, and construction materials imported for the Facility during the Development Period;
- (c) **Export Duty Exemptions:** Exemption from export duties on halal meat products and Critical Minerals;
- (d) **Withholding Tax Exemptions:** Exemption from withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties paid to foreign investors for the first **[10] years**; and
- (e) **VAT/GST Exemptions:** Exemption from value-added tax or goods and services tax on transactions within the Berbera Free Zone.

The Grantor shall issue a tax exemption certificate or ruling to the Concessionaire confirming the above incentives.

## 11. INFRASTRUCTURE

### 11.1 Access to Utilities
The Grantor shall ensure that the Concessionaire has access to the following utilities within the Concession Area:
- (a) **Electricity:** Reliable grid connection or backup power (diesel generators, solar, or hybrid) sufficient to meet the Facility's peak demand (estimated at **[2 MW]**);

- (d) **Telecommunications:** High-speed internet, telephone, and mobile connectivity; and
- (e) **Roads:** All-weather access roads connecting the Concession Area to the Port of Berbera and the main highway.

The Grantor shall complete the infrastructure connections within [12] months of the Effective Date, or shall provide the Concessionaire with a cost-sharing arrangement if the Concessionaire elects to build the infrastructure itself.

### 11.2 Security
The Grantor shall provide security within the Berbera Free Zone, including perimeter security, access control, and emergency response. The Concessionaire may supplement BFZ security with its own private security personnel, subject to the Grantor's approval and compliance with local laws.

### 11.3 Telecommunications and Digital Infrastructure
The Grantor shall facilitate the Concessionaire's access to digital infrastructure, including:
- (a) High-speed fiber optic internet;
- (b) Mobile network coverage (4G/5G);
- (c) Coordination with the AISTA Corridor digital settlement infrastructure (CSFI blockchain/DLT) for trade documentation, payment settlement, and supply chain traceability; and
- (d) Support for the Concessionaire's integration with the SSTP digital platforms.

## 12. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT AND INVESTMENT GUARANTEES

### 12.1 Investment Guarantees
The Grantor shall provide the Concessionaire with the following investment guarantees:
- (a) **Stability of Terms:** The Grantor shall not unilaterally amend the tax incentives, land rent, or royalty rates for the first **[10] years** of the Operations Period, except by mutual agreement;
- (b) **Expropriation Protection:** The Grantor shall not expropriate the Concessionaire's assets or rights without due process and fair compensation, as determined by an independent appraisal or international arbitration;
- (c) **Repatriation of Profits:** The Concessionaire shall have the right to repatriate profits, dividends, and capital in foreign currency (U.S. Dollars), subject to applicable foreign exchange regulations; and
- (d) **Dispute Resolution:** The Grantor shall honor the dispute resolution provisions of this Agreement (Section 16) and shall not invoke sovereign immunity in arbitration or enforcement proceedings.

### 12.2 Government Cooperation
The Grantor shall cooperate with the Concessionaire in obtaining all necessary permits, licenses, and approvals for the Facility and the Mineral Extraction Area, including:
- Environmental permits and EIA approvals;
- Building permits and construction licenses;
- Halal certification and veterinary approvals;
- Mining licenses for Critical Minerals (if separate from this Agreement);
- Import and export licenses;
- Employment permits for expatriate staff; and
- Customs and port clearances.

The Grantor shall establish a dedicated liaison officer or concession oversight committee to facilitate communication and resolve issues.

## 13. TERMINATION

### 13.1 Termination by Grantor for Default
The Grantor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the Concessionaire:
- (a) Fails to pay any fee or royalty when due and does not cure within [30] days;
- (b) Fails to achieve the Operations Commencement Date within [3] years of the Effective Date (subject to Force Majeure and Grantor delays);
- (c) Materially breaches the development obligations (Section 4) or production milestones and does not cure within [90] days;
- (d) Materially breaches the halal processing standards (Section 5.1) or loses halal certification and does not cure within [60] days;
- (e) Materially breaches the environmental obligations (Section 15) and does not cure within [90] days;
- (f) Abandons the Facility or ceases operations for a period of [6] months (other than due to Force Majeure);
- (g) Commits fraud, misrepresentation, or bribery in connection with this Agreement;
- (h) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed; or
- (i) Transfers this Agreement in violation of Section 14.

### 13.2 Termination by Concessionaire for Default
The Concessionaire may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the Grantor:
- (a) Materially breaches its obligations (e.g., failure to provide infrastructure, failure to issue permits, expropriation without compensation) and does not cure within [90] days;
- (b) Fails to maintain the tax incentives or investment guarantees (Section 12) and does not cure within [90] days; or
- (c) Engages in conduct that makes it impossible for the Concessionaire to operate the Facility in accordance with this Agreement.

### 13.3 Termination by Mutual Agreement
The Parties may terminate this Agreement by mutual written agreement, upon terms negotiated between the Parties, including compensation for the Concessionaire's invested capital and ongoing obligations.

### 13.4 Termination for Force Majeure
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [180] days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon [30] days' written notice. Upon termination for Force Majeure, the Grantor shall compensate the Concessionaire for the fair market value of its capital investment in the Facility (as determined by an independent appraisal), minus any amounts drawn from the Environmental Bond.

### 13.5 Compensation on Termination
Upon termination by the Grantor for the Concessionaire's default (other than fraud or bribery), the Grantor shall compensate the Concessionaire for the fair market value of its capital investment in the Facility, minus any unpaid fees, royalties, or penalties, and minus any amounts drawn from the Environmental Bond. - (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event; and
- (c) Resume performance as soon as practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

### 17.3 Extended Force Majeure
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [180] days, either Party may terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 13.4.
