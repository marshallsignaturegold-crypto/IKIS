## 1. DEFINED TERMS

**"AISTA"** means the AISTA Corridor under the Sovereign SafeTrade Program (SSTP).

**"BCRs"** means Binding Corporate Rules, a mechanism for cross-border data transfers within a multinational group of companies, approved by a data protection authority.

**"CCPA"** means the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020).

**"Child"** means a natural person under the age of [13 / 16 / 18], depending on the applicable jurisdiction (e.g., under 13 under COPPA; under 16 under GDPR; under 18 under some jurisdictions).

**"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.

**"Cross-Border Transfer"** means the transfer of personal data from one jurisdiction to another, including transfers between the Enterprise entities, to third-party processors, or to cloud service providers.

**"Customer Data"** means personal data collected from customers, including loyalty program members, online ordering users, delivery platform users, and in-store purchasers.

**"DPA"** means a Data Processing Agreement (or Data Processing Addendum), a contract between a Controller and a Processor that sets out the terms and conditions for the processing of personal data.

**"DPIA"** means a Data Protection Impact Assessment (or Privacy Impact Assessment), a systematic evaluation of the privacy risks and mitigations associated with a processing activity.

**"Data Protection Officer"** or **"DPO"** means the individual appointed by the Enterprise to oversee data privacy compliance, as required by applicable law (e.g., GDPR, India DPDP Act).

**"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed by the Enterprise.

**"Employee Data"** means personal data collected from employees, contractors, and job applicants, including HR records, payroll data, performance evaluations, and monitoring data.

**"Franchisee Data"** means personal data collected from franchisees, franchisee employees, franchise applicants, and franchise investors, including financial data, operational data, and compliance data.

**"GDPR"** means the Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (General Data Protection Regulation), applicable to the processing of personal data of data subjects in the European Union.

**"India DPDP Act 2023"** means the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India), applicable to the processing of digital personal data of data subjects in India.

**"Kenya DPA"** means the Data Protection Act, 2019 (Kenya), applicable to the processing of personal data of data subjects in Kenya.

**"PDPL (UAE)"** means the Personal Data Protection Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 45/2021) of the United Arab Emirates, applicable to the processing of personal data of data subjects in the UAE.

**"PDPL (Saudi)"** means the Personal Data Protection Law (Royal Decree M/19/2023) of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, applicable to the processing of personal data of data subjects in Saudi Arabia.

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including name, identification number, location data, online identifier, contact information, financial information, health information, and any other information that can be used to identify a natural person.

**"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**"Processor"** means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes personal data on behalf of the Controller.

**"ROPA"** means a Record of Processing Activities, a comprehensive inventory of all processing activities conducted by the Enterprise, as required by applicable law (e.g., GDPR Article 30, India DPDP Act Section 10).

**"SCCs"** means Standard Contractual Clauses, a mechanism for cross-border data transfers approved by the European Commission or other data protection authorities (e.g., the EU SCCs under GDPR, the UK SCCs, and other jurisdiction-specific SCCs).

**"Sensitive Personal Data"** or **"Special Category Data"** means personal data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, sex life or sexual orientation, and, in some jurisdictions, financial data, government identifiers, and children's data.

**"SSTP"** means the Sovereign SafeTrade Program.

- Processes personal data of employees or contractors located in the jurisdiction.

The Enterprise shall conduct a jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction analysis to determine the applicability of each data privacy law and shall implement compliance measures accordingly.

## 4. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

### 4.1 Legal Basis Categories
Under the applicable data privacy laws, the Enterprise shall identify and document a valid legal basis for each processing activity. The legal basis categories are:

| Legal Basis | Description | Applicable Jurisdictions | Example Processing Activities |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Consent** | Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes | GDPR, UK GDPR, PDPL (UAE), PDPL (Saudi), DPDP Act (India), DPA (Kenya), CCPA/CPRA | Marketing emails, cookies, loyalty program enrollment, newsletter subscriptions, children's data |
| **Contract** | Processing necessary for the performance of a contract or pre-contractual steps | GDPR, UK GDPR, PDPL (UAE), PDPL (Saudi), DPDP Act (India), DPA (Kenya), CCPA/CPRA | Franchise agreements, employment contracts, online ordering, supply chain transactions, investor agreements |
| **Legal Obligation** | Processing necessary for compliance with a legal obligation | GDPR, UK GDPR, PDPL (UAE), PDPL (Saudi), DPDP Act (India), DPA (Kenya), CCPA/CPRA | Tax reporting, regulatory filings, KYC/AML compliance, employment law compliance, court orders |
| **Legitimate Interest** | Processing necessary for the legitimate interests of the Enterprise or a third party, except where overridden by the data subject's interests | GDPR, UK GDPR, DPA (Kenya), CCPA/CPRA (opt-out right) | Analytics, fraud prevention, network security, internal reporting, product development, customer service |
| **Vital Interests** | Processing necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another person | GDPR, UK GDPR, PDPL (UAE), PDPL (Saudi), DPA (Kenya) | Emergency medical treatment, health and safety incidents |
| **Public Task** | Processing necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest | GDPR, UK GDPR | Rarely applicable to the Enterprise |

### 4.2 Consent Requirements
Consent shall be obtained in accordance with the strictest applicable standard (typically GDPR). Consent shall be:
- **Freely given:** The data subject shall have a genuine choice and shall not be penalized for refusing consent. Consent shall not be a condition of service unless the processing is strictly necessary for the service.
- **Specific:** Consent shall be obtained for each distinct processing purpose. Bundled or blanket consent shall not be used.
- **Informed:** The data subject shall be provided with clear, plain-language information about the processing, including the controller's identity, the purpose, the data types, the retention period, and the data subject's rights.
- **Unambiguous:** Consent shall be indicated by a clear affirmative action (e.g., ticking a box, clicking a button, signing a form). Pre-ticked boxes, implied consent, or silence shall not constitute valid consent.
- **Documented:** The Enterprise shall maintain a record of when and how consent was obtained, including the version of the consent form, the date and time, and the method of consent (e.g., online form, paper form, verbal consent recorded).
- **Withdrawable:** The data subject shall be informed of the right to withdraw consent at any time, and the withdrawal process shall be as easy as the consent process. Withdrawal shall not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal.

### 4.3 Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)
For processing activities based on legitimate interest, the Enterprise shall conduct a Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) that documents:
- (a) The legitimate interest pursued (e.g., fraud prevention, network security, product development, internal analytics);
- (b) The necessity of the processing for the legitimate interest;
- (c) The impact on the data subject's privacy and rights; and
- (d) The balancing of the legitimate interest against the data subject's interests, rights, and freedoms.

The LIA shall be reviewed annually and shall be made available to the DPO and the data protection authorities upon request.

## 5. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

### 5.1 Rights Under Applicable Laws
The Enterprise shall respect and facilitate the exercise of the following data subject rights, as applicable under each jurisdiction:

| Right | Description | GDPR / UK GDPR | UAE PDPL | Saudi PDPL | India DPDP Act | Kenya DPA | CCPA / CPRA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Right to Access** | Obtain confirmation of processing and a copy of personal data | Yes (Art. 15) | Yes | Yes | Yes (Sec. 11) | Yes | Yes (right to know) |
| **Right to Rectification** | Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data | Yes (Art. 16) | Yes | Yes | Yes (Sec. 12) | Yes | Yes (right to correct) |
| **Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)** | Request deletion of personal data | Yes (Art. 17) | Yes (with limitations) | Yes (with limitations) | Yes (Sec. 12) | Yes | Yes (right to delete) |
| **Right to Restriction** | Request restriction of processing | Yes (Art. 18) | Yes (with limitations) | Yes (with limitations) | Yes (Sec. 12) | Yes | Limited |
| **Right to Data Portability** | Receive data in a structured, machine-readable format and transmit it to another controller | Yes (Art. 20) | No | No | No | No | Yes (limited) |
| **Right to Object** | Object to processing based on legitimate interest or direct marketing | Yes (Art. 21) | Yes (direct marketing) | Yes (direct marketing) | Yes (Sec. 12) | Yes | Yes (right to opt-out of sale/sharing) |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Withdraw consent at any time | Yes (Art. 7) | Yes | Yes | Yes (Sec. 6) | Yes | Yes |
| **Right to Non-Discrimination** | Not be discriminated against for exercising privacy rights | No (explicit) | No | No | No | No | Yes (CCPA/CPRA) |
| **Right to Opt-Out of Sale / Sharing** | Opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal data for cross-context behavioral advertising | No (explicit) | No | No | No | No | Yes (CPRA) |
| **Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Data** | Limit the use and disclosure of sensitive personal information | No (explicit) | No | No | No (but DPDP Act has additional safeguards for sensitive data) | No | Yes (CPRA) |
| **Right to Appeal** | Appeal a controller's refusal to act on a data subject request | No (explicit) | No | No | No | No | Yes (CPRA) |
| **Right to Lodge a Complaint** | Lodge a complaint with the data protection authority | Yes (Art. 77) | Yes | Yes | Yes (Sec. 13) | Yes | Yes (with AG/CPPA) |

### 5.2 Data Subject Request (DSR) Process
The Enterprise shall implement a standardized Data Subject Request (DSR) process across all entities and jurisdictions, adapted to local requirements. The DSR process shall include:
- (a) **Request Submission:** Data subjects may submit requests via a dedicated privacy portal, email, phone, or mail. The request form shall be available in English and local languages (Arabic, Hindi, Swahili, etc.).
- (b) **Identity Verification:** The Enterprise shall verify the identity of the data subject before processing the request, using reasonable methods (e.g., email verification, ID document, account login). For sensitive requests (e.g., erasure, rectification), additional verification may be required.
- (c) **Request Processing:** The Enterprise shall process the request within the applicable timeframe:
  - GDPR / UK GDPR: [1 month] (extendable to 3 months for complex requests);
  - UAE PDPL: [30 days] (or as specified by the DPA);
  - Saudi PDPL: [30 days] (or as specified by SDAIA);
  - India DPDP Act: [30 days] (or as specified by the DPBI);
  - Kenya DPA: [30 days] (or as specified by the ODPC);
  - CCPA / CPRA: [45 days] (extendable to 90 days for complex requests).
- (d) **Response:** The Enterprise shall provide a clear, plain-language response, including the outcome of the request, the data subject's right to appeal, and the contact information for the DPO and the data protection authority.
- (e) **Record-Keeping:** The Enterprise shall maintain a record of all DSRs, including the date of receipt, the nature of the request, the verification method, the processing steps, the response date, and the outcome.
- (f) **Appeal Process:** If the Enterprise refuses to act on a request (in whole or in part), the data subject shall be informed of the reasons for refusal and the right to appeal to the DPO or the data protection authority.

## 6. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFERS

### 6.1 Transfer Mechanisms
The Enterprise shall implement appropriate safeguards for all cross-border transfers of personal data, using one or more of the following mechanisms:

| Mechanism | Description | Applicable Jurisdictions | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adequacy Decision** | Transfer to a country recognized as providing an adequate level of data protection | EU / UK (e.g., Japan, South Korea, UK-EU adequacy, etc.) | Transfers to EU/UK-approved adequate countries |
| **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** | EU Commission-approved contractual clauses (Module 1: Controller-Controller; Module 2: Controller-Processor; Module 3: Processor-Processor; Module 4: Processor-Controller) | EU, UK, India (DPDP Act recognizes SCCs), UAE (PDPL recognizes SCCs), Saudi (PDPL recognizes SCCs), Kenya (DPA recognizes SCCs) | Transfers to countries without adequacy decisions; used for most Enterprise transfers |
| **UK Addendum to SCCs** | UK-specific addendum to the EU SCCs, incorporating UK GDPR requirements | UK | Transfers from the UK to non-adequate countries |
| **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)** | Internal rules for data transfers within a multinational group, approved by a DPA | EU, UK (for intra-group transfers) | Intra-group transfers between Enterprise entities in multiple jurisdictions |
| **Certification Mechanisms** | Approved certification schemes (e.g., EU Cloud Code of Conduct) | EU | Transfers to certified cloud providers or processors |
| **Derogations / Exceptions** | Specific derogations for occasional transfers (e.g., explicit consent, contract performance, vital interests, public register) | EU, UK, UAE, Saudi, India, Kenya | Occasional, non-repetitive transfers where no other mechanism is available |
| **Local Data Localization Laws** | Laws requiring data to be stored or processed within the jurisdiction | India (government data), UAE (certain sectors), Saudi (certain sectors), China (not applicable to Enterprise) | Compliance with local data localization requirements; may require local data centers or processing |

### 6.2 Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)
For each cross-border transfer mechanism (SCCs, BCRs), the Enterprise shall conduct a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) that evaluates:
- (a) The laws and practices of the destination country regarding government access to data (e.g., surveillance laws, national security laws, law enforcement access);
- (b) The adequacy of the contractual, technical, and organizational safeguards in the SCCs or BCRs;
- (c) The nature of the data being transferred (e.g., standard personal data, sensitive personal data, children's data);
- (d) The purpose and duration of the transfer; and
- (e) The supplementary measures that may be necessary to ensure an adequate level of protection (e.g., encryption, pseudonymization, additional contractual provisions).

The TIA shall be documented and reviewed annually or upon any material change in the destination country's laws or practices.

### 6.3 Cross-Border Transfer Register
The Enterprise shall maintain a Cross-Border Transfer Register that records:
- The data exporter (entity, jurisdiction, DPO contact);
- The data importer (entity, jurisdiction, DPO contact);
- The data types and volumes transferred;
- The transfer mechanism (SCCs, BCRs, adequacy, derogation);
- The date of the transfer and the duration;
- The TIA reference and the date of the TIA;
- The supplementary measures implemented; and
- The review date and the responsible officer.

### 6.4 SCC Implementation
The Enterprise shall use the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (as updated in 2021) for all cross-border transfers to non-adequate countries. The SCCs shall be adapted as follows:
- **Module 1 (Controller-Controller):** For transfers between Enterprise entities (e.g., Parent to UAE subsidiary, Parent to India subsidiary) where both entities are controllers.
- **Module 2 (Controller-Processor):** For transfers from an Enterprise entity to a third-party processor (e.g., cloud provider, payment processor, marketing platform, delivery platform).
- **Module 3 (Processor-Processor):** For transfers from a third-party processor to a sub-processor (e.g., cloud provider to a sub-processor for data storage).
- **Module 4 (Processor-Controller):** For transfers from a third-party processor back to the Enterprise entity (e.g., delivery platform returning customer data to the franchisee).

The SCCs shall be signed by both parties and shall be supplemented by a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) where the recipient is a processor. The SCCs shall include the optional clauses for government access requests and the supplementary measures for encryption and pseudonymization.

### 6.5 BCR Implementation
The Enterprise shall develop and submit Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) for approval by the lead supervisory authority (e.g., the DPA of the primary EU establishment). The BCRs shall cover all Enterprise entities and shall establish:
- The binding nature of the BCRs for all employees and entities;
- The data protection principles (lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality, accountability);
- The data subject rights and the mechanisms for exercising them;
- The liability and compensation mechanisms;
- The cooperation with data protection authorities; and
- The monitoring and audit mechanisms.

BCR development is a complex and lengthy process. The Enterprise shall engage qualified BCR counsel and shall target BCR approval within [2–3] years.

### 6.6 Data Localization
The Enterprise shall comply with data localization requirements in the following jurisdictions:
- **India:** Government data (data processed by government entities or on behalf of the government) must be stored and processed in India. The DPDP Act allows the government to notify categories of personal data that must be stored in India. The Enterprise shall monitor notifications and shall implement local data storage in India if required.
- **UAE:** Certain sectors (e.g., healthcare, financial services, government) may have data localization requirements. The Enterprise shall monitor sector-specific regulations and shall implement local data storage if required.
- **Saudi Arabia:** Certain sectors (e.g., healthcare, government, critical infrastructure) may have data localization requirements under the National Data Management Office (NDMO) regulations. The Enterprise shall monitor NDMO notifications and shall implement local data storage if required.
- **Other Jurisdictions:** The Enterprise shall monitor data localization developments in all jurisdictions and shall implement local data storage if required by law.

The Enterprise shall use a multi-region cloud infrastructure (e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) with data centers in the US, EU, UAE, India, and Kenya to support data localization requirements.

## 7. DATA SECURITY

### 7.1 Security Principles
The Enterprise shall implement a comprehensive data security program based on the following principles:
- **Confidentiality:** Personal data shall be accessible only to authorized individuals and systems.
- **Integrity:** Personal data shall be accurate, complete, and protected against unauthorized modification or destruction.
- **Availability:** Personal data and the systems processing it shall be available when needed, with appropriate redundancy and recovery mechanisms.
- **Accountability:** The Enterprise shall be accountable for data security, with clear roles, responsibilities, and audit trails.

### 7.2 Technical and Organizational Security Measures
The Enterprise shall implement the following technical and organizational security measures:

| Measure | Description | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **Encryption** | Encryption of personal data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+) | All databases, file storage, backups, and network communications |
| **Access Controls** | Role-based access control (RBAC), least privilege, multi-factor authentication (MFA), and password policies | All systems, applications, and cloud services; MFA enforced for all administrative and privileged accounts |
| **Network Security** | Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS), network segmentation, VPNs for remote access, and secure Wi-Fi | All corporate networks, data centers, cloud environments, and franchisee networks |
| **Endpoint Security** | Anti-malware, endpoint detection and response (EDR), patch management, device encryption, and mobile device management (MDM) | All corporate devices, franchisee devices, and employee devices (BYOD) |
| **Application Security** | Secure development lifecycle (SDLC), code reviews, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, and web application firewalls (WAF) | All Enterprise applications, POS systems, online ordering platforms, and mobile apps |
| **Data Loss Prevention (DLP)** | DLP tools to prevent unauthorized exfiltration of personal data via email, cloud storage, USB, or other channels | All corporate email, cloud storage, and file transfer systems |
| **Security Monitoring** | Security information and event management (SIEM), log management, threat intelligence, and 24/7 security operations center (SOC) | Centralized security monitoring for all Enterprise systems and cloud environments |
| **Vulnerability Management** | Regular vulnerability scanning, patch management, and remediation of critical vulnerabilities | Monthly vulnerability scans; critical patches within 24–48 hours |
| **Penetration Testing** | Annual external penetration testing and bi-annual internal penetration testing by qualified third-party testers | All critical systems (POS, online ordering, cloud infrastructure, payment systems) |
| **Incident Response** | Incident response plan, incident response team, and regular incident response drills | Documented incident response plan; 24/7 incident response capability; annual drills |
| **Backup and Recovery** | Regular backups, off-site storage, encryption of backups, and tested disaster recovery procedures | Daily backups; off-site storage in a different geographic region; annual disaster recovery test |
| **Physical Security** | Access controls, surveillance, and environmental controls for data centers, offices, and facilities | Biometric access, CCTV, fire suppression, and environmental monitoring for all data centers and critical facilities |
| **Third-Party Security** | Security assessments of third-party processors and vendors; DPA security requirements; regular audits | Annual security assessments of all critical vendors; SCCs and DPAs with security clauses |

### 7.3 Security Standards and Certifications
The Enterprise shall pursue and maintain the following security certifications and standards:
- **ISO 27001:** Information Security Management System (ISMS) certification for the Enterprise's IT infrastructure and data centers.
- **PCI DSS:** Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard compliance for all entities processing payment card data (franchisees, online ordering platforms, POS systems).
- **SOC 2 Type II:** Service Organization Control 2 (Trust Services Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy) for cloud services and SaaS providers.
- **ISO 27701:** Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) extension to ISO 27001, specifically for privacy management.
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework:** Adoption of the NIST CSF for risk management and security governance.

### 7.4 Incident Response Plan
The Enterprise shall maintain an Incident Response Plan (IRP) that defines the procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from data security incidents (including personal data breaches). The IRP shall include:
- (a) **Incident Detection:** Automated monitoring (SIEM, IDS, DLP), manual detection (employee reports, customer complaints), and third-party notifications (law enforcement, regulators, vendors).
- (b) **Incident Classification:** Classification of incidents by severity (low, medium, high, critical) and type (unauthorized access, data exfiltration, ransomware, insider threat, system failure, physical breach).
- (c) **Incident Response Team:** A cross-functional incident response team (IRT) comprising the DPO, CISO, General Counsel, IT, HR, Communications, and relevant business unit representatives. The IRT shall be activated for all high and critical incidents.
- (d) **Containment and Eradication:** Immediate steps to contain the incident (isolate affected systems, revoke compromised credentials, block malicious IPs), eradicate the threat (remove malware, patch vulnerabilities, restore clean backups), and recover affected systems.
- (e) **Investigation and Forensics:** Preservation of evidence, forensic analysis, root cause analysis, and identification of affected data subjects and data types.
- (f) **Notification:** Notification of the breach to the affected data subjects, the data protection authorities, and other relevant parties, in accordance with Section 11 (Breach Notification).
- (g) **Post-Incident Review:** A post-incident review (PIR) to identify lessons learned, update security measures, and revise the IRP. The PIR shall be documented and reported to the Board and the Risk Committee.

### 8.3 Deletion and Destruction Procedures
When the retention period expires, or when a data subject exercises the right to erasure, the Enterprise shall securely delete or destroy the personal data in accordance with the following procedures:
- (a) **Electronic Data:** Secure deletion using NIST-approved methods (e.g., cryptographic erasure, secure overwrite, degaussing for magnetic media). Cloud data shall be deleted using the cloud provider's secure deletion tools, with verification of deletion.
- (b) **Physical Media:** Secure destruction of physical media (hard drives, tapes, USB drives, paper records) using shredding, incineration, or pulverization. Third-party destruction services shall be certified and shall provide a certificate of destruction.
- (c) **Backups:** Personal data in backups shall be deleted or rendered inaccessible when the backup retention period expires. If a backup is restored, the deleted data shall be re-deleted.
- (d) **Third-Party Processors:** The Enterprise shall ensure that third-party processors delete or destroy personal data in accordance with the DPA and the Enterprise's deletion procedures. The Enterprise shall request and verify certificates of deletion.
- (e) **Documentation:** The Enterprise shall maintain a record of all deletions, including the date, the data category, the deletion method, the responsible officer, and the verification method.

### 8.4 Archival and Anonymization
If personal data is retained for archival, research, or statistical purposes beyond the standard retention period, the Enterprise shall:
- (a) Anonymize or pseudonymize the data to the extent possible, so that the data subject is no longer identifiable;
- (b) Implement technical and organizational measures to prevent re-identification;
- (c) Limit access to the archived data to authorized personnel with a legitimate need;
- (d) Document the purpose of the archival and the measures taken to protect the data; and
- (e) Review the archival necessity annually and delete the data if it is no longer necessary.

## 9. PRIVACY BY DESIGN AND DEFAULT

### 9.1 Privacy by Design Principles
The Enterprise shall integrate privacy into the design of all products, services, systems, and processes, in accordance with the seven Foundational Principles of Privacy by Design (Ann Cavoukian, 2011):
- (a) **Proactive not Reactive:** Anticipate and prevent privacy-invasive events before they happen.
- (b) **Privacy as the Default:** Personal data shall be automatically protected in any given IT system or business practice. The default settings shall be the most privacy-friendly settings.
- (c) **Privacy Embedded into Design:** Privacy shall be embedded into the design specifications of technologies, business practices, and physical infrastructures.
- (d) **Full Functionality:** Privacy by design shall accommodate all legitimate interests and objectives (e.g., security, functionality, innovation) in a positive-sum manner, not a zero-sum approach.
- (e) **End-to-End Security:** Privacy shall be protected throughout the entire data lifecycle, from collection to destruction.
- (f) **Visibility and Transparency:** Privacy by design shall be visible and transparent to users and providers. Privacy practices shall be documented and communicated.
- (g) **Respect for User Privacy:** Privacy by design shall respect user privacy and empower users to exercise their privacy rights.

### 9.2 Privacy by Default Settings
The Enterprise shall implement the following privacy-by-default settings for all products, services, and systems:
- (a) **Data Minimization:** Collect only the personal data that is strictly necessary for the specific purpose. Do not collect data "just in case."
- (b) **Purpose Limitation:** Use personal data only for the purpose for which it was collected. Do not use data for secondary purposes without a new legal basis or consent.
- (c) **Storage Minimization:** Retain personal data only for the minimum necessary period. Delete or anonymize data when it is no longer needed.
- (d) **Opt-In for Marketing:** Marketing communications (email, SMS, push notifications) shall be opt-in only. Pre-ticked boxes or implied consent shall not be used.
- (e) **Opt-In for Non-Essential Cookies:** Non-essential cookies (analytics, advertising, social media) shall be disabled by default. Users must opt-in to enable non-essential cookies.
- (f) **Minimal Data Sharing:** Share personal data with third parties only when necessary and with appropriate safeguards (DPAs, SCCs, encryption).
- (g) **Anonymization and Pseudonymization:** Use anonymization or pseudonymization where possible, especially for analytics, research, and product development.
- (h) **Transparent Privacy Notices:** Provide clear, concise, and plain-language privacy notices at the point of data collection. Privacy notices shall be easily accessible and understandable.
- (i) **User Control:** Provide users with easy-to-use tools to exercise their privacy rights (access, rectification, erasure, opt-out, data portability).
- (j) **Secure Defaults:** Use strong encryption, secure authentication, and secure configurations by default. Do not use weak passwords, default credentials, or unencrypted communications.

### 9.3 Privacy by Design in Product Development
The Enterprise shall incorporate privacy by design into its product development lifecycle (SDLC), including:
- (a) **Privacy Requirements:** Define privacy requirements (data minimization, purpose limitation, user consent, data subject rights, security) at the start of the project.
- (b) **Privacy Review:** Conduct a privacy review at each stage of the SDLC (design, development, testing, deployment). The privacy review shall assess compliance with privacy requirements and identify privacy risks.
- (c) **Privacy Testing:** Conduct privacy testing (e.g., consent flow testing, cookie testing, data flow testing, security testing) as part of the quality assurance process.
- (d) **Privacy Documentation:** Document the privacy design decisions, data flows, and privacy controls in the project documentation.
- (e) **Privacy Sign-Off:** Require a privacy sign-off from the DPO or privacy team before product launch. The sign-off shall confirm that the product meets privacy requirements and that a DPIA has been conducted (if required).

## 10. PRIVACY IMPACT ASSESSMENTS (DPIA)

### 10.1 DPIA Trigger Events
A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) shall be conducted for any processing activity that is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects. The Enterprise shall conduct a DPIA for the following trigger events (non-exhaustive list):
- (a) Systematic and extensive evaluation of personal aspects (e.g., profiling, automated decision-making, scoring, risk assessment);
- (b) Large-scale processing of sensitive personal data (e.g., health data, biometric data, financial data, children's data);
- (c) Large-scale monitoring of public areas (e.g., CCTV, facial recognition, location tracking);
- (d) New technologies or processing methods that may pose privacy risks (e.g., AI, machine learning, blockchain, biometrics);
- (e) Cross-border transfers of sensitive personal data or large-scale data;
- (f) Processing of children's data for marketing, profiling, or online services;
- (g) High-risk processing identified by the DPO or the data protection authority;
- (h) Changes to existing processing activities that increase the risk to data subjects; and
- (i) Any processing activity that the DPO or the Risk Committee determines requires a DPIA.

### 10.2 DPIA Process
The DPIA shall follow a structured process:
- (a) **Identify the Need for a DPIA:** The project team or DPO identifies a trigger event and initiates the DPIA.
- (b) **Describe the Processing:** Document the nature, scope, context, and purposes of the processing, including the data types, data subjects, processing activities, and data flows.
- (c) **Assess Necessity and Proportionality:** Assess whether the processing is necessary and proportionate to the intended purpose. Consider data minimization, purpose limitation, and less privacy-invasive alternatives.
- (d) **Identify and Assess Risks:** Identify the privacy risks to data subjects (e.g., unauthorized access, data breach, discrimination, loss of control, reputational harm). Assess the likelihood and severity of each risk.
- (e) **Identify Mitigation Measures:** Identify and implement measures to mitigate the risks (e.g., encryption, anonymization, access controls, consent mechanisms, data subject rights, transparency, training). Assess the residual risk after mitigation.
- (f) **Consult Stakeholders:** Consult with the DPO, the project team, the data subjects (where appropriate), and the data protection authority (if required by law or if the residual risk is high).
- (g) **Document and Approve:** Document the DPIA in a standard template (see Section 10.3). The DPIA shall be approved by the DPO and the project sponsor. If the residual risk is high, the DPIA shall be escalated to the Risk Committee and the Board.
- (h) **Monitor and Review:** Monitor the processing activity and the effectiveness of the mitigation measures. Review the DPIA annually or upon any material change in the processing activity.

### 10.3 DPIA Template
A standard DPIA template shall be maintained by the DPO and shall include the following sections:
- (a) Project identification (name, description, owner, date, DPO contact);
- (b) Processing description (data types, data subjects, purposes, data flows, legal basis, retention);
- (c) Necessity and proportionality assessment (purpose, data minimization, alternatives, proportionality conclusion);
- (d) Risk assessment (risk description, likelihood, severity, risk level, mitigation measures, residual risk);
- (e) Stakeholder consultation (consultation method, participants, feedback, outcomes);
- (f) Data protection authority consultation (if required, consultation reference, authority response);
- (g) Approval (DPO sign-off, project sponsor sign-off, Risk Committee sign-off if escalated);
- (h) Monitoring and review plan (review date, responsible officer, monitoring measures); and
- (i) Appendices (data flow diagrams, legal basis documentation, consent forms, DPA references, SCC references).

### 10.4 DPIA Register
The Enterprise shall maintain a DPIA Register that records all DPIAs conducted, including:
- The project name and description;
- The date of the DPIA and the review date;
- The DPO and project sponsor;
- The risk level (low, medium, high, critical);
- The mitigation measures and the residual risk;
- The approval status and the escalation level; and
- The monitoring and review plan.

## 11. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 11.1 Breach Definition
A **"personal data breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed. This includes:
- (a) **Confidentiality Breach:** Unauthorized or accidental disclosure of, or access to, personal data.
- (b) **Integrity Breach:** Unauthorized or accidental alteration of personal data.
- (c) **Availability Breach:** Unauthorized or accidental loss of access to, or destruction of, personal data.

### 11.2 Breach Assessment
When a personal data breach is detected, the Enterprise shall conduct a breach assessment to determine:
- (a) The nature of the breach (confidentiality, integrity, availability);
- (b) The data types and categories affected (e.g., names, contact information, financial data, health data, children's data);
- (c) The number of data subjects affected;
- (d) The likely consequences for the data subjects (e.g., identity theft, financial loss, reputational harm, discrimination, physical harm);
- (e) The severity of the breach (low, medium, high, critical); and
- (f) The remedial actions taken or planned (e.g., containment, eradication, recovery, notification, compensation).

### 11.3 Breach Notification Timeline
The Enterprise shall notify personal data breaches in accordance with the following timelines:

| Jurisdiction | Authority Notification | Data Subject Notification | Responsible Officer |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GDPR / UK GDPR** | Within 72 hours of becoming aware (to the lead DPA) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / Data Protection Lead |
| **UAE PDPL** | Within [72 hours] of becoming aware (to the DPA) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / UAE Privacy Lead |
| **Saudi PDPL** | Within [72 hours] of becoming aware (to SDAIA) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / KSA Privacy Lead |
| **India DPDP Act** | Within [72 hours] of becoming aware (to the DPBI) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / India Privacy Lead |
| **Kenya DPA** | Within [72 hours] of becoming aware (to the ODPC) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / Kenya Privacy Lead |
| **CCPA / CPRA** | No explicit authority notification (unless required by state law) | Without undue delay (if high risk) | DPO / US Privacy Lead |
| **Other Jurisdictions** | As required by local law | As required by local law | DPO / Local Privacy Lead |

### 11.4 Authority Notification Content
The notification to the data protection authority shall include, where possible:
- (a) The nature of the personal data breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected, and the categories and approximate number of personal data records affected;
- (b) The name and contact details of the DPO or other contact point for more information;
- (c) The likely consequences of the personal data breach;
- (d) The measures taken or proposed to address the personal data breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects; and
- (e) Where appropriate, the measures taken to prevent similar breaches in the future.

If it is not possible to provide all the information at the same time, the notification may be made in phases without undue further delay.

### 11.5 Data Subject Notification Content
The notification to affected data subjects shall include, in clear and plain language:
- (a) The nature of the personal data breach;
- (b) The name and contact details of the DPO or other contact point for more information;
- (c) The likely consequences of the personal data breach;
- (d) The measures taken or proposed to address the personal data breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects; and
- (e) The data subject's rights and the steps the data subject can take to protect themselves (e.g., change passwords, monitor credit reports, report identity theft).

### 11.6 Documentation
The Enterprise shall maintain a record of all personal data breaches, including:
- The date and time of detection;
- The nature of the breach;
- The data types and categories affected;
- The number of data subjects affected;
- The severity assessment;
- The remedial actions taken;
- The notification dates and recipients (authority, data subjects, others);
- The post-incident review and lessons learned; and
- The responsible officer and the review date.

The breach record shall be maintained for a minimum of [5] years and shall be made available to the data protection authorities upon request.

## 12. VENDOR MANAGEMENT AND DPAs

### 12.1 Vendor Due Diligence
Before engaging any third-party vendor that will process personal data on behalf of the Enterprise (a **"Processor"**), the Enterprise shall conduct vendor due diligence to assess the vendor's data privacy and security capabilities. The due diligence shall include:
- (a) A review of the vendor's privacy policy, security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI DSS), and data protection practices;
- (b) A review of the vendor's sub-processor list and cross-border transfer mechanisms;
- (c) A security questionnaire or assessment (e.g., NIST, ISO 27001, CSA STAR);
- (d) A review of the vendor's incident response plan and breach notification procedures;
- (e) A review of the vendor's insurance coverage (cyber liability, professional liability); and
- (f) Reference checks and background checks (if the vendor will have access to sensitive data).

### 12.2 Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Template
The Enterprise shall execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with all Processors. The DPA shall be based on the applicable SCCs (for cross-border transfers) and shall include, at a minimum, the following provisions:
- (a) **Subject Matter and Duration:** The subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of the processing, and the types of personal data and categories of data subjects.
- (b) **Controller Obligations:** The Enterprise's obligations as a Controller (e.g., providing lawful instructions, ensuring data subject rights, maintaining the ROPA).
- (c) **Processor Obligations:** The Processor's obligations (e.g., processing only on documented instructions, ensuring confidentiality, implementing security measures, engaging sub-processors only with prior written consent, assisting with data subject requests, assisting with DPIAs, deleting or returning data at termination, providing audit rights).
- (d) **Sub-Processors:** The Processor shall not engage sub-processors without the Enterprise's prior written consent. The Processor shall maintain a list of sub-processors and shall notify the Enterprise of any changes. The Processor shall ensure that sub-processors are bound by the same data protection obligations.
- (e) **Security Measures:** The technical and organizational security measures implemented by the Processor (encryption, access controls, network security, incident response, etc.), as described in Section 7.
- (f) **Breach Notification:** The Processor shall notify the Enterprise of any personal data breach within [24 hours] of becoming aware. The notification shall include the details required for the Enterprise to comply with its breach notification obligations.
- (g) **Audit Rights:** The Enterprise shall have the right to audit the Processor's compliance with the DPA, including on-site audits, document reviews, and third-party audits. The Processor shall cooperate with the audit and shall provide all necessary information and assistance.
- (h) **Data Subject Rights:** The Processor shall assist the Enterprise in responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection).
- (i) **Cross-Border Transfers:** If the Processor transfers personal data to a third country, the Processor shall implement the applicable transfer mechanism (SCCs, adequacy, BCRs) and shall provide the Enterprise with the transfer documentation.
- (j) **Termination and Data Return/Deletion:** Upon termination of the DPA, the Processor shall return or delete all personal data (and copies thereof) in accordance with the Enterprise's instructions, and shall provide a certificate of deletion.
- (k) **Liability and Indemnification:** The Processor shall be liable for any breach of the DPA and shall indemnify the Enterprise for any damages, fines, or costs incurred as a result of the Processor's breach.
- (l) **Governing Law and Dispute Resolution:** The DPA shall be governed by the law of the Enterprise's primary jurisdiction and shall include dispute resolution provisions (e.g., arbitration, mediation).

### 12.3 Sub-Processor List
The Enterprise shall maintain a register of all Processors and sub-processors, including:
- The processor name, address, and contact details;
- The processing activity and the data types processed;
- The jurisdiction of the processor and any sub-processors;
- The transfer mechanism (SCCs, adequacy, BCRs);
- The DPA reference and the date of execution;
- The security certifications and assessments;
- The sub-processor list and the approval date; and
- The review date and the responsible officer.

### 12.4 Critical Vendor Categories
The Enterprise's critical vendor categories (requiring enhanced due diligence and DPAs) include:
- Cloud service providers (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) — e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud;
- Payment processors — e.g., Stripe, PayPal, local payment gateways;
- Delivery platforms — e.g., UberEats, Deliveroo, Talabat, Swiggy;
- Online ordering platforms — e.g., proprietary platforms, third-party platforms;
- POS system providers — e.g., Toast, Square, Oracle MICROS, local providers;
- CRM and marketing platforms — e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot, Mailchimp, Klaviyo;
- HR and payroll systems — e.g., Workday, ADP, local payroll providers;
- Analytics and tracking platforms — e.g., Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude;
- Cybersecurity and monitoring providers — e.g., SIEM, EDR, DLP providers;
- Legal and compliance consultants — e.g., law firms, compliance advisors, audit firms.

## 13. CHILDREN'S DATA

### 13.1 Children's Data Protection
The Enterprise shall implement special protections for children's personal data, in accordance with the applicable laws:
- **GDPR / UK GDPR:** Children under 16 (or under 13 with parental consent) require parental consent for information society services. The Enterprise shall make reasonable efforts to verify parental consent.
- **COPPA (US):** Children under 13 require verifiable parental consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of personal data. The Enterprise shall comply with COPPA if it operates websites, online services, or mobile apps directed to children under 13.
- **UAE PDPL:** Children require parental consent for the processing of their personal data. The Enterprise shall verify parental consent.
- **Saudi PDPL:** Children require parental consent for the processing of their personal data. The Enterprise shall verify parental consent.
- **India DPDP Act:** Children (under 18) require parental consent. The Enterprise shall not process personal data that is likely to cause harm to the child. The Enterprise shall not track or monitor the behavior of children or target advertising to children.
- **Kenya DPA:** Children require parental consent. The Enterprise shall protect children's data from harm.

### 13.2 Children's Data Processing Activities
The Enterprise's processing activities that may involve children's data include:
- (a) **Online Ordering:** Children may use the online ordering platform (with parental supervision). The Enterprise shall not collect personal data from children under 13 without parental consent.
- (b) **Loyalty Program:** Children may participate in the loyalty program (with parental consent). The Enterprise shall not collect sensitive data from children.
- (c) **Marketing:** The Enterprise shall not target marketing communications to children under 16 (or the applicable age threshold) without parental consent.
- (d) **Website and Apps:** The Enterprise's websites and apps shall include age-gating mechanisms and parental consent flows for children under the applicable age threshold.
- (e) **Franchisee Activities:** Franchisees shall be required to comply with children's data protection requirements in their local operations.

### 13.3 Parental Consent Verification
The Enterprise shall verify parental consent using reasonable methods, such as:
- (a) Credit card or debit card verification (a small charge to the parent's card);
- (b) Video conference with the parent;
- (c) Signed consent form (physical or digital) with a copy of the parent's ID;
- (d) Phone call to the parent; or
- (e) Other methods approved by the DPO and the data protection authorities.

The Enterprise shall maintain a record of parental consent, including the method of verification, the date, and the parent's contact information.

## 14. EMPLOYEE DATA

### 14.1 Employee Data Categories
The Enterprise collects and processes the following categories of employee data:
- (a) **Personal and Contact Information:** Name, address, phone number, email, emergency contacts, next of kin;
- (b) **Identification and Demographics:** National ID, passport, visa, work permit, date of birth, nationality, gender, photograph;
- (c) **Employment Information:** Job title, department, employment contract, start date, termination date, performance evaluations, disciplinary records, training records, attendance records;
- (d) **Financial Information:** Bank account details, salary, bonuses, deductions, tax information, social security numbers, pension contributions, expense claims;
- (e) **Health and Safety Information:** Medical records, disability accommodations, occupational health assessments, safety incident reports, workers' compensation claims;
- (f) **Benefits Information:** Health insurance, life insurance, retirement plans, leave balances, benefits elections;
- (g) **Monitoring Data:** Email monitoring, internet usage monitoring, CCTV footage, access logs, GPS tracking (for delivery drivers), keystroke logging (if applicable); and
- (h) **Background Check Data:** Criminal records, credit checks, reference checks, verification of qualifications.

### 14.2 Employee Data Processing Principles
- (a) **Lawful Basis:** Employee data shall be processed based on contract, legal obligation, or legitimate interest (for monitoring, security, and productivity). Consent shall not be used as the legal basis for employee data processing, as employees may not have a free choice.
- (b) **Transparency:** Employees shall be provided with a privacy notice (employee privacy notice) at the start of employment and upon any material change in processing. The notice shall explain the data types, purposes, legal basis, recipients, retention, and rights.
- (c) **Data Minimization:** Employee data shall be limited to what is necessary for the employment relationship, legal compliance, and legitimate business needs.
- (d) **Access and Correction:** Employees shall have the right to access and correct their personal data. The Enterprise shall provide a self-service portal or process for employees to exercise these rights.
- (e) **Monitoring:** Employee monitoring shall be proportionate, transparent, and justified by a legitimate interest (e.g., security, productivity, compliance). Employees shall be informed of monitoring practices (e.g., email monitoring, CCTV, internet usage). Covert monitoring shall be prohibited unless authorized by law and approved by the DPO and the General Counsel.
- (f) **BYOD:** If employees use personal devices (Bring Your Own Device — BYOD) for work purposes, the Enterprise shall implement a BYOD policy that requires device encryption, mobile device management (MDM), separation of work and personal data, and remote wipe capability for work data. The BYOD policy shall be agreed upon by the employee in writing.
- (g) **Cross-Border Transfers:** Employee data may be transferred between Enterprise entities (e.g., HR shared services, payroll processing) and to third-party processors (e.g., cloud HR systems, payroll providers). Cross-border transfers shall be protected by SCCs, BCRs, or other transfer mechanisms.
- (h) **Retention:** Employee data shall be retained in accordance with the retention schedule (Section 8). Upon termination, data shall be retained for the applicable period and then securely deleted or archived (if anonymized).

### 14.3 Employee Data Privacy Notice
The Enterprise shall provide an employee privacy notice that includes:
- The identity of the Controller and the DPO;
- The data types collected and the purposes of processing;
- The legal basis for processing;
- The recipients of the data (internal and external);
- The cross-border transfers and the transfer mechanisms;
- The data retention periods;
- The data subject rights and how to exercise them;
- The monitoring practices and the legal basis for monitoring;
- The BYOD policy (if applicable);
- The complaint mechanism (DPO contact, data protection authority); and
- The date of the notice and the review date.

## 15. FRANCHISEE DATA

### 15.1 Franchisee Data Categories
The Enterprise collects and processes the following categories of franchisee data:
- (a) **Franchisee Personal Data:** Name, contact information, identification, financial information, background check data, and franchise application data;
- (b) **Franchisee Business Data:** Business name, business address, tax ID, business registration, financial statements, operational data, sales data, royalty reports, and compliance data;
- (c) **Franchisee Employee Data:** Personal data of franchisee employees (if collected by the Enterprise for training, certification, or payroll processing);
- (d) **Franchisee Customer Data:** Personal data of customers collected by the franchisee and shared with the Enterprise (e.g., loyalty program data, online ordering data, delivery data); and
- (e) **Franchisee Financial Data:** Bank account details, payment information, royalty payments, marketing fund contributions, and tax information.

### 15.2 Franchisee Data Processing Principles
- (a) **Controller-Processor Relationship:** The Enterprise is typically a Controller for franchisee data (e.g., franchisee application data, franchisee financial data). The franchisee is typically a Controller for its own employee data and customer data. The Enterprise may be a Joint Controller with the franchisee for certain processing activities (e.g., loyalty program data, online ordering data). The roles and responsibilities shall be defined in the franchise agreement and the DPA.
- (b) **Franchise Agreement Privacy Clauses:** The franchise agreement shall include privacy clauses that require the franchisee to:
  - Comply with all applicable data privacy laws;
  - Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures;
  - Provide privacy notices to employees and customers;
  - Obtain consent for marketing and non-essential cookies;
  - Respond to data subject requests and notify the Enterprise;
  - Notify the Enterprise of any data breaches within [24 hours];
  - Not share customer data with third parties without the Enterprise's consent (except for delivery platforms, with a DPA);
  - Delete or return customer data upon termination of the franchise agreement; and
  - Allow the Enterprise to audit the franchisee's data privacy compliance.
- (c) **Franchisee DPA:** The Enterprise shall execute a DPA with each franchisee for the processing of customer data shared with the Enterprise (e.g., loyalty program data, online ordering data). The DPA shall define the roles (Controller-Processor or Joint Controller), the data types, the purposes, the security measures, the breach notification obligations, and the audit rights.
- (d) **Franchisee Training:** The Enterprise shall provide data privacy training to franchisees as part of the initial training program and ongoing training. The training shall cover privacy principles, legal requirements, consent management, data subject rights, breach notification, and security best practices.
- (e) **Franchisee Audits:** The Enterprise shall conduct periodic audits of franchisee data privacy compliance (e.g., annual self-assessments, spot checks, third-party audits). The audit shall assess compliance with the franchise agreement privacy clauses, the DPA, and the applicable data privacy laws.

## 16. CUSTOMER DATA

### 16.1 Customer Data Categories
The Enterprise collects and processes the following categories of customer data:
- (a) **Contact and Account Information:** Name, email, phone number, address, date of birth, gender, and account credentials (username, password);
- (b) **Transaction and Order Data:** Order history, purchase details, payment information, delivery addresses, and transaction receipts;
- (c) **Loyalty Program Data:** Loyalty points, rewards, preferences, purchase history, and engagement data;
- (d) **Online Ordering and Delivery Data:** Order details, delivery addresses, delivery instructions, driver tracking data, and delivery platform account data;
- (e) **Marketing and Communication Data:** Marketing preferences, consent records, email open rates, click-through rates, and communication history;
- (f) **Location Data:** GPS location (for delivery, store finder, and proximity marketing), IP address, and geolocation data;
- (g) **Device and Browser Data:** Device type, operating system, browser type, IP address, cookie data, and browsing history;
- (h) **Feedback and Support Data:** Customer reviews, complaints, support tickets, and survey responses;
- (i) **Social Media Data:** Social media profiles, interactions, and public posts (if the customer interacts with the Enterprise's social media accounts); and
- (j) **CCTV and In-Store Data:** CCTV footage of customers in franchisee locations (if applicable, with notice).

### 16.2 Customer Data Processing Principles
- (a) **Transparency:** Customers shall be provided with a clear, concise, and plain-language privacy notice at the point of data collection (e.g., website, app, in-store, loyalty program enrollment). The notice shall explain the data types, purposes, legal basis, recipients, retention, and rights.
- (b) **Consent:** Consent shall be obtained for all marketing communications, non-essential cookies, loyalty program enrollment, and any processing that is not strictly necessary for the transaction. Consent shall be opt-in, specific, informed, and unambiguous.
- (c) **Data Minimization:** The Enterprise shall collect only the data necessary for the specific purpose (e.g., name and address for delivery; email for marketing). The Enterprise shall not collect unnecessary data (e.g., not collecting health data unless required for a specific service).
- (d) **Purpose Limitation:** Customer data shall be used only for the purpose for which it was collected. Secondary uses (e.g., marketing, analytics, third-party sharing) shall require a separate legal basis or consent.
- (e) **Customer Rights:** The Enterprise shall facilitate the exercise of customer rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, opt-out) through a dedicated privacy portal, email, or phone. The DSR process shall be easy to use and shall be completed within the applicable timeframe.
- (f) **Loyalty Program:** The loyalty program shall have a separate privacy notice and consent process. Customers shall be informed of how their data is used for personalization, rewards, and analytics. Customers shall have the right to opt out of the loyalty program and request deletion of their loyalty data.
- (g) **Online Ordering:** The online ordering platform shall collect only the data necessary for order processing and delivery. Payment data shall be processed by a PCI DSS-compliant payment processor (tokenized; no storage of CVV). Delivery data shall be shared with the delivery platform only with a DPA and only for the purpose of delivery.
- (h) **Delivery Platforms:** The Enterprise shall execute DPAs with all delivery platforms and shall ensure that customer data is shared only for the purpose of delivery and is not used by the delivery platform for marketing or other purposes.
- (i) **Marketing:** Marketing communications shall be opt-in only. Customers shall have the right to opt out of marketing at any time (via unsubscribe link, preference center, or DSR). The Enterprise shall maintain a suppression list to ensure that opted-out customers are not contacted.
- (j) **Children:** The Enterprise shall not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 (or the applicable age threshold) without parental consent. Online ordering, loyalty programs, and marketing shall not be directed to children.
- (k) **CCTV:** If franchisees use CCTV in customer areas, the franchisee shall post clear notices and shall comply with local CCTV regulations. CCTV footage shall be retained for the minimum necessary period (e.g., 30 days) and shall be securely deleted.

## 17. RECORDS OF PROCESSING ACTIVITIES (ROPA)

### 17.1 ROPA Requirements
The Enterprise shall maintain a comprehensive Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) that documents all processing activities conducted by the Enterprise and its subsidiaries. The ROPA shall be maintained in accordance with the requirements of GDPR Article 30, the India DPDP Act Section 10, and other applicable laws. The ROPA shall be made available to the data protection authorities upon request.

### 17.2 ROPA Template
The ROPA shall be maintained in a standard template and shall include the following information for each processing activity:
- (a) **Processing Activity ID:** A unique identifier for each processing activity.
- (b) **Controller Information:** The name and contact details of the Controller (entity, DPO contact).
- (c) **Joint Controller Information:** If applicable, the name and contact details of any joint Controller.
- (d) **DPO Contact:** The name and contact details of the DPO.
- (e) **Processing Purpose:** The purpose(s) of the processing (e.g., customer service, marketing, payroll, compliance).
- (f) **Data Subject Categories:** The categories of data subjects (e.g., customers, employees, franchisees, suppliers, investors).
- (g) **Personal Data Categories:** The categories of personal data processed (e.g., name, contact, financial, health, biometric).
- (h) **Sensitive Personal Data:** Whether sensitive personal data or special category data is processed.
- (i) **Data Recipients:** The categories of recipients (internal and external) to whom the data is disclosed.
- (j) **Cross-Border Transfers:** Whether the data is transferred to third countries, and the transfer mechanisms (SCCs, adequacy, BCRs).
- (k) **Data Retention Period:** The retention period for each data category.
- (l) **Security Measures:** The technical and organizational security measures implemented for the processing activity.
- (m) **Legal Basis:** The legal basis for the processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest).
- (n) **Data Source:** The source of the data (data subject, third party, public source).
- (o) **Automated Decision-Making:** Whether automated decision-making or profiling is used, and the logic, significance, and consequences.
- (p) **DPIA Reference:** Whether a DPIA has been conducted for the processing activity, and the DPIA reference.
- (q) **Responsible Officer:** The name and contact details of the responsible officer for the processing activity.
- (r) **Review Date:** The date of the last review and the next review date.

### 17.3 ROPA Maintenance and Audit
- (a) **Maintenance:** The ROPA shall be maintained by the DPO and updated whenever a new processing activity is introduced, an existing activity is modified, or an activity is discontinued. The ROPA shall be reviewed annually.
- (b) **Integration:** The ROPA shall be integrated with the Enterprise's data inventory, data flow diagrams, and DPIA register. Changes to the ROPA shall trigger updates to the data inventory and DPIAs (if applicable).
- (c) **Audit:** The ROPA shall be audited by the internal audit function or an external auditor at least annually. The audit shall verify the accuracy, completeness, and currency of the ROPA and shall identify any gaps or non-compliance.
- (d) **Reporting:** The ROPA shall be reported to the Board and the Risk Committee annually. The report shall summarize the processing activities, the risk assessment, the compliance status, and any remediation actions.

## 19. GOVERNANCE AND COMPLIANCE

### 19.1 Data Protection Officer (DPO)
The Enterprise shall appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) who shall be responsible for overseeing the data privacy compliance program. The DPO shall:
- (a) Be independent and report directly to the highest management level (Board or CEO);
- (b) Have sufficient resources, authority, and expertise to perform the role;
- (c) Be involved in all data privacy matters, including policy development, DPIAs, breach management, DSR handling, and vendor management;
- (d) Be the primary contact point for the data protection authorities and the data subjects;
- (e) Monitor compliance with the data privacy laws and the Enterprise's policies;
- (f) Provide training and awareness to employees and franchisees;
- (g) Conduct internal audits and assessments;
- (h) Report to the Board and the Risk Committee on data privacy risks and compliance; and
- (i) Maintain the ROPA, DPIA register, breach register, and DSR register.

The DPO shall be appointed under the GDPR, UAE PDPL, Saudi PDPL, India DPDP Act, and Kenya DPA requirements. The DPO's contact details shall be published in the privacy notice and communicated to the data protection authorities.

### 19.2 Privacy Team
The Enterprise shall establish a privacy team (or assign privacy responsibilities to existing staff) to support the DPO. The privacy team shall include:
- Privacy analysts and specialists;
- Legal counsel (privacy-focused);
- IT security specialists;
- HR privacy coordinators;
- Franchisee privacy coordinators;
- Marketing privacy coordinators; and
- Customer service privacy coordinators.

### 19.3 Training and Awareness
The Enterprise shall provide data privacy training and awareness to:
- (a) **All Employees:** Annual general privacy training (e.g., principles, rights, consent, security, breach reporting).
- (b) **Privacy Team:** Quarterly specialized training (e.g., DPIAs, SCCs, breach management, DSR handling, vendor audits).
- (c) **Franchisees:** Privacy training as part of the initial and ongoing training programs (e.g., franchisee privacy obligations, customer data handling, consent management, breach notification).
- (d) **Senior Management and Board:** Annual privacy briefing on risks, compliance, and regulatory developments.
- (e) **Third-Party Processors:** Privacy training as part of the DPA and vendor onboarding (e.g., DPA obligations, security measures, breach notification, audit cooperation).

### 19.4 Internal Audits
The Enterprise shall conduct internal privacy audits at least annually, covering:
- (a) Compliance with the data privacy laws and the Enterprise's policies;
- (b) Accuracy and completeness of the ROPA, data inventory, and data flow diagrams;
- (c) Effectiveness of the DSR process;
- (d) Effectiveness of the breach notification process;
- (e) Vendor compliance with DPAs and security measures;
- (f) Franchisee compliance with privacy clauses and DPAs;
- (g) Security measures and incident response capabilities;
- (h) Training and awareness effectiveness; and
- (i) DPIA and PIA completion and quality.

### 19.5 External Audits and Certifications
The Enterprise shall pursue external privacy audits and certifications, including:
- (a) **ISO 27701 (Privacy Information Management System):** Certification of the privacy management system.
- (b) **SOC 2 Type II (Privacy Trust Service Criteria):** Audit of the privacy controls for cloud services and SaaS providers.
- (c) **GDPR Audit:** External audit of GDPR compliance (e.g., by a qualified auditor or law firm).
- (d) **PCI DSS (for payment data):** Compliance audit for payment card data security.
- (e) **Privacy Seal / Certification:** Participation in privacy certification programs (e.g., EU-US Data Privacy Framework, TRUSTe, BSI privacy certification) if applicable and beneficial.

### 19.6 Regulatory Engagement
The Enterprise shall engage proactively with the data protection authorities in all jurisdictions, including:
- (a) Registration and notification (if required by local law);
- (b) DPIA consultation (if required for high-risk processing);
- (c) Breach notification (as required);
- (d) Compliance inquiries and investigations (cooperating fully and transparently);
- (e) Participation in stakeholder consultations and regulatory guidance development; and
- (f) Seeking regulatory guidance on complex or novel privacy issues (e.g., AI, blockchain, biometric data).
