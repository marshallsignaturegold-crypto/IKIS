## 3. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

### 3.1 Initial Capital Contributions
The Shareholders shall make the following initial capital contributions to the JV Company:

| Shareholder | Cash Contribution | In-Kind Contribution | Total Contribution | % of Share Capital |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Corleone Holdings | $3,000,000 | $3,000,000 | $6,000,000 | 60% |
| Local Partners | $1,000,000 | $3,000,000 | $4,000,000 | 40% |
| **Total** | **$4,000,000** | **$6,000,000** | **$10,000,000** | **100%** |

### 3.2 Corleone Holdings' In-Kind Contributions
Corleone Holdings' in-kind contributions shall include:
- (a) Transfer of technical expertise, engineering designs, and construction management services for the Facility (valued at $1,500,000);
- (b) Transfer of proprietary halal processing technology, cold chain systems, and quality control protocols (valued at $1,000,000);
- (c) Assignment of the Concession Agreement (or the right to enter into the Concession Agreement) and related government relationships (valued at $500,000); and
- (d) Provision of training programs, equipment, and operational know-how (valued at $1,000,000).

The valuation of in-kind contributions shall be confirmed by an independent appraiser appointed by the Board within [90] days of the Effective Date. If the independent appraiser determines that the fair market value of the in-kind contributions differs from the values stated above, the Shareholders shall adjust the shareholdings or make additional cash contributions to reflect the appraised values.

### 3.3 Local Partners' In-Kind Contributions
The Local Partners' in-kind contributions shall include:
- (a) Contribution of land or land use rights within or near the Berbera Free Zone (valued at $1,500,000), subject to a title search and independent valuation;
- (b) Contribution of livestock (cattle, sheep, goats, camels) for initial operations and herd development (valued at $500,000);
- (c) Local knowledge, relationships, and facilitation of government permits, licenses, and community engagement (valued at $500,000); and
- (d) Government relationships and introductions to key ministries, the Berbera Free Zone Authority, and local authorities (valued at $500,000).

The valuation of the Local Partners' in-kind contributions shall be confirmed by the same independent appraiser. If the appraised value differs from the stated values, the Shareholders shall adjust the shareholdings or make additional cash contributions.

### 3.4 Additional Capital Contributions
If the JV Company requires additional capital beyond the initial contributions (e.g., for facility expansion, additional equipment, or working capital), the Board shall approve a capital call. Each Shareholder shall have the right to contribute its pro-rata share (60% Corleone Holdings / 40% Local Partners) of the additional capital. If a Shareholder declines to contribute its pro-rata share, the other Shareholder may contribute the shortfall, and the declining Shareholder's ownership percentage shall be diluted on a full ratchet basis (i.e., the declining Shareholder's shares shall be repriced to reflect the lower valuation implied by the additional capital).

### 3.5 Capital Contribution Schedule
The capital contributions shall be made in tranches in accordance with the development schedule attached as **Annex A** (Capital Contribution Schedule). The initial cash contributions shall be made within [30] days of the incorporation of the JV Company. In-kind contributions shall be transferred within [90] days of incorporation, subject to completion of title transfers, valuations, and government approvals.

### 3.6 Failure to Contribute
If a Shareholder fails to make a capital contribution when due, the other Shareholder may:
- (a) Make the delinquent contribution on behalf of the defaulting Shareholder, with the defaulting Shareholder's ownership diluted accordingly; or
- (b) Provide a loan to the JV Company (with interest at [8%] per annum) and secure the loan with a pledge of the defaulting Shareholder's shares; or
- (c) Terminate this Agreement if the default continues for more than [90] days and is not cured.

## 4. GOVERNANCE

### 4.1 Board Composition
The JV Company shall be managed by a Board of Directors (the **"Board"**) consisting of **[5] directors**, composed as follows:
- **[3] directors** appointed by Corleone Holdings (the **"Corleone Directors"**); and
- **[2] directors** appointed by the Local Partners (the **"Local Directors"**).

The Board shall have overall responsibility for the strategic direction, management, and oversight of the JV Company. The Board shall be the ultimate decision-making body of the JV Company, subject to the Reserved Matters in Schedule C.

### 4.2 Board Meetings
The Board shall meet at least **[quarterly]** (or more frequently as required). Board meetings may be held in person (in Hargeisa, Berbera, or another mutually agreed location) or by video conference. A quorum shall consist of at least [3] directors, including at least [1] Corleone Director and [1] Local Director. Board decisions shall be made by a simple majority of the directors present at a duly convened meeting, except for Reserved Matters, which require a supermajority of [4 out of 5] directors or the affirmative vote of both Shareholder-appointed director blocs.

### 4.3 Board Responsibilities
The Board shall be responsible for:
- (a) Approval of the annual Business Plan and budget;
- (b) Appointment, termination, and oversight of the CEO and senior management;
- (c) Approval of all Major Decisions (Reserved Matters);
- (d) Oversight of financial reporting, audit, and internal controls;
- (e) Risk management and compliance oversight;
- (f) Oversight of the Concession Agreement and government relations;
- (g) Approval of Related Party Transactions; and
- (h) Dividend policy and capital allocation decisions.

### 4.4 Board Charter
The Board shall operate in accordance with a written Board Charter, attached as **Annex B** (Board Charter), which shall set forth the rules of procedure, voting mechanisms, committee structures, director responsibilities, and conflict-of-interest policies. The Board Charter shall be consistent with the Corporate Governance Framework (`corporate_governance_framework.md`) and the Board Charter & Committee Structures (`board_charter_committee_structures.md`), as adapted for the JV Company.

### 4.5 CEO and Management
- **CEO:** The Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of the JV Company shall be appointed by Corleone Holdings, subject to the Board's approval. The CEO shall be responsible for the day-to-day management and operations of the JV Company, in accordance with the Business Plan and Board resolutions. The CEO shall report to the Board at each quarterly meeting and shall provide monthly operational and financial reports.
- **COO / Operations Manager:** The Chief Operating Officer (COO) or Operations Manager shall be appointed by the Board, with input from both Shareholders. The COO shall be responsible for facility operations, production, cold chain management, and quality control.
- **CFO / Finance Manager:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or Finance Manager shall be appointed by the Board, with input from both Shareholders. The CFO shall be responsible for financial management, accounting, budgeting, reporting, and tax compliance.
- **Local Manager / Government Liaison:** The Local Manager or Government Liaison shall be appointed by the Local Partners, subject to the Board's approval. The Local Manager shall be responsible for government relations, community engagement, local employment, and compliance with Somaliland regulations.

### 4.6 Financial Management
- (a) **Accounting Standards:** The JV Company shall maintain its books and records in accordance with **IFRS** (International Financial Reporting Standards) or **U.S. GAAP**, as determined by the Board.
- (b) **Bank Accounts:** The JV Company shall maintain bank accounts in reputable banks in Somaliland and the UAE (or other jurisdictions as approved by the Board). All bank accounts shall require dual signatories (one from Corleone Holdings and one from the Local Partners, or as otherwise approved by the Board).
- (c) **Budget and Reporting:** The JV Company shall prepare an annual Business Plan and budget, approved by the Board before the start of each fiscal year. Monthly financial reports shall be provided to the Board within [15] days of month-end. Quarterly management accounts and annual audited financial statements shall be prepared by an independent auditor approved by the Board.
- (d) **Audit:** The JV Company's annual financial statements shall be audited by an independent auditor (a Big Four firm or a reputable regional firm) appointed by the Board. The audit report shall be provided to both Shareholders within [90] days of the fiscal year end.
- (e) **Dividend Policy:** The JV Company shall distribute dividends in accordance with the profit distribution provisions in Section 7. The Board shall determine the timing and amount of dividends, subject to the JV Company's cash flow requirements, debt obligations, and reinvestment needs.

### 4.7 Shareholder Meetings
The JV Company shall hold an annual general meeting of shareholders (or equivalent) within [6] months of the fiscal year end, to approve the annual financial statements, the auditor's report, the Business Plan, and any other matters requiring shareholder approval. Special shareholder meetings may be called by either Shareholder upon [14] days' notice.

## 5. PROFIT DISTRIBUTION

### 5.1 Profit Distribution Ratio
The Net Profit of the JV Company shall be distributed to the Shareholders in proportion to their shareholdings:
- **Corleone Holdings:** [60%] of Net Profit;
- **Local Partners:** [40%] of Net Profit.

### 5.2 Dividend Policy
The Board shall adopt a dividend policy that balances the Shareholders' desire for returns with the JV Company's need for reinvestment and growth. The target dividend payout ratio shall be **[50%]** of Net Profit, subject to the Board's discretion based on:
- (a) The JV Company's cash flow and liquidity requirements;
- (b) Capital expenditure plans and debt service obligations;
- (c) The need to retain earnings for working capital, facility expansion, or mineral extraction development; and
- (d) The Concession Agreement's development milestones and production targets.

### 5.3 Dividend Timing
Dividends shall be declared and paid [annually / semi-annually / quarterly], as determined by the Board. Dividends shall be paid in [U.S. Dollars] (or another currency agreed by the Shareholders) within [30] days of the Board's declaration. The JV Company shall use commercially reasonable efforts to facilitate the repatriation of dividends to Corleone Holdings, subject to applicable foreign exchange controls and Somaliland regulations.

### 5.4 Retained Earnings
Retained earnings shall be used for:
- (a) Reinvestment in the Facility, cold chain, and mineral extraction infrastructure;
- (b) Debt repayment and interest obligations;
- (c) Working capital and contingency reserves;
- (d) Reserve for the Environmental Bond and rehabilitation obligations; and
- (e) Reserve for the Local Employment Target and training programs.

The Board shall approve the allocation of retained earnings as part of the annual Business Plan.

### 5.5 Tax Distributions
If the JV Company is a pass-through entity for tax purposes (e.g., a partnership or LLC), the Shareholders shall receive tax distributions sufficient to cover their respective tax liabilities on the JV Company's taxable income, at the estimated tax rate of each Shareholder. Tax distributions shall be made at least [annually] and shall be treated as advances against future dividends.

## 6. TRANSFER PRICING

### 6.1 Intercompany Transactions
The JV Company shall engage in intercompany transactions with Corleone Holdings, its affiliates, and other subsidiaries, including:
- (a) Sale of halal meat products to Corleone Products LLC for distribution to franchisees and third-party customers;
- (b) Sale of Critical Minerals (salt, gypsum) to Corleone Products LLC or other affiliates;
- (c) Purchase of equipment, technology, and management services from Corleone Holdings or its affiliates;
- (d) Licensing of intellectual property (trademarks, brand names, proprietary technology) from Corleone Holdings or Vito Corleone Media & Licensing LLC; and
- (e) Shared services (accounting, legal, IT, HR) provided by Corleone Holdings or its affiliates.

### 6.2 Arm's Length Pricing
All intercompany transactions shall be priced at **arm's length**, in accordance with the **OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines** and the **UN Practical Manual on Transfer Pricing for Developing Countries**. The Transfer Pricing Policy shall be documented in a written policy approved by the Board and reviewed annually by the independent auditor.

### 6.3 Transfer Pricing Documentation
The JV Company shall maintain contemporaneous transfer pricing documentation, including:
- (a) A description of the intercompany transactions and the business rationale;
- (b) A functional analysis (functions performed, risks assumed, assets employed by each party);
- (c) A comparability analysis (benchmarking against third-party transactions or industry data);
- (d) The selection and application of the most appropriate transfer pricing method (e.g., comparable uncontrolled price, resale price, cost plus, transactional net margin method); and
- (e) A conclusion on whether the pricing is consistent with the arm's length principle.

### 6.4 Cost Plus Method for Services
For intercompany services (management, technical, shared services), the cost plus method shall be applied. The service provider shall charge the JV Company the direct and indirect costs of providing the services, plus a **cost-plus markup of [5–10%]**, as approved by the Board and consistent with industry benchmarks.

### 6.5 Licensing Royalties
For the licensing of intellectual property (trademarks, brand names, proprietary technology), the JV Company shall pay a royalty to the licensor (Corleone Holdings or Vito Corleone Media & Licensing LLC) of **[2–5%]** of the net sales revenue of the licensed products, as approved by the Board and consistent with industry benchmarks for similar licensing arrangements.

### 6.6 Audit and Dispute Resolution
The JV Company's transfer pricing documentation shall be reviewed by the independent auditor as part of the annual audit. If the tax authorities of Somaliland or any other jurisdiction challenge the transfer pricing, the JV Company and the relevant Shareholder shall cooperate to defend the pricing and shall share the costs of any dispute resolution or adjustment.

## 7. EXIT PROVISIONS

### 7.1 IPO Exit
If Corleone Holdings consummates an IPO (as defined in the Shareholder Agreement Templates, `shareholder_agreement_templates.md`), the JV Company shall be consolidated into the IPO group or the JV Shares shall be exchanged for shares of Corleone Holdings (or a holding company) at a valuation determined by the IPO underwriters. The Local Partners shall have the right to:
- (a) Exchange their JV Shares for shares of Corleone Holdings at the IPO valuation, subject to lock-up and registration rights;
- (b) Retain their JV Shares in the JV Company (if the JV Company remains a subsidiary post-IPO); or
- (c) Sell their JV Shares to Corleone Holdings at a fair market value determined by an independent appraiser.

The terms of the IPO exit shall be negotiated in good faith between the Shareholders and shall be documented in a separate agreement or the IPO prospectus.

### 7.2 Buy-Sell (Shotgun) Provision
At any time after the [5th] anniversary of the Effective Date, either Shareholder may initiate a buy-sell process by delivering a written notice to the other Shareholder (the **"Shotgun Notice"**), offering to:
- (a) Purchase all of the other Shareholder's JV Shares at a price per share specified in the Shotgun Notice; or
- (b) Sell all of its own JV Shares to the other Shareholder at the same price per share.

The receiving Shareholder shall have [60] days to elect either to:
- (a) Purchase the initiating Shareholder's JV Shares at the specified price; or
- (b) Sell its own JV Shares to the initiating Shareholder at the specified price.

If the receiving Shareholder fails to elect within [60] days, the initiating Shareholder shall purchase the receiving Shareholder's JV Shares at the specified price. The purchase price shall be paid in cash within [90] days of the election (or the expiration of the election period). The purchase price may be paid in installments over [12] months, with interest at [8%] per annum, if the purchase price exceeds [$5,000,000].

### 7.3 Tag-Along Rights
If Corleone Holdings proposes to sell all or a majority of its JV Shares to a third party (other than in an IPO or to an affiliate), the Local Partners shall have the right to sell their pro-rata share of JV Shares (based on their ownership percentage) to the same third party on the same terms and conditions. Tag-along rights shall be exercised by written notice within [30] days of the Local Partners' receipt of Corleone Holdings' sale notice. If the third party is unwilling to purchase the Local Partners' shares, Corleone Holdings shall not complete the sale unless it purchases the Local Partners' shares at the same price and terms.

### 7.4 Drag-Along Rights
If Corleone Holdings receives a bona fide offer from a third party to purchase all or a majority of the JV Shares and the Board (including the Local Directors) approves the sale, Corleone Holdings may require the Local Partners to sell their JV Shares to the third party on the same terms and conditions, provided that:
- (a) The sale price per share is not less than the fair market value of the JV Shares, as determined by an independent appraiser;
- (b) The Local Partners receive the same consideration (cash, stock, or mixed) as Corleone Holdings;
- (c) The Local Partners are not required to provide representations, warranties, or indemnities beyond those provided by Corleone Holdings; and
- (d) The Local Partners are not liable for any breach of representations or warranties by Corleone Holdings or the third party.

Drag-along rights may be exercised by Corleone Holdings upon written notice to the Local Partners at least [60] days before the proposed closing.

### 7.5 Put/Call Options
- **Put Option (Local Partners):** At any time after the [7th] anniversary of the Effective Date, the Local Partners may deliver a written notice to Corleone Holdings exercising a put option to sell all (but not less than all) of their JV Shares to Corleone Holdings at a price equal to the fair market value of the JV Shares, as determined by an independent appraiser. The purchase price shall be paid in cash within [90] days of the appraisal, or in installments over [12] months with interest at [8%] if the purchase price exceeds [$5,000,000].
- **Call Option (Corleone Holdings):** At any time after the [7th] anniversary of the Effective Date, if the Local Partners have materially breached this Agreement (and failed to cure within [90] days), or if the Local Partners have failed to meet their capital contribution obligations, or if the Local Partners have been convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude, Corleone Holdings may deliver a written notice to the Local Partners exercising a call option to purchase all (but not less than all) of the Local Partners' JV Shares at a price equal to the fair market value of the JV Shares, as determined by an independent appraiser, discounted by [15%] if the call is exercised due to the Local Partners' material breach.

### 7.6 Fair Market Value Determination
The fair market value of the JV Shares for purposes of the buy-sell, put/call, and drag-along provisions shall be determined by an independent appraiser appointed by the Board. If the Shareholders cannot agree on an appraiser, each Shareholder shall appoint an appraiser, and the two appraisers shall appoint a third appraiser. The average of the three appraisals (or the median, if the two appraisers agree on a third appraiser) shall be the fair market value. The appraisal process shall be completed within [90] days of the appointment of the appraiser(s). The costs of the appraisal shall be shared equally by the Shareholders.

### 7.7 Exit in Connection with Concession Termination
If the Concession Agreement is terminated (for any reason), the Shareholders shall negotiate in good faith the winding-up or restructuring of the JV Company. If the Concession is terminated due to the Concessionaire's default, Corleone Holdings shall have the right to purchase the Local Partners' JV Shares at a price equal to the lesser of (a) the fair market value, or (b) the Local Partners' original capital contribution (plus any unpaid dividends). If the Concession is terminated due to the Grantor's default, the Local Partners shall have the right to purchase Corleone Holdings' JV Shares at fair market value, or the Shareholders shall share any compensation received from the Grantor in proportion to their shareholdings.

## 8. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

The JV Company shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and ordinances of Somaliland, the Berbera Free Zone, and the AISTA Corridor importing jurisdictions, including:
- (a) Somaliland Company Law (incorporation, governance, reporting, dissolution);
- (b) Berbera Free Zone regulations (licensing, land use, environmental compliance, employment);
- (c) Concession Agreement obligations (development milestones, production targets, halal certification, environmental rehabilitation);
- (d) Tax laws (corporate income tax, withholding tax, VAT/GST, if applicable);
- (e) Labor laws (minimum wage, working hours, health and safety, non-discrimination, freedom of association);
- (f) Environmental laws (EIA, environmental monitoring, waste management, rehabilitation);
- (g) Halal certification requirements (local and international);
- (h) Import/export regulations (customs, phytosanitary certificates, trade facilitation); and
- (i) Anti-corruption and anti-money laundering laws (FCPA, UK Bribery Act, local equivalents).

### 8.2 Tax Compliance
The JV Company shall engage a qualified tax advisor to ensure compliance with Somaliland tax laws and the tax incentive provisions of the Concession Agreement. The JV Company shall file all required tax returns and pay all taxes due. The Shareholders shall cooperate to optimize the tax efficiency of the JV Company's structure, including:
- (a) Utilizing the tax incentives provided under the Concession Agreement (corporate income tax exemption, import duty exemptions, export duty exemptions, withholding tax exemptions);
- (b) Structuring intercompany transactions in accordance with the Transfer Pricing Policy to minimize double taxation;
- (c) Obtaining tax residency certificates and treaty benefits (if applicable); and
- (d) Complying with FATCA, CRS, and other international tax information exchange requirements.

### 8.3 Labor Compliance
The JV Company shall comply with all applicable labor laws and regulations, including:
- Minimum wage and overtime requirements (if applicable);
- Safe working conditions and PPE;
- Non-discrimination and equal opportunity;
- Prohibition of child labor and forced labor;
- Freedom of association and collective bargaining (to the extent permitted by local law);
- Fair disciplinary and grievance procedures;
- The Local Employment Target (30% Somaliland nationals) and skills transfer programs; and
- Health and safety training and accident reporting.

### 8.4 Environmental Compliance
The JV Company shall comply with the environmental obligations set forth in the Concession Agreement and Section 15 of the Concession Agreement (`somaliland_concession_agreement_draft.md`), including:
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and approval;
- Environmental monitoring and reporting;
- Waste management and pollution control;
- Water usage and conservation;
- Environmental rehabilitation and the Environmental Bond; and
- Compliance with ISO 14001 or equivalent environmental management standards.

### 8.5 Anti-Corruption and AML Compliance
The JV Company shall comply with the anti-corruption and anti-money laundering provisions of the Corporate Governance Framework (`corporate_governance_framework.md`) and the AML/KYC Framework (`aml_kyc_framework.md`). The JV Company shall:
- (a) Prohibit bribery, kickbacks, and improper payments to government officials, customers, or third parties;
- (b) Maintain accurate books and records;
- (c) Conduct due diligence on all suppliers, distributors, agents, and intermediaries;
- (d) Implement a whistleblower mechanism;
- (e) Report suspicious transactions to the relevant authorities; and
- (f) Train all employees on anti-corruption and AML policies.

Each Shareholder shall maintain the confidentiality of all non-public information relating to the JV Company, the other Shareholder, the Concession Agreement, the Business Plan, financial data, customer lists, and proprietary technology (collectively, **"Confidential Information"**). Confidential Information does not include information that is publicly available (other than through the receiving Shareholder's breach), was independently developed, or was rightfully received from a third party.
