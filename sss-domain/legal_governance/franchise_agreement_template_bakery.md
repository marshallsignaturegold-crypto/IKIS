## 1. DEFINED TERMS

**"Gross Revenue"** means the total gross revenue from the operation of the Cafe, including dine-in, takeout, delivery, catering, merchandise, and CPG sales, before any deductions, excluding applicable sales taxes collected and remitted to taxing authorities.

## 3. GRANT OF FRANCHISE

### 3.1 Grant
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Franchisor hereby grants to the Franchisee the limited, non-transferable right and license to operate one (1) Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe at the location described in **Exhibit A** (the **"Premises"**), using the System and the Marks, within the Territory.

### 3.2 Territory
The Franchisee shall have the exclusive right to operate a Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe within the Territory during the Term, subject to the following reservations by the Franchisor:
- (a) The Franchisor reserves the right to operate, license, or franchise other Bakery & Cafe locations outside the Territory;
- (b) The Franchisor reserves the right to operate, license, or franchise any other concept (including Don Vito's Pizza Oven and Corleone Products retail) within or outside the Territory;
- (c) The Franchisor reserves the right to sell products and services through alternative channels (e.g., grocery, e-commerce, wholesale, catering, food trucks) within the Territory; and
- (d) The Franchisor reserves the right to relocate or establish cafes within the Territory in the event of a change in the competitive landscape, subject to [90] days' notice and the Franchisee's right of first refusal on the new location.

### 3.3 Term
The initial term of this Agreement shall be **ten (10) years** from the Effective Date (the **"Initial Term"**), subject to earlier termination as provided herein.

### 3.4 Renewal
The Franchisee shall have the right to renew this Agreement for **two (2) additional terms of five (5) years each**, provided that:
- (a) The Franchisee is not in default under this Agreement or any other agreement with the Franchisor or its affiliates;
- (b) The Franchisee has complied with all System standards and has achieved a customer satisfaction score of at least [85%] for the [24] months preceding the renewal request;
- (c) The Franchisee executes the Franchisor's then-current renewal agreement;
- (d) The Franchisee pays a renewal fee of **$8,000**;
- (e) The Franchisee completes any required refresher training; and
- (f) The Franchisee renovates the Cafe to conform to the then-current design and equipment standards, at the Franchisee's sole expense.

## 4. FEES

### 4.1 Initial Franchise Fee
The Franchisee shall pay the Franchisor a non-refundable Initial Franchise Fee of **$35,000** upon execution of this Agreement.

### 4.2 Royalty
The Franchisee shall pay the Franchisor a monthly Royalty equal to **4.5% of Gross Revenue** for the preceding month. The Royalty shall be due by the [15th] day of each month, accompanied by a monthly sales report in the format prescribed by the Franchisor.

### 4.3 Marketing Fund Contribution
The Franchisee shall pay a monthly Marketing Fund Contribution equal to **1.5% of Gross Revenue** for the preceding month, due concurrently with the Royalty. The Marketing Fund shall be administered by the Franchisor for System-wide marketing, advertising, and brand development.

### 4.4 Local Store Marketing
The Franchisee shall spend at least **1% of Gross Revenue** per month on local store marketing, subject to the Franchisor's brand guidelines and prior approval for campaigns exceeding [$5,000].

### 4.5 Technology Fees
The Franchisee shall pay all technology fees as set forth in **Exhibit F** (including POS system licensing, online ordering platform fees, and delivery aggregator integration fees).

### 4.6 Late Fees
Any fee not paid within [10] days of its due date shall bear interest at the lesser of [1.5%] per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, plus a late fee of [$250] per month.

### 5.1 Site Selection
The Franchisee shall select a site for the Cafe within the Territory, subject to the Franchisor's approval. The Franchisor shall approve or reject the proposed site within [30] days of receiving a complete site package (demographics, foot traffic, lease terms, site plan, and competitor analysis). The Franchisor's approval shall not be unreasonably withheld.

### 5.2 Buildout and Opening Timeline
- (a) The Franchisee shall execute a lease for the Premises within [90] days of the Effective Date;
- (b) The Franchisee shall commence construction within [30] days of lease execution and obtain all required permits;
- (c) The Franchisee shall complete buildout and open the Cafe within [180] days of lease execution (the **"Opening Deadline"**);
- (d) Failure to open by the Opening Deadline shall constitute a default, subject to cure periods and the Franchisor's right to terminate, unless the delay is due to force majeure or delays caused by the Franchisor.

### 5.3 Design and Construction Standards (Bakery/Cafe Specific)
The Franchisee shall construct, equip, and furnish the Cafe in strict accordance with the Franchisor's design and construction standards, as detailed in **Exhibit G**. Key specifications for the Bakery & Cafe include:
- **Smaller footprint:** Typically [800–1,500] square feet (compared to [2,000–2,500] for Pizza Oven), optimized for high foot traffic and quick service;
- **Display cases:** Front-counter pastry and baked goods display with temperature-controlled cases;
- **Coffee bar:** Espresso machine station, grinder, syrup station, and cold brew tap system;
- **Bakery kitchen:** Compact prep area with mixers, ovens, proofing cabinets, and cooling racks;
- **Seating:** Mix of cafe seating (banquettes, high-tops, communal tables) and grab-and-go counters;
- **CPG shelf:** Dedicated retail shelf for Corleone Products CPG items, positioned at the point of sale;

All materials, equipment, and signage must be purchased from Approved Suppliers or meet the Franchisor's specifications.

### 5.4 Equipment List (Bakery/Cafe Specific)
The Franchisee shall purchase and install all equipment required for the operation of a Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe, including:

## 6. OPERATIONS

### 6.1 System Standards
The Franchisee shall operate the Cafe in strict compliance with the System standards set forth in the Brand Standards Manual and all updates.

### 6.2 Menu and Recipes (Bakery/Cafe Specific)
The Franchisee shall offer the full menu prescribed by the Franchisor, which includes:
- **Coffee program:** Espresso-based drinks (cappuccino, latte, americano, macchiato), pour-over, cold brew, and seasonal specialty drinks;
- **Bakery items:** Croissants, muffins, danishes, scones, cookies, tarts, and seasonal pastries;
- **Cafe menu:** Sandwiches, salads, soups, and light breakfast/lunch items;
- **CPG retail:** Packaged Corleone Products items (cookies, crackers, spice blends, sauces) displayed on the CPG shelf; and
- **Seasonal and limited-time offerings:** As directed by the Franchisor.

All recipes, coffee blends, and pastry formulations are Confidential Information and trade secrets of the Franchisor. The Franchisee shall use only approved ingredients, coffee beans, and bakery mixes. The Franchisee may not add, delete, or modify any menu item without the Franchisor's prior written approval.

### 6.3 Coffee Program Standards
The Franchisee shall maintain the coffee program in accordance with the Franchisor's standards, including:
- Use of approved coffee bean blends and roasts (sourced from Approved Suppliers, including Corleone Products or designated coffee roasters);
- Daily calibration of espresso machines and grinders;
- Standardized drink recipes, portion sizes, and presentation (including latte art for cappuccinos and lattes);
- Regular cleaning and maintenance of coffee equipment;
- Barista Certification for all staff preparing espresso-based drinks; and
- Customer service standards for coffee customization, allergen information, and order accuracy.

### 6.4 Quality Control
The Franchisee shall maintain the highest standards of quality, cleanliness, sanitation, and customer service. The Franchisor shall have the right to inspect the Cafe (with or without notice) and to require the Franchisee to correct any deficiencies within [48] hours. Repeated failures to meet quality standards shall constitute a default.

### 6.5 Hours of Operation (Daypart Focus)
The Franchisee shall operate the Cafe during the minimum hours prescribed by the Franchisor, typically:
- **Morning focus:** Open by [6:00 AM or 7:00 AM] to capture the morning coffee and breakfast rush;
- **Afternoon service:** Continuous service through lunch and afternoon snack daypart;
- **Evening hours:** Close by [8:00 PM or 9:00 PM], subject to local market conditions and Franchisor approval;
- **Seven days per week:** Subject to local law and holiday variations.

The Franchisee shall notify the Franchisor of any temporary closures exceeding [24] hours.

### 6.6 Staffing and Training
The Franchisee shall employ a sufficient number of trained and competent staff, including certified baristas and pastry preparers. The Operating Principal and all managers must be certified by the Franchisor. The Franchisee shall ensure all employees comply with the System standards, dress code, and conduct policies.

### 6.7 Customer Service
The Franchisee shall maintain a customer complaint resolution process and shall report all customer complaints involving food safety, injury, or significant service failures to the Franchisor within [24] hours.

### 6.8 CPG Shelf Integration
The Franchisee shall maintain a dedicated CPG retail shelf within the Cafe, displaying and selling approved Corleone Products CPG items. The Franchisee shall:
- Stock the CPG shelf with at least [8] SKUs at all times;
- Maintain inventory rotation (FIFO) and check expiration dates weekly;
- Display CPG items in accordance with the Franchisor's planogram and merchandising guidelines;
- Report CPG sales separately in the monthly sales report; and
- Receive CPG products from Corleone Products or its designated distributor at the cross-subsidy pricing set forth in **Exhibit H**.

## 7. SUPPLY CHAIN AND CROSS-SUBSIDY

### 7.1 Approved Suppliers
The Franchisee shall purchase all products, ingredients, packaging, and equipment from Approved Suppliers, as listed in **Exhibit D**. The Franchisor may update the Approved Supplier list upon [30] days' notice.

### 7.2 Mandatory Purchasing from Corleone Products
The Franchisee shall purchase the following products from Corleone Products (or its designated distributors) at the cross-subsidy pricing set forth in **Exhibit H**:
- Bakery mixes (muffin, croissant, cookie, cake mixes);
- Proprietary coffee blends and flavor syrups;
- Branded packaging, cups, lids, and pastry boxes;
- CPG shelf products (packaged cookies, crackers, spice blends, sauces);
- Branded merchandise and promotional materials; and
- Coffee beans and roasted coffee (if sourced through Corleone Products' designated coffee program).

### 7.3 Quality Standards for Suppliers (Bakery/Cafe Specific)
All Approved Suppliers must meet the Franchisor's quality standards, including:
- **Coffee beans:** Single-origin or blended arabica beans, roasted to the Franchisor's specifications, with fair trade or ethical sourcing certifications where available;
- **Dairy:** Fresh, locally sourced dairy products (milk, cream, butter) meeting the Franchisor's quality standards;
- **Bakery ingredients:** Flour, sugar, yeast, chocolate, and fruit fillings meeting food safety certifications (HACCP, ISO 22000);
- **Halal certification:** Where required by local law or market conditions; and
- **Ethical sourcing:** Compliance with the Franchisor's supplier code of conduct.

### 7.4 Inventory Minimums
The Franchisee shall maintain minimum inventory levels of critical products (coffee beans, dairy, bakery mixes, pastries) to ensure uninterrupted service, as prescribed by the Franchisor's inventory guidelines. The Franchisee shall use a FIFO rotation system and shall not use expired or spoiled products.

### 7.5 Delivery and Logistics
The Franchisee shall use the Franchisor's designated logistics providers or Approved Suppliers for delivery of perishable goods, where available. The Franchisee shall maintain cold chain integrity for all temperature-sensitive products (dairy, pastries, cold brew).

## 8. TRAINING

### 8.1 Initial Training Program
Prior to the opening of the Cafe, the Franchisee and the Operating Principal shall complete the Franchisor's initial training program (the **"Initial Training"**), which includes:
- Cafe operations and management (32 hours);
- Food safety and sanitation (16 hours);
- **Barista Certification Program:** Espresso preparation, milk steaming, latte art, drink recipes, equipment maintenance (24 hours);
- **Pastry Training Program:** Croissant, muffin, cake, and tart preparation, baking techniques, decoration, and display (24 hours);
- Coffee program and bean sourcing (8 hours);
- POS system and technology (8 hours);
- Customer service and complaint resolution (8 hours);
- CPG shelf management and retail sales (4 hours);
- Local store marketing and grand opening (8 hours); and
- Financial management and reporting (8 hours).

The Initial Training shall be conducted at a designated training facility or corporate cafe. The Franchisee shall bear all travel, lodging, and meal expenses. The Initial Training fee of **$5,000** per person is included in the Initial Franchise Fee.

### 8.2 Franchisee Manager Certification
All general managers and assistant managers must successfully complete the Franchisor's manager certification program within [90] days of hire, including both the Barista Certification and Pastry Training modules. Certification includes a written examination and a practical assessment. Managers must be re-certified annually.

### 8.3 Ongoing Training
The Franchisee shall ensure that all staff attend ongoing training programs (in-person or online) as required by the Franchisor. The Franchisor shall provide at least [4] hours of online training per quarter per employee. The Franchisee shall maintain training records for all employees and shall submit them to the Franchisor upon request.

### 8.4 Refresher Training
The Franchisee and the Operating Principal shall attend refresher training at least once every [3] years. The Franchisor may require additional training if the Cafe fails an inspection or receives a customer satisfaction score below [75%].

## 12. TERMINATION

### 12.1 Termination by Franchisor for Default
The Franchisor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the Franchisee:
- (a) Fails to pay any fee when due and does not cure within [10] days;
- (b) Fails to open the Cafe by the Opening Deadline and does not cure within [30] days;
- (c) Materially breaches the System standards or Brand Standards Manual and does not cure within [30] days (or [48] hours for food safety violations);
- (d) Commits fraud, misrepresentation, or theft;
- (e) Is convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude;
- (f) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed;
- (g) Transfers this Agreement in violation of Section 11;
- (h) Repeatedly fails to submit required reports or breaches the POS reporting requirements;
- (i) Fails to maintain required insurance; or
- (j) Engages in conduct that materially damages the reputation of the System or the Marks.

### 12.2 Termination by Franchisor Without Cause
The Franchisor may terminate this Agreement without cause upon [180] days' written notice, subject to payment of the fair market value of the Cafe (as determined by an independent appraiser). This provision may be limited by applicable franchise relationship laws.

### 12.3 Termination by Franchisee
The Franchisee may terminate this Agreement without cause upon [180] days' written notice, subject to the payment of all accrued fees and the obligation to comply with the post-termination obligations in Section 12.5.

### 12.4 Post-Termination Obligations
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Franchisee shall:
- (a) Immediately cease using the Marks, System, and Confidential Information;
- (b) Return all manuals, materials, and proprietary information to the Franchisor;
- (c) Permanently remove all signage and markings identifying the Cafe as a Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe;
- (d) Pay all accrued fees and obligations to the Franchisor and its affiliates;
- (e) Transfer all customer data to the Franchisor or destroy it as directed;
- (f) Comply with the non-compete obligations in Section 12.5; and
- (g) Cooperate with the Franchisor's transition plan.

### 12.5 Non-Compete
For a period of **[24] months** following termination or expiration, the Franchisee shall not, directly or indirectly, own, operate, manage, or be employed by any business that competes with the System within the Territory or within a **[5]-mile** radius of any Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe. In jurisdictions where a [24]-month non-compete is unenforceable, the period shall be reduced to the maximum enforceable period.
