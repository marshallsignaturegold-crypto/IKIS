The initial term of this Agreement shall be **[three (3) years]** from the Effective Date (the **"Initial Term"**), subject to earlier termination as provided herein.

### 4.2 Renewal
This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **[one (1)-year]** terms (each a **"Renewal Term"**), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [90] days before the expiration of the then-current term. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon [180] days' written notice, subject to the post-termination obligations in Section 12.

### 4.3 Performance Milestones
The Distributor shall achieve the following minimum performance milestones during each contract year:
- **Year 1:** Minimum Net Sales of [$500,000] or minimum units of [50,000] across the Approved Products;
- **Year 2:** Minimum Net Sales of [$1,000,000] or minimum units of [100,000]; and
- **Year 3:** Minimum Net Sales of [$1,500,000] or minimum units of [150,000].

Failure to achieve the minimum performance milestone for any contract year shall constitute a breach, subject to a [90]-day cure period. If the Distributor fails to cure, the Supplier may terminate this Agreement or convert the appointment from exclusive to non-exclusive for the underperforming channels or geographies.

## 5. PRICING

### 5.1 Wholesale Pricing Schedule
The Supplier shall supply the Approved Products to the Distributor at the wholesale prices set forth in **Exhibit C** (Pricing Schedule). The Pricing Schedule sets forth the unit price, case price, and volume discount tiers for each SKU.

### 5.2 Volume Discounts
The Distributor shall be eligible for volume discounts based on the total value of purchase orders placed in each calendar quarter, as follows:
| Quarterly Purchase Value | Discount Off List Price |
|---|---|
| $0 – $49,999 | 0% |
| $50,000 – $99,999 | 5% |
| $100,000 – $249,999 | 7.5% |
| $250,000 – $499,999 | 10% |
| $500,000+ | 12.5% |

Volume discounts shall be applied as a credit against the Distributor's next purchase order or as a rebate within [30] days after the end of the quarter.

### 5.3 Price Increases
The Supplier may increase the wholesale prices of the Approved Products by providing at least [90] days' written notice before the effective date of the increase. Price increases shall not exceed [10%] in any single contract year, unless the increase is due to: (a) changes in raw material costs exceeding [15%]; (b) changes in currency exchange rates affecting the cost of imported ingredients; (c) changes in regulatory requirements (e.g., new labeling, halal certification fees); or (d) changes in tariffs or duties. In the event of a price increase exceeding [10%], the Distributor may terminate this Agreement upon [30] days' written notice.

### 5.4 Promotional Pricing
The Supplier may, from time to time, offer promotional pricing, bundled offers, or special discounts on the Approved Products. The Distributor shall pass through promotional pricing to its customers in accordance with the Supplier's promotional guidelines. The Supplier shall reimburse the Distributor for agreed-upon trade spend and promotional expenditures in accordance with Section 7.2.

### 5.5 Most Favored Customer
If the Supplier offers more favorable pricing, payment terms, or promotional support to any other distributor or customer in the Territory for the same or comparable products, the Supplier shall promptly offer the same terms to the Distributor, subject to the Distributor matching the volume and commitment levels of the favored customer.

### 5.6 Currency and Taxes
All prices are quoted in [U.S. Dollars / UAE Dirhams / Indian Rupees / Kenyan Shillings / Saudi Riyals], as designated in **Exhibit C**. Prices are exclusive of applicable taxes, duties, and shipping costs, unless otherwise stated. The Distributor shall be responsible for all import duties, VAT/GST, and local taxes on the Approved Products.

## 6. ORDERING AND DELIVERY

### 6.1 Purchase Orders
The Distributor shall submit purchase orders to the Supplier in writing (email or through the Supplier's ordering portal). Each purchase order shall specify the SKU, quantity, delivery date, and delivery location. The Supplier shall confirm or reject each purchase order within [5] business days. A purchase order shall be deemed accepted upon the Supplier's written confirmation or upon shipment of the ordered products.

### 6.2 Minimum Orders
The Distributor shall order at least the Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) per SKU per purchase order, as set forth in **Exhibit C**. The Supplier may waive the MOQ for initial launch orders or for new product introductions.

### 6.3 Lead Times
The Supplier shall use commercially reasonable efforts to deliver the Approved Products within the lead times set forth in **Exhibit C**, typically [14–21] days for in-stock items and [45–60] days for made-to-order or seasonal items. The Supplier shall promptly notify the Distributor of any anticipated delays.

### 6.4 Delivery Schedules and Freight Terms
Deliveries shall be made [EXW / FOB / CIF / DDP] (as designated in **Exhibit C**) to the Distributor's designated warehouse or distribution center. The Supplier shall arrange shipping at the Distributor's cost, unless otherwise agreed. The Distributor shall inspect all deliveries upon receipt and shall notify the Supplier of any shortages, damages, or non-conforming products within [48] hours of receipt.

### 6.5 Risk of Loss and Title
Risk of loss and title to the Approved Products shall pass to the Distributor upon [delivery to the Distributor's designated location / delivery to the carrier, depending on Incoterms].

### 6.6 Force Majeure — Supply Disruptions
If a Force Majeure Event prevents the Supplier from manufacturing or delivering the Approved Products, the Supplier shall promptly notify the Distributor and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact. The Supplier shall not be liable for delays caused by a Force Majeure Event, but the Distributor may terminate this Agreement if the Force Majeure Event continues for more than [90] days.

## 7. PAYMENT TERMS

### 7.1 Credit Terms
The Supplier shall extend credit terms to the Distributor as follows: [Net 30 / Net 45 / Net 60] days from the date of invoice, subject to the Distributor's creditworthiness and the Supplier's discretion. The Supplier may require advance payment, a letter of credit, or other security instruments for new Distributors or for orders exceeding the Distributor's credit limit.

### 7.2 Invoicing
The Supplier shall issue invoices upon shipment of the Approved Products. Invoices shall include the SKU, quantity, unit price, volume discount, taxes, shipping costs, and total amount due. The Distributor shall pay all invoices in full, without deduction or set-off, by the due date.

### 7.3 Late Payments
If the Distributor fails to pay any invoice by the due date, the Supplier may:
- (a) Charge interest on the overdue amount at the lesser of [1.5%] per month or the maximum rate permitted by law;
- (b) Suspend further shipments until all overdue amounts are paid;
- (c) Reduce or withdraw the Distributor's credit limit; and
- (d) Terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 12.2.

### 7.4 Set-Off
The Distributor shall not set off any amounts owed to the Supplier against any claims, counterclaims, or disputes, except with the Supplier's prior written consent.

## 8. MARKETING AND PROMOTION

### 8.1 Co-Op Marketing
The Supplier and Distributor shall collaborate on marketing and promotional activities for the Approved Products in the Territory. The Distributor shall allocate at least **[5%] of Net Sales** per quarter to local marketing and trade spend. The Supplier shall match the Distributor's co-op marketing expenditures up to **[2%] of Net Sales** per quarter, subject to the Distributor's submission of marketing plans and expense reports.

### 8.2 Trade Spend
Trade spend shall include:
- In-store displays, shelf-talkers, and end-cap promotions;
- Sampling events, demonstrations, and tasting booths;
- Retailer incentives, slotting fees, and listing fees (if approved by the Supplier);
- Digital advertising, social media campaigns, and influencer marketing;
- Seasonal and holiday promotions; and
- Trade show participation and industry events.

All trade spend activities exceeding [$5,000] per activity shall require the Supplier's prior written approval. The Distributor shall submit a quarterly trade spend plan to the Supplier for approval at least [30] days before the start of each quarter.

### 8.3 Promotional Materials
The Supplier shall provide the Distributor with promotional materials, including product images, videos, recipe cards, social media content, and point-of-sale displays. The Distributor shall use the promotional materials only in accordance with the Supplier's brand guidelines and shall not modify or alter the materials without the Supplier's prior written consent. All promotional materials are the Intellectual Property of the Supplier.

### 8.4 Display Requirements
The Distributor shall ensure that the Approved Products are displayed in retail outlets in accordance with the Supplier's display guidelines, including:
- Minimum shelf facings and shelf placement (eye-level, category adjacency);
- Use of approved shelf-talkers, wobblers, and promotional signage;
- Clean, well-stocked, and organized shelves;
- Rotation of products (FIFO) to ensure freshness; and
- Compliance with retailer category management and planogram requirements.

### 8.5 Marketing Fund
If the Distributor and Supplier agree to establish a dedicated Marketing Fund for a specific Territory or channel, the terms of the Marketing Fund (contributions, expenditures, administration, and reporting) shall be set forth in a separate addendum to this Agreement.

## 9. QUALITY CONTROL

### 9.1 Product Standards
The Supplier warrants that all Approved Products shall:
- (a) Conform to the product specifications in **Exhibit A**;
- (b) Be manufactured in accordance with HACCP, ISO 22000, and Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP);
- (c) Be free from defects in materials and workmanship;
- (d) Comply with all applicable food safety, labeling, and halal certification requirements in the Territory; and
- (e) Be fit for human consumption and merchantable.

### 9.2 Inspection and Rejection
The Distributor shall inspect all deliveries upon receipt and shall notify the Supplier of any non-conforming products (defective, damaged, short-dated, incorrect SKU, or non-compliant labeling) within [48] hours of receipt. The Supplier shall, at its option, replace the non-conforming products, issue a credit, or refund the purchase price, including shipping costs, within [14] days of notification. The Distributor shall hold the non-conforming products for the Supplier's inspection or disposal instructions.

### 9.3 Returns
The Distributor may return products that are:
- (a) Non-conforming or defective (as determined by the Supplier or a mutually agreed third-party inspector);
- (b) Expired or within [30] days of expiration at the time of delivery; or
- (c) Discontinued products that the Distributor cannot sell within [180] days of discontinuation notice.

Returns shall be made in accordance with the Supplier's return policy, as updated from time to time. The Supplier shall bear the cost of return shipping for defective or non-conforming products. The Distributor shall bear the cost of return shipping for expired products unless the expiration was due to the Supplier's error.

### 9.4 Product Recalls
In the event of a product recall (voluntary or mandatory), the Supplier shall promptly notify the Distributor and shall provide recall instructions, replacement products, and reimbursement for all costs associated with the recall (including retrieval, disposal, and customer notification). The Distributor shall cooperate fully with the recall and shall maintain records of all recalled products and their disposition.

### 9.5 Halal Certification and Regulatory Compliance
All Approved Products sold in jurisdictions requiring halal certification (e.g., Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Malaysia, Indonesia) shall bear the halal certification mark of a recognized certifying body (e.g., UAE ESMA, Saudi SFDA, or an equivalent). The Supplier shall maintain valid halal certifications and shall provide copies to the Distributor upon request. The Distributor shall ensure that all labeling, packaging, and marketing materials comply with local regulatory requirements in the Territory.

## 10. INVENTORY

### 10.1 Minimum Stock
The Distributor shall maintain minimum stock levels of the Approved Products as set forth in **Exhibit D** (Inventory Guidelines), to ensure continuous availability and avoid stockouts. Minimum stock levels shall be reviewed quarterly and adjusted based on sales velocity, seasonality, and lead times.

### 10.2 Rotation (FIFO)
The Distributor shall implement a first-in-first-out (FIFO) inventory rotation system to ensure that the oldest products are sold first. The Distributor shall not sell products that are expired or within [30] days of expiration without the Supplier's prior written consent.

### 10.3 Expiry Management
The Distributor shall monitor the expiration dates of all Approved Products and shall report products approaching expiration (within [60] days) to the Supplier. The Supplier may, at its discretion, offer credit, replacement, or promotional support for slow-moving or near-expiry products. The Distributor shall dispose of expired products in accordance with local food safety and waste disposal regulations and shall provide the Supplier with a certificate of destruction if requested.

### 10.4 Inventory Reporting
The Distributor shall provide the Supplier with a monthly inventory report, including SKU-level stock levels, units sold, units received, and expired or damaged products. The Supplier shall have the right to audit the Distributor's inventory (with [48] hours' notice) no more than [2] times per contract year.

## 11. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 11.1 Trademark License
The Supplier hereby grants the Distributor a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Intellectual Property (trademarks, logos, and brand names) solely in connection with the distribution, marketing, and sale of the Approved Products in the Territory during the Term. The Distributor shall not use the Intellectual Property for any other purpose or in any manner that disparages the Supplier or the Approved Products.

### 11.2 Brand Guidelines
The Distributor shall use the Intellectual Property in strict accordance with the Supplier's brand guidelines, as set forth in the Trademark & IP Protection Strategy (`trademark_ip_protection_strategy.md`) and as provided to the Distributor in writing. The Distributor shall submit all proposed uses of the Intellectual Property (including local marketing materials, packaging modifications, and co-branded promotions) to the Supplier for prior written approval.

### 11.3 Ownership
The Distributor acknowledges that the Supplier is the sole owner of all right, title, and interest in and to the Intellectual Property and the Approved Products. The Distributor shall not apply for or register any trademark, domain name, or business name that is identical or confusingly similar to the Intellectual Property. The Distributor shall not contest the validity or ownership of the Intellectual Property.

### 11.4 Infringement
The Distributor shall promptly notify the Supplier of any suspected infringement of the Intellectual Property in the Territory. The Supplier shall have the sole right to enforce the Intellectual Property, and the Distributor shall cooperate in any enforcement action, including providing evidence, testimony, and assistance in customs recordation.

### 11.5 Customs Recordation
The Distributor shall assist the Supplier in recordation of the Intellectual Property with customs authorities in the Territory (if applicable) to prevent the importation of counterfeit or infringing products. The Distributor shall report any suspected counterfeit products to the Supplier and to the relevant customs authorities.

## 12. TERMINATION

### 12.1 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party:
- (a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [30] days (or [10] days for payment breaches);
- (b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed;
- (c) Commits fraud, misrepresentation, or theft;
- (d) Is convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude; or
- (e) Engages in conduct that materially damages the reputation of the Supplier or the Approved Products.

### 12.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon [180] days' written notice. Upon termination for convenience, the Supplier shall have the right to repurchase the Distributor's remaining inventory of the Approved Products at the Distributor's original purchase price (less any credits or discounts), provided the products are in saleable condition and within their shelf life.

### 12.3 Termination for Failure to Meet Performance Milestones
If the Distributor fails to meet the minimum performance milestones for two (2) consecutive contract years, the Supplier may terminate this Agreement upon [90] days' written notice or convert the appointment from exclusive to non-exclusive.

### 12.4 Post-Termination Obligations
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement (for any reason), the Distributor shall:
- (a) Immediately cease using the Intellectual Property and return all promotional materials, manuals, and proprietary information to the Supplier;
- (b) Permanently remove all signage, displays, and references to the Supplier and the Approved Products;
- (c) Pay all outstanding invoices and obligations to the Supplier;
- (d) Transfer all customer data (in accordance with the Data Privacy Framework) to the Supplier or destroy it as directed;
- (e) Cooperate with the Supplier's transition plan, including introductions to key retailers and customers; and
- (f) Comply with the non-compete obligations in Section 12.5.

### 12.5 Non-Compete
For a period of **[12] months** following termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Distributor shall not, directly or indirectly, distribute, market, or sell any products that are directly competitive with the Approved Products in the Territory. This non-compete shall be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law. In exchange for the non-compete, the Supplier shall pay the Distributor a non-compete fee of **[10%] of the average annual Net Sales** during the [2] years preceding termination, payable in quarterly installments.

## 14. FORCE MAJEURE

### 14.1 Definition
A **"Force Majeure Event"** means any event beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including: natural disasters (earthquake, flood, hurricane, pandemic); war, terrorism, civil unrest, or armed conflict; government action, trade embargoes, sanctions, or export/import restrictions; port closures, border closures, or transportation disruptions; failure of critical infrastructure (power, telecommunications, water); and labor strikes or lockouts affecting the Supplier's manufacturing facilities or the Distributor's logistics providers.

### 14.2 Effect of Force Majeure
Neither Party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) due to a Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall:
- (a) Promptly notify the other Party of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration;
- (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event; and
- (c) Resume performance as soon as practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

### 14.3 Extended Force Majeure
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [90] days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon [30] days' written notice. Upon termination due to Force Majeure, the Supplier shall have the right to repurchase the Distributor's remaining inventory at the original purchase price, and the Distributor shall have no further obligations under this Agreement.
