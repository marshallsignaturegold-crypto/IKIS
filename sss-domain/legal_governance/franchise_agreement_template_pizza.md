## 3. GRANT OF FRANCHISE

### 3.1 Grant
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Franchisor hereby grants to the Franchisee the limited, non-transferable right and license to operate one (1) Don Vito's Pizza Oven Restaurant at the location described in **Exhibit A** (the **"Premises"**), using the System and the Marks, within the Territory.

### 3.2 Territory
The Franchisee shall have the exclusive right to operate a Don Vito's Pizza Oven Restaurant within the Territory during the Term, subject to the following reservations by the Franchisor:
- (a) The Franchisor reserves the right to operate, license, or franchise other Don Vito's Pizza Oven Restaurants outside the Territory;
- (b) The Franchisor reserves the right to operate, license, or franchise any other concept (including Vito Corleone Bakery & Cafe and Corleone Products retail) within or outside the Territory;
- (c) The Franchisor reserves the right to sell products and services through alternative channels (e.g., grocery, e-commerce, catering, food trucks) within the Territory; and
- (d) The Franchisor reserves the right to relocate or establish restaurants within the Territory in the event of a change in the competitive landscape (e.g., a competitor opening within [1] mile), subject to [90] days' notice and the Franchisee's right of first refusal on the new location.

### 3.3 Term
The initial term of this Agreement shall be **ten (10) years** from the Effective Date (the **"Initial Term"**), subject to earlier termination as provided herein.

### 3.4 Renewal
The Franchisee shall have the right to renew this Agreement for **two (2) additional terms of five (5) years each** (each a **"Renewal Term"**), provided that:
- (a) The Franchisee is not in default under this Agreement or any other agreement with the Franchisor or its affiliates;
- (b) The Franchisee has complied with all System standards and has achieved a customer satisfaction score of at least [85%] for the [24] months preceding the renewal request;
- (c) The Franchisee executes the Franchisor's then-current renewal agreement, which may include updated fees, standards, and obligations;
- (d) The Franchisee pays a renewal fee of **$10,000**;
- (e) The Franchisee completes any required refresher training; and
- (f) The Franchisee renovates the Restaurant to conform to the then-current design and equipment standards, at the Franchisee's sole expense.

## 4. FEES

### 4.1 Initial Franchise Fee
The Franchisee shall pay the Franchisor a non-refundable Initial Franchise Fee of **$45,000** upon execution of this Agreement. This fee compensates the Franchisor for the costs of franchise development, site evaluation, and initial training coordination.

### 4.2 Royalty
The Franchisee shall pay the Franchisor a monthly Royalty equal to **5% of Gross Revenue** for the preceding month. The Royalty shall be due by the [15th] day of each month, accompanied by a monthly sales report in the format prescribed by the Franchisor.

### 4.3 Marketing Fund Contribution
The Franchisee shall pay a monthly Marketing Fund Contribution equal to **2% of Gross Revenue** for the preceding month, due concurrently with the Royalty. The Marketing Fund shall be administered by the Franchisor for System-wide marketing, advertising, and brand development. The Franchisor shall provide an annual accounting of Marketing Fund expenditures to all franchisees.

### 4.4 Local Store Marketing
In addition to the Marketing Fund Contribution, the Franchisee shall spend at least **1% of Gross Revenue** per month on local store marketing (e.g., community events, local digital advertising, grand opening promotions), subject to the Franchisor's brand guidelines and prior approval for campaigns exceeding [$5,000].

### 4.5 Technology Fees
The Franchisee shall pay all technology fees as set forth in **Exhibit F** (including POS system licensing, online ordering platform fees, and delivery aggregator integration fees), as updated from time to time.

### 4.6 Late Fees
Any fee not paid within [10] days of its due date shall bear interest at the lesser of [1.5%] per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, plus a late fee of [$250] per month.

### 5.1 Site Selection

The Franchisor shall approve or reject the proposed site within [30] days of receiving a complete site package (demographics, traffic counts, lease terms, site plan, and competitor analysis). ### 5.2 Buildout and Opening Timeline

### 5.3 Design and Construction Standards
The Franchisee shall construct, equip, and furnish the Restaurant in strict accordance with the Franchisor's design and construction standards, including the Brand Standards Manual and **Exhibit G** (buildout specifications). ### 5.4 Equipment List
The Franchisee shall purchase and install all equipment required for the operation of a Don Vivo's Pizza Oven Restaurant, including: deck ovens, dough mixers, prep tables, refrigeration, POS systems, display cases, and furniture, as detailed in **Exhibit G**.

## 6. OPERATIONS

### 6.1 System Standards
The Franchisee shall operate the Restaurant in strict compliance with the System standards set forth in the Brand Standards Manual and all updates. The Franchisee shall not deviate from the System standards without the Franchisor's prior written consent.

### 6.2 Menu and Recipes
The Franchisee shall offer the full menu prescribed by the Franchisor and shall use only the approved recipes, ingredients, and preparation methods. The Franchisee may not add, delete, or modify any menu item without the Franchisor's prior written approval. All recipes are Confidential Information and trade secrets of the Franchisor.

### 6.3 Quality Control
The Franchisee shall maintain the highest standards of quality, cleanliness, sanitation, and customer service. The Franchisor shall have the right to inspect the Restaurant (with or without notice) and to require the Franchisee to correct any deficiencies within [48] hours. Repeated failures to meet quality standards shall constitute a default.

### 6.4 Hours of Operation
The Franchisee shall operate the Restaurant during the minimum hours prescribed by the Franchisor (typically [11:00 AM to 11:00 PM, 7 days per week]), subject to local law and holiday variations. The Franchisee shall notify the Franchisor of any temporary closures exceeding [24] hours.

### 6.5 Staffing and Training
The Franchisee shall employ a sufficient number of trained and competent staff to operate the Restaurant. The Operating Principal and all managers must be certified by the Franchisor. The Franchisee shall ensure all employees comply with the System standards, dress code, and conduct policies.

### 6.6 Customer Service
The Franchisee shall maintain a customer complaint resolution process and shall report all customer complaints involving food safety, injury, or significant service failures to the Franchisor within [24] hours.

## 7. SUPPLY CHAIN AND CROSS-SUBSIDY

### 7.1 Approved Suppliers
The Franchisee shall purchase all products, ingredients, packaging, and equipment from Approved Suppliers, as listed in **Exhibit D**. The Franchisor may update the Approved Supplier list upon [30] days' notice. The Franchisee may request approval of a new supplier, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if the supplier meets the System standards.

### 7.2 Mandatory Purchasing from Corleone Products
The Franchisee shall purchase the following products from Corleone Products (or its designated distributors) at the cross-subsidy pricing set forth in **Exhibit H**:
- Pizza dough mix and flour blends;
- Proprietary sauces and seasoning packets;
- Frozen pizza toppings and prepared ingredients;
- Branded packaging, boxes, and bags;
- Branded merchandise and promotional materials.

The Franchisee acknowledges that the cross-subsidy pricing supports the overall quality, consistency, and supply chain integrity of the System. The Franchisee shall maintain minimum inventory levels as prescribed by the Franchisor.

All Approved Suppliers must meet the Franchisor's quality standards, including halal certification (where required by local law or market conditions), food safety certifications (e.g., HACCP, ISO 22000), and ethical sourcing requirements. The Franchisor may audit suppliers at any time.

### 7.4 Inventory Minimums
The Franchisee shall maintain minimum inventory levels of critical products (dough, sauce, cheese) to ensure uninterrupted service, as prescribed by the Franchisor's inventory guidelines. The Franchisee shall use a first-in-first-out (FIFO) rotation system and shall not use expired or spoiled products.

### 7.5 Delivery and Logistics
The Franchisee shall use the Franchisor's designated logistics providers or Approved Suppliers for delivery of perishable goods, where available. The Franchisee shall maintain cold chain integrity for all temperature-sensitive products.

## 8. TRAINING

### 8.1 Initial Training Program
Prior to the opening of the Restaurant, the Franchisee and the Operating Principal shall complete the Franchisor's initial training program (the **"Initial Training"**), which includes:
- Restaurant operations and management (40 hours);
- Food safety and sanitation (16 hours);
- Pizza preparation and oven management (24 hours);
- POS system and technology (8 hours);
- Customer service and complaint resolution (8 hours);
- Local store marketing and grand opening (8 hours); and
- Financial management and reporting (8 hours).

The Initial Training shall be conducted at a designated training facility or corporate restaurant. The Franchisee shall bear all travel, lodging, and meal expenses for the Initial Training. The Initial Training fee of **$5,000** per person is included in the Initial Franchise Fee.

### 8.2 Franchisee Manager Certification
All general managers and assistant managers must successfully complete the Franchisor's manager certification program within [90] days of hire. Certification includes a written examination and a practical assessment. Managers must be re-certified annually.

### 8.3 Ongoing Training
The Franchisee shall ensure that all staff attend ongoing training programs (in-person or online) as required by the Franchisor. The Franchisor shall provide at least [4] hours of online training per quarter per employee. The Franchisee shall maintain training records for all employees and shall submit them to the Franchisor upon request.

### 8.4 Refresher Training
The Franchisee and the Operating Principal shall attend refresher training at least once every [3] years. The Franchisor may require additional training if the Restaurant fails an inspection or receives a customer satisfaction score below [75%].

The Franchisee shall install and maintain the Franchisor's approved POS system (currently [BRAND/MODEL], as updated) at the Restaurant. The POS system shall be used for all sales transactions, inventory tracking, and labor management. The Franchisee shall submit daily sales reports, weekly inventory reports, and monthly financial statements to the Franchisor through the approved reporting platform. ### 10.3 Online Ordering
The Franchisee shall participate in the Franchisor's online ordering platform (or an approved third-party platform) and shall ensure that all online orders are fulfilled in accordance with the System standards. The Franchisee shall pay all platform fees as set forth in **Exhibit F**.

The Franchisee shall implement reasonable data security measures (encryption, access controls, password policies) to protect customer data, payment card data, and System data. The Franchisee shall install all required software updates, patches, and hardware upgrades within [30] days of the Franchisor's notice. ## 11. TRANSFER AND ASSIGNMENT

### 11.1 Restrictions on Transfer
The Franchisee shall not Transfer this Agreement, the Restaurant, or any interest therein without the Franchisor's prior written consent. This restriction applies to direct and indirect transfers (e.g., changes in ownership of the Franchisee entity, issuance of new equity, pledge of assets).

### 11.2 Right of First Refusal
If the Franchisee receives a bona fide offer from a third party to purchase the Restaurant or this Agreement, the Franchisee shall provide the Franchisor with written notice of the offer, including the purchase price, terms, and the identity of the proposed transferee. The Franchisor shall have [30] days to exercise its right to purchase the Restaurant on the same terms and conditions. If the Franchisor declines, the Franchisee may complete the Transfer to the third party within [60] days, on terms no more favorable than those offered to the Franchisor.

### 11.3 Franchisee Qualifications
Any proposed transferee must meet the Franchisor's then-current qualifications for new franchisees, including:
- Net worth of at least [$500,000] and liquid assets of at least [$150,000];
- Restaurant or retail management experience (preferred);
- Successful completion of the Franchisor's interview and background check process; and
- Execution of the Franchisor's then-current franchise agreement and training requirements.

### 11.4 Transfer Fee
The Franchisee shall pay a transfer fee of **$10,000** to the Franchisor upon any approved Transfer. The Franchisee shall also reimburse the Franchisor for all legal and administrative costs associated with the Transfer.

### 11.5 Death or Disability of Franchisee
If the Franchisee is an individual and dies or becomes permanently disabled, the Franchisee's estate or legal representative shall have [90] days to nominate a qualified successor (e.g., a family member or approved manager). If a qualified successor is not approved within [180] days, the Franchisor may terminate this Agreement.

## 12. TERMINATION

### 12.1 Termination by Franchisor for Default
The Franchisor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the Franchisee:
- (a) Fails to pay any fee when due and does not cure within [10] days;
- (b) Fails to open the Restaurant by the Opening Deadline and does not cure within [30] days;
- (c) Materially breaches the System standards or Brand Standards Manual and does not cure within [30] days (or [48] hours for food safety violations);
- (d) Commits fraud, misrepresentation, or theft;
- (e) Is convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude;
- (f) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed;
- (g) Transfers this Agreement in violation of Section 11;
- (h) Repeatedly fails to submit required reports or breaches the POS reporting requirements;
- (i) Fails to maintain required insurance; or
- (j) Engages in conduct that materially damages the reputation of the System or the Marks.

### 12.2 Termination by Franchisor Without Cause
The Franchisor may terminate this Agreement without cause upon [180] days' written notice, provided that the Franchisor pays the Franchisee the fair market value of the Restaurant (excluding the value of the Franchisee's unamortized leasehold improvements and personal property), as determined by an independent appraiser. This provision may be limited or prohibited by applicable franchise relationship laws.

### 12.3 Termination by Franchisee
The Franchisee may terminate this Agreement without cause upon [180] days' written notice, subject to the payment of all accrued fees and the obligation to comply with the post-termination obligations in Section 12.5. The Franchisee may terminate for cause if the Franchisor materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [60] days.

### 12.4 Post-Termination Obligations
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement (for any reason), the Franchisee shall:
- (a) Immediately cease using the Marks, System, and Confidential Information;
- (b) Return all manuals, materials, and proprietary information to the Franchisor;
- (c) Permanently remove all signage and markings identifying the Restaurant as a Don Vito's Pizza Oven;
- (d) Pay all accrued fees and obligations to the Franchisor and its affiliates;
- (e) Transfer all customer data (in accordance with the Data Privacy Framework) to the Franchisor or destroy it as directed;
- (f) Comply with the non-compete obligations in Section 12.5; and
- (g) Cooperate with the Franchisor's transition plan, including training any replacement operator.

### 12.5 Non-Compete
For a period of **[24] months** following termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Franchisee shall not, directly or indirectly, own, operate, manage, or be employed by any business that competes with the System within the Territory or within a **[5]-mile** radius of any Don Vito's Pizza Oven Restaurant. This non-compete shall be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law. In jurisdictions where a [24]-month non-compete is unenforceable, the period shall be reduced to the maximum enforceable period (e.g., [12] months or [6] months).
