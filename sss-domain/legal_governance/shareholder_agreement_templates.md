## 1. DEFINED TERMS

**"AISTA"** means the AISTA Corridor under the Sovereign SafeTrade Program (SSTP).

**"Board"** means the Board of Directors of Corleone Enterprise Holdings, a Delaware corporation.

**"Common Stock"** means the common stock, par value [$0.0001] per share, of the Company.

**"Company"** means Corleone Enterprise Holdings, a Delaware corporation.

**"Conversion Price"** means the original issue price per share of a series of Preferred Stock, as adjusted for stock splits, dividends, recapitalizations, and anti-dilution provisions.

**"DGCL"** means the Delaware General Corporation Law.

**"ESOP"** means the Employee Stock Ownership Plan, representing 15% of the equity of each franchise subsidiary.

**"FIV"** means the Franchisee Investment Vehicle, a Delaware LLC holding 5% of the Company via Series A/B participation.

**"Founders"** means the founding shareholders of the Company designated on Schedule A.

**"IPO"** means the Company's first underwritten public offering of Common Stock pursuant to an effective registration statement under the U.S. Securities Act of 1933, with gross proceeds to the Company of at least [$500,000,000].

**"Liquidation Preference"** means the preferential distribution right of Preferred Stockholders upon a Liquidation Event.

**"Liquidation Event"** means (a) a liquidation, dissolution, or winding up of the Company; (b) a Deemed Liquidation Event (merger, consolidation, sale of substantially all assets, or change of control); or (c) a redemption of the Preferred Stock as provided herein.

**"Major Investor"** means any investor holding at least [10%] of the outstanding Preferred Stock (on an as-converted basis) or any investor holding at least [$1,000,000] in original principal amount of Preferred Stock.

**"Original Issue Price"** means the per-share price paid by investors in a given financing round.

**"Preferred Stock"** means the respective series of preferred stock issued in Series A, B, or C.

**"Seed Round"** means the $4,000,000 financing completed at Year 0.

### 3.3 Pre-emptive Rights
Each Major Investor shall have a right to purchase its pro-rata share (based on fully diluted ownership) of any new issuance of equity securities by the Company (excluding excluded securities: ESOP grants, issuances in connection with acquisitions, and issuances under the FIV Operating Agreement). If any Major Investor declines to purchase its full pro-rata share, the remaining Major Investors may purchase their proportion of the declined shares.

### 3.4 Anti-Dilution (Full Ratchet)
If the Company issues equity securities at a price per share less than the then-applicable Conversion Price for Series A Preferred, the Conversion Price shall be reduced to the price at which such new securities are issued. This is a **full ratchet** adjustment, providing maximum downside protection to Series A Investors. Weighted average anti-dilution is an alternative bracketed option for negotiation.

### 3.5 Board Seat
The Series A Investors shall have the right to designate [1] director to the Board (the **"Investor Director"**), provided the Investor Director meets the independence standards and qualifications set forth in the Board Charter. The Investor Director shall have the right to serve on the Audit Committee and Risk Committee, subject to independence and qualification requirements.

### 3.6 Information Rights
The Company shall deliver to each Major Investor:
- (a) annual audited financial statements within [90] days of fiscal year end;
- (b) quarterly unaudited financial statements within [45] days of quarter end;
- (c) annual operating budget and business plan at least [30] days before the start of each fiscal year;
- (d) prompt notice of any material litigation, regulatory action, or government investigation;
- (e) inspection rights (upon reasonable notice) to examine the Company's books and records; and
- (f) access to the Company's management for quarterly investor calls.

### 3.7 Drag-Along Rights
If the holders of at least [75%] of the outstanding Common Stock (on an as-converted basis) and at least [66.67%] of the outstanding Preferred Stock (voting as a separate class) approve a sale of the Company, all shareholders shall be required to participate in such sale on the same terms and conditions, including customary representations, warranties, and indemnities, provided that (a) the liquidation preference of Preferred Stock is satisfied, and (b) any indemnity obligations are limited to [10%] of the purchase price and survive for no more than [18] months.

### 3.8 Tag-Along Rights
If the Founders (or any holder of more than [5%] of Common Stock) propose to sell shares to a third party, each Major Investor shall have the right to sell a pro-rata portion of its holdings (based on fully diluted ownership) on the same terms and conditions. Tag-along rights do not apply to transfers to permitted transferees (family trusts, estate planning vehicles) or to sales in a registered public offering.

### 3.9 Liquidation Preference (1x Non-Participating)
In the event of a Liquidation Event, Series A Preferred Stockholders shall receive an amount equal to the greater of (a) [1x] the Original Issue Price per share (plus accrued and unpaid dividends), or (b) the amount they would receive if all Preferred Stock were converted to Common Stock immediately prior to the Liquidation Event. After payment of the Liquidation Preference, any remaining proceeds shall be distributed to Common Stockholders. This is **non-participating** preferred stock.

### 3.10 Redemption Rights
At any time after the [5th] anniversary of the Series A Closing, the holders of at least [66.67%] of the outstanding Series A Preferred may demand that the Company redeem all outstanding Series A Preferred at a price per share equal to the Original Issue Price plus all accrued and unpaid dividends, plus [8%] simple interest per annum. Redemption shall be subject to legal availability of funds and may be made in annual installments if full redemption would violate DGCL solvency requirements.

### 3.11 Registration Rights
- **Demand Registration:** Holders of at least [25%] of Registrable Securities may demand that the Company file a registration statement on Form S-1 (or equivalent) after the [5th] anniversary of Series A Closing or if the Company's market capitalization exceeds [$50,000,000]. The Company shall not be required to effect more than [2] demand registrations.
- **Piggyback Registration:** Holders of Registrable Securities shall have the right to include their shares in any registration statement initiated by the Company (including the IPO), subject to underwriter cutbacks (with Founders and Company shares cut back first, then Preferred on a pro-rata basis).
- **Expenses:** The Company shall bear all registration expenses (excluding underwriting discounts and selling shareholders' legal fees).

### 4.2 Series B Protective Provisions
The following actions shall require the approval of holders of at least [66.67%] of the outstanding Preferred Stock (voting as a single class, or by series if required):
- (a) Liquidation, dissolution, or winding up;
- (b) Merger, consolidation, or sale of substantially all assets;
- (c) Amendment of the Certificate of Incorporation or Bylaws adversely affecting Preferred rights;
- (d) Creation or issuance of any new class or series of stock senior to or pari passu with Preferred;
- (e) Declaration or payment of dividends on Common or other junior stock;
- (f) Incurrence of debt exceeding [$5,000,000] in aggregate principal amount;
- (g) Change in the number of directors;
- (h) Entry into a related party transaction exceeding [$250,000];
- (i) Approval or amendment of the annual operating budget (if the Board-approved budget exceeds the prior budget by [20%] or more);
- (j) Material change to the ESOP or creation of any new equity incentive plan exceeding [5%] of the fully diluted capitalization; and
- (k) Entry into or amendment of any concession, franchise, or joint venture agreement in Somaliland, India, Saudi Arabia, or the UAE with a value exceeding [$1,000,000].

### 4.3 Pay-to-Play
In any subsequent financing round (after Series B) where the Company issues equity securities at a price per share less than the Series B Original Issue Price, each holder of Series B Preferred must purchase its full pro-rata share of the new financing to maintain its anti-dilution protection and preferential rights. If a Series B holder fails to purchase its pro-rata share, its Series B Preferred shall either (a) convert to Common Stock, or (b) lose its full ratchet anti-dilution protection and convert to weighted-average protection, as determined by the Board with the consent of the participating Series B holders.

### 4.4 Management Vesting Acceleration
All management and key employee equity grants (including unvested ESOP shares and Founder shares) shall be subject to the following acceleration provisions:
- **Single Trigger:** Upon a change of control (merger, sale, or consolidation), [50%] of all unvested shares shall immediately vest.
- **Double Trigger:** If, within [12] months following a change of control, the holder's employment is terminated without cause or the holder resigns for good reason, [100%] of all unvested shares shall immediately vest.
- **Good Reason** includes a material reduction in duties, compensation, or relocation of more than [50] miles.

### 4.5 Founder Lock-Up
The Founders shall agree to a lock-up period of [180] days following the IPO, and [12] months following any sale of the Company, during which they shall not sell, transfer, or pledge their shares without the consent of the Board and the underwriters (if applicable).

### 5.2 IPO Commitment
The Company shall use its best efforts to consummate an IPO (as defined) by no later than [Year 7] (the **"IPO Target Date"**). The IPO shall be on a globally recognized stock exchange (NASDAQ, LSE, or Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ADX)). The Company shall:
- (a) Engage underwriters and legal counsel no later than [6] months prior to the IPO Target Date;
- (b) File a registration statement and all required listing documentation;
- (c) Maintain audited financials in accordance with U.S. GAAP or IFRS;
- (d) Ensure all subsidiary governance, regulatory, and compliance matters are resolved prior to filing; and
- (e) Provide quarterly updates to the Series C Investors on IPO readiness.
If the IPO is not consummated by the IPO Target Date, the Series C Investors shall have enhanced redemption rights or the right to demand a sale of the Company.

### 5.3 Right of First Refusal on Secondary Sales
If a Major Investor (other than the FIV) proposes to sell shares in a private secondary transaction, the Company shall have the first right to purchase such shares at the proposed price. If the Company declines, the remaining Major Investors shall have the right to purchase their pro-rata share of the proposed sale. If neither the Company nor the remaining Major Investors purchase all offered shares, the selling Major Investor may complete the sale to the proposed third-party purchaser within [30] days, at a price no lower than the proposed price and on terms no more favorable.

### 5.4 Co-Sale Rights
If a Major Investor proposes to sell shares in a private secondary transaction, each other Major Investor shall have the right to participate in such sale on a pro-rata basis (based on fully diluted ownership). Co-sale rights apply in addition to ROFR and are triggered simultaneously.

### 5.5 Management Carve-Out Plan
In the event of a sale of the Company (merger, consolidation, or sale of substantially all assets), [5%] of the aggregate transaction proceeds shall be set aside as a management carve-out plan for distribution to key employees (excluding the Founders and Investor Directors) as determined by the Compensation Committee. The carve-out plan shall be documented in a separate agreement and shall be subject to Board approval.

### 5.6 No-Shop Clause
From the date of execution of the Series C term sheet until the earlier of (a) [60] days, or (b) the closing of the Series C financing, the Company shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, initiate, encourage, or negotiate any alternative financing, acquisition, or investment proposal from any third party. The Company shall notify the Series C lead investor immediately upon receipt of any unsolicited proposal.

## 8. GENERAL PROVISIONS APPLICABLE TO ALL SERIES

### 8.1 Governing Law and Dispute Resolution
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict of laws principles. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Wilmington, Delaware, under the JAMS Comprehensive Arbitration Rules. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

### 8.2 Confidentiality
All parties shall maintain the confidentiality of the terms of this Agreement and all information provided pursuant to the information rights, except as required by law, stock exchange rules, or for internal due diligence purposes subject to a non-disclosure agreement.

### 8.3 Amendments
This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument executed by the Company and the holders of at least [66.67%] of the outstanding Preferred Stock (voting as a single class) and a majority of the Common Stock.

### 8.4 Transfer Restrictions
No party may transfer its shares except in accordance with the terms of this Agreement, the Certificate of Incorporation, and any applicable securities laws. All transfers shall be subject to the right of first refusal, co-sale rights, and drag-along rights set forth herein.

### 8.5 Termination
This Agreement shall terminate upon the consummation of an IPO or a sale of the Company, except for provisions that by their nature survive termination (indemnification, confidentiality, dispute resolution).
