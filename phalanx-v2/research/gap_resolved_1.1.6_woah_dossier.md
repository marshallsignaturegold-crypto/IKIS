# Gap Resolution 1.1.6 — Ethiopia WOAH FMD Dossier Status

## Gap Summary
Phase 1 research flagged uncertainty over whether Ethiopia had submitted a WOAH dossier for Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) free-zone recognition. This is critical because FMD status determines whether Ethiopian livestock can access premium export markets (Saudi Arabia, UAE, EU) without quarantine or processing restrictions. With 20.8% FMD prevalence in Bale Zone, this was a potential project derailer.

---

## Resolution Status: ✅ RESOLVED — NO DOSSIER SUBMITTED; ALTERNATIVE ROUTE REQUIRED

### T1 — WOAH / Peer-Reviewed Scientific Literature
1. **WOAH Regional Representation for Africa (2025)** — "An overview of FMD Epidemiology, Diagnostic and Surveillance in Ethiopia: Challenges and progress." Explicitly states Ethiopia has not submitted a dossier for FMD-free zone recognition. [Confidence: T1]
2. **WOAH Regional Representation for Africa — Outcomes of 5th RMM-FMD-EA (2025)** — Confirms Ethiopia remains at PCP-FMD Stage 1; Risk-Based Strategic Plan (RBSP) submitted in 2018; target transition to Stage 2 by 2027. [Confidence: T1]

5. **BMC Veterinary Research (2025)** — "Spatial and temporal patterns of FMD outbreaks (2011–2022) in cattle export-sourcing areas of southeastern Ethiopia." [Confidence: T1]

### Why a DFZ is Not Realistic for Bale Zone

1. **High endemic prevalence:** 20.8%–35.2% seroprevalence means continuous viral circulation.
2. **No vaccination program:** In sourcing districts, no routine FMD vaccination means no pathway to "vaccination-to-trade" under current practices.
3. **Wildlife reservoir:** Bale Mountains National Park harbors wild ungulates that can transmit FMD to domestic herds; a 10 km buffer zone (WOAH requirement) is impractical.
4. **Vaccine shortage:** National production <500,000 doses/year vs. need >3 million; even if vaccination were initiated, supply cannot cover the national herd.
5. **Multiple serotypes:** O, A, SAT1, SAT2 all circulate, requiring polyvalent vaccines and complex serotype-specific surveillance.
6. **12-month zero-case requirement:** Given current prevalence, achieving 12 consecutive months with zero FMD cases in Bale Zone is a multi-year endeavor.

## Alternative Routes for SSTP (FMD-Endemic Status)

### Route 1: Commodity-Based Trade (CBT) — **RECOMMENDED**
- **WOAH Terrestrial Code Article 8.8.22** permits beef export from FMD-endemic areas if:
  - Cattle are sourced from endemic areas but pass through **strict quarantine** (e.g., Adama export quarantine station).
  - Meat is **deboned and matured** to naturally inactivate FMD virus (pH reduction + time).
- **Suitability for SSTP:** HIGH. Project is already planning meat processing in Bihar SEZ; CBT aligns with processed/cold-chain export model rather than live animal trade.
- **Limitation:** Does NOT solve live animal export to Saudi Arabia (see Gap 1.1.4 for that route, which uses quarantine + vaccination, not DFZ).

### Route 2: FMD-Free Compartment (WOAH Article 8.8.4)
- **Concept:** A biosecure compartment (e.g., a closed feedlot or ranch) can be recognized as FMD-free independent of surrounding area.
- **Requirements:** Strict biosecurity, no vaccination, 10 km buffer, complete traceability.
- **Suitability for SSTP:** LOW for Bale Zone. Open-range pastoralism and proximity to wildlife make compartmentalization impractical. More feasible for commercial feedlot models in other regions.

### Route 3: Live Animal Export with Quarantine + Vaccination (Current Saudi Route)
- **Already operational** (see Gap 1.1.4): Mille Quarantine Station + 21-day hold + vaccination within 60 days + negative test.
- **Suitability for SSTP:** HIGH for live animal segment; does not require DFZ status.
- **Limitation:** Dependent on Saudi MEWA policy; could be revoked if FMD outbreaks spike.

## Implications for SSTP Project

| Implication | Assessment |
|-------------|------------|
| **WOAH DFZ is NOT a viable project milestone** | Do not plan around achieving FMD-free zone status in Bale Zone within the project timeline. |
| **CBT is the strategic export pathway** | Processed beef (deboned, matured, frozen/chilled) from an Adama or Bihar SEZ facility can legally enter FMD-sensitive markets (UAE, Saudi, Oman) under WOAH CBT rules. |
| **Live animal export remains viable** | Saudi corridor works via quarantine/vaccination, not DFZ. This is a separate operational track. |
| **Invest in quarantine infrastructure** | Rather than investing in DFZ dossier preparation, allocate resources to: (a) Mille Quarantine capacity expansion, (b) Adama export quarantine for CBT, (c) cold-chain processing at Bihar SEZ. |
| **Long-term FMD control is still valuable** | Even without DFZ ambitions, reducing FMD prevalence improves animal health, weight gain, and carcass quality. Support PCP-FMD Stage 2 transition as a **livestock productivity** initiative, not an **export enabler**. |

---

## Recommended Action

**MODIFY — abandon the WOAH DFZ milestone; pivot to CBT + quarantine-led export model.**

1. **Immediate (July 2026):** Remove "WOAH FMD-free zone recognition" from the SSTP critical path. Reclassify it as a long-term (10+ year) strategic aspiration, not a project blocker.
2. **Short-term (Q3 2026):** Engage WOAH regional office for Africa and Ethiopian EAA to design a **CBT-compliant value chain** for SSTP-sourced cattle:
   - Quarantine at Adama or Mille for 21 days.
   - Transport to Bihar SEZ processing facility.
   - Deboning + maturation + cold-chain packaging.
3. **Parallel (2026–2027):** Support Ethiopia's PCP-FMD Stage 2 transition as a **parallel livestock health investment** (improved productivity, reduced mortality), but do not tie project go/no-go to it.
4. **Contingency:** Maintain dual-track export strategy:
   - **Track A:** Live animals to Saudi Arabia (via Mille quarantine + vaccination).
   - **Track B:** Processed beef to UAE/Oman/Global markets (via CBT + cold chain).
