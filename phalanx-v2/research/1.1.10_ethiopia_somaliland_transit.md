# Stream 1.1.10 — Ethiopia-Somaliland Transit Agreement

**Research Date:** 2025-07-10

---

## 1. Existing Transit Agreements

- **Primary Instrument:** A **Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)** signed on **January 1, 2024**, between Ethiopia and Somaliland to secure sea access and diversify port options, including a potential coastal lease for a naval/commercial port.
- **Status:** Full execution has been **delayed by broader geopolitical disputes** (Somalia's objections, regional tensions). However, both sides **reaffirm readiness** to negotiate port access.
- **Other Frameworks:**
  - Bilateral efforts to design a **Simplified Trade Regime (STR)** based on COMESA standards
  - Implementation of **One-Stop Border Post (OSBP)** models
- **Assessment:** No ratified, legally binding **transit treaty** was found in public sources. The MoU appears to be the primary instrument.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandCurrent; EU-Som ITBP; HornPost; AA.com.tr; Somaliland Chronicle; Saxafi Media

---

## 2. Customs Protocols for Cross-Border Trade

- **Ethiopia:** Emphasizes revenue collection and tariff enforcement; integrated digital cargo manifests via **ASYCUDA system** (2020)
- **Somaliland:** Utilizes **clan-based trust networks** alongside basic digital systems introduced in 2018
- **Joint Efforts:** OSBP training conducted **June 2026** to harmonize systems, digitize documentation, and synchronize customs
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandCurrent; WCO Magazine; BorderCrossingHub

---

## 3. Border Crossings and Capacities

- **Primary Crossing:** **Tog-Wajaale** (Somaliland side) / **Togo Wuchale** (Ethiopian side)
- **Capacity:** Handles **over 30,000 metric tons of cargo per month**
- **Operating Hours:** Daylight hours (06:00–18:00 or 07:00–17:00)
- **Truck Volume:** ~**1,000 trucks daily** during peak periods
- **Infrastructure:** Togwajaale Border Bridge reconstruction began **May 2026** to replace vulnerable concrete riverbeds/fords that previously caused up to **6-hour delays** during rains
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** TradeMark Africa; BorderCrossingHub; LCA LogCluster; Grokipedia

---

## 4. Transit Times

- **Total Corridor Distance:** **937 km** (241 km Somaliland, 696 km Ethiopia)
- **Land Transit:** Recent upgrades have **cut land transit times by more than one-third**
- **Border Clearance:**
  - Pedestrian / passenger: **30–90 minutes**
  - Commercial truck (Ethiopian side, peak): **2–3 days**
- **Truck Turnaround (to Dire Dawa):** ~**3 days**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Berbera Corridor Pre-Feasibility Study; BorderCrossingHub; LCA LogCluster; Saxafi Media

---

## 5. Freight Costs

- **Ethiopia Inland (containers):** ~**$0.92 per TEU per km**
- **Ethiopia Inland (dry bulk, e.g., fertilizer):** ~**$0.068 per ton-km**
- **Somaliland Short-Haul:** Cross-border truckloads of agricultural/food commodities: ~**$400 per trip**
- **Note:** No comprehensive **per-ton-km figure** for the full Berbera Corridor (Ethiopia to Berbera port) was found.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Sources:** Journal of Transport and Supply Chain Management; FEWS NET Togwajale Profile

---

## 6. Customs Procedures

- **Documentation:**
  - Ethiopia: Electronic cargo manifests via **ASYCUDA**
  - Somaliland: Basic digital systems + clan-based networks
  - Maritime: **Electronic Cargo Tracking Note (ECTN)** required at least 5 days prior to vessel departure for Berbera shipments
- **Inspection:** Physical inspection rates vary; Ethiopian side emphasizes revenue protection
- **Clearance Time:** 2–3 days for trucks at peak on Ethiopian side; Somaliland side generally faster
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandCurrent; WCO Magazine; BorderCrossingHub; ECTN Somaliland FAQ

---

## 7. Duties and Tariffs on Ethiopian Exports Through Somaliland

- **Somaliland Standard Tariff:** Simple unweighted average **22%**
  - Food imports: **26%**
  - Fuel/transportation: **12%**
- **Trade Area Status:** Neither country currently participates in the **COMESA Free Trade Area**, so standard schedules apply
- **Simplified Trade Regime (STR):** Under bilateral negotiation to exonerate small-scale border trade under value thresholds (historically aligned with COMESA **$2,000 threshold**)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** EU-Som ITBP Trade Barriers Report; ODI AfCFTA and Berbera Corridor; STR Roadmap PDF

---

## 8. Infrastructure for Transit

- **Roads:** Well-maintained tarmac roads on Berbera Corridor funded by **Abu Dhabi Fund for Development** and **UK DFID**
- **Somaliland Side:** Rebuilt **250 km highway** and **22.5 km Hargeisa Bypass**
- **Bridges:** Togwajaale Border Bridge reconstruction began May 2026; late 2025 flooding collapsed a key bridge (now being rebuilt by UAE contractor NBHH)
- **Checkpoints / Quarantine:** Animal quarantine stations exist at border (Jijiga, Mile, Metema on Ethiopian side)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** TradeMark Africa; Saxafi Media; Berbera Corridor Pre-Feasibility Study; DP World Ethiopia MoU

---

## 9. Security Conditions

- **Corridor Stability:** Highly stable compared to alternative routes through Somalia
- **Sporadic Issues:** Localized cargo audits to counter contraband smuggling
- **Ethiopian Side:** Regional conflict has occurred but cargo generally moves normally
- **Somaliland Side:** No major banditry reported; land security is stable
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** TradeMark Africa; Saxafi Media; Somaliland Chronicle

---

## 10. Role of Ethiopian Ministry of Trade and Regional Integration

- **Active Role:** Along with the Ethiopian Customs Commission, engaged in bilateral negotiations with Somaliland's Ministry of Trade and Tourism
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Implementing OSBP frameworks
  - Establishing STR common list of originating goods
  - Organizing training to standardize border procedures
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandCurrent; EU-Som ITBP STR Roadmap

---

## 11. Bilateral Trade Volume

- **Formal Trade:** Estimated **30,000–35,000 tons annually**
- **Informal Trade:** Significantly larger than formal trade
- **Berbera Share:** By early 2026, transit volumes through Berbera accounted for **22% of Ethiopia's total maritime trade**
- **Somaliland Livestock Exports:** **$170–180 million annually** (mostly to Gulf markets — not to Ethiopia)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Berbera Corridor Pre-Feasibility Study; ODI; GovSomaliland Trade Statistical Bulletin; FEWS NET

---

## 12. Main Ethiopian Exports Through Somaliland

- **Ethiopia → Somaliland:**
  - Agricultural produce: **potatoes, onions, khat, fruits, coffee**
  - Manufactured items
- **Somaliland → Ethiopia:**
  - Food items: **rice, sugar, wheat flour, cooking oil** (imported via Berbera)
  - Local washing powder
- **Note:** Livestock flows primarily from Somaliland to Gulf markets, not to Ethiopia
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Berbera Corridor Pre-Feasibility Study; ODI; FEWS NET; Horn Tribune
