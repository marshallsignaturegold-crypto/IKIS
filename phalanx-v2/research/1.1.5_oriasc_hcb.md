## 1. OIC Halal Certification System (SMIIC Framework)

The Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) halal system is governed by standards developed by the **Standards and Metrology Institute for Islamic Countries (SMIIC)**.

### Key Standards

| Standard | Title | Price (USD) |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **OIC/SMIIC 1:2019** | General Requirements for Halal Food | $64 |
| **OIC/SMIIC 2:2019** | Conformity Assessment – Requirements for Bodies Providing Halal Certification | $80 |
| **OIC/SMIIC 3:2019** | Conformity Assessment – Requirements for Halal Accreditation Bodies | $68 |

- **OIC/SMIIC 2:2019** draws on **ISO/IEC 17065** (product/service certification) with normative references to **ISO/IEC 17021-1** and **ISO/TS 22003**.
- **Purpose:** Facilitate recognition and acceptance of halal certifications on a national, OIC, and international basis to support international trade.
- **SMIIC/CCA:** SMIIC Committee on Standards for Conformity Assessment prepares policies and standards for testing, calibration, certification, inspection, and accreditation.

## 2. What is a Halal Certification Body (HCB)?

A **Halal Certification Body (HCB)** is an organization that operates halal product, service, process, or management system certification in a **competent, consistent, and impartial manner**.

### General Requirements for HCBs (per OIC/SMIIC 2)
- Must be an **Islamic entity**
- Take **full responsibility** for conformity with all Islamic requirements
- Sign halal certification agreements with clients
- Have full responsibility for halal certification decisions
- Use halal certification marks adhering to Islamic values
- Maintain **impartiality** (avoid offering consultancy services)
- Evaluate and minimize conflicts of interest
- Ensure **financial stability and liability coverage**
- Maintain transparent and non-discriminatory fee policies
- Implement **traceability** with unique identifiers on products and packaging

### Resource Requirements
- **Competent management and personnel** with appropriate knowledge
- **Audit team composition:** At least two personnel:
  1. **Technical auditor** — lead auditor qualifications, at least 4 years work experience (2 years in halal certification)
  2. **Islamic affairs expert** — at least 2 years work experience in Islamic work area
- All personnel must adhere to **Islamic work ethics**
- Defined competence criteria and ongoing evaluation of performance

### Process Requirements
- Initial audit and certification
- **Surveillance** activities (ongoing monitoring)
- **Recertification** (periodic renewal)
- **Special audits** (triggered by complaints or operational changes)
- Formal **appeals and complaints** handling
- Record management and change control

## 3. ORIASC-HCB (Ethiopia)

### Identity
- **Full Name:** Oromiya Region Islamic Affairs Supreme Council Department of Halal Certification Body (ORIASC-HCB)
- **Websites:** [oriasc.org](https://oriasc.org/) | [halal.parfonet-tech.com](https://halal.parfonet-tech.com/)
- **Location:** Ethiopia (Oromia Regional State)

### Accreditation
- **Emirates International Accreditation Center (EIAC)**
- **Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA) Halal Center**

### Role
National halal certification body in Ethiopia. Inspects and certifies agro-food and consumer products in accordance with **Sharia law** and **Gulf Standardization Organization (GSO)** standards to facilitate exports to Saudi Arabia, UAE, and other Gulf markets.

### Services
- Halal product certification
- Halal standards development
- Halal product inspection
- Training of experts
- Halal awareness promotion
- Online application tracking via Oromia Majlis HRMS

### Key Personnel

- **Chairman:** Sheikh Bedru Nuru Tolosa (spoke at 1st Global Halal Industry Summit 2024 on "The Responsibility of Issuing Halal Certificate")

## 4. ORIASC vs. SFDA Role

| Entity | Role | Scope |
|--------|------|-------|
| **ORIASC-HCB** | National HCB in Ethiopia | Issues halal certificates to Ethiopian facilities; conducts inspections and audits per Sharia/GSO standards |
| **SFDA Saudi Halal Center** | Saudi regulatory authority | Recognizes and accredits foreign HCBs; operates mandatory halal.gov.sa portal; sets import rules for Saudi market |
| **OIC/SMIIC** | Standard-setting body | Develops international halal conformity assessment standards (OIC/SMIIC 1, 2, 3) |

**Relationship:** ORIASC-HCB must be recognized by SFDA Saudi Halal Center to issue certificates that Saudi customs and MEWA will accept for meat and food imports.

---

## 5. Requirements for an HCB to be Recognized by Saudi Arabia

1. **Formal accreditation** by a recognized accreditation body (e.g., EIAC, or national accreditation body aligned with ISO/IEC 17011).
2. **SFDA Saudi Halal Center recognition** — foreign HCBs must be listed/approved by SFDA to issue certificates through **halal.gov.sa**.
3. **Compliance with OIC/SMIIC 2:2019** — competent personnel, impartial processes, surveillance, complaint handling, traceability.
4. **Islamic entity status** — must operate as an Islamic organization adhering to Sharia principles.
5. **Audit capability** — must have qualified technical auditors and Islamic affairs experts.
6. **Agreement with clients** — formal halal certification agreements with facilities being certified.

---

## 6. Audit Requirements for HCBs

| Audit Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Initial Audit** | Full facility audit by audit team (technical + Islamic expert) to verify halal compliance |
| **Surveillance** | Ongoing monitoring; frequency not explicitly defined in OIC/SMIIC 2 but typically annual or bi-annual |
| **Recertification** | Periodic renewal audit to maintain HCB recognition |
| **Special Audits** | Triggered by complaints, operational changes, or non-compliance findings |
| **Complaint Handling** | Formal procedures required; records and corrective actions must be maintained |

## 8. Bilateral Agreements

### Saudi Arabia — Ethiopia
- **Saudi-Ethiopian Business Council** established under the **Federation of Saudi Chambers** (announced February 2026 per Capital Ethiopia).
- Focus: overcoming barriers to livestock and meat exports, including halal certification alignment.
- **Specific halal mutual recognition MOU:** Not located in public sources.

### Saudi Arabia — Somaliland
- Saudi Arabia requested Somaliland initiate **fish exports** to replace disrupted supplies from Yemen.
- Key condition: establishment of a **dedicated health certification laboratory** in Somaliland.
- This mirrors the veterinary checks already in place for Somaliland's live animal exports.

### GCC Recognition Precedent
- **Eatsafe Certification PLC** (Ethiopian halal certifier, established 2018) received **GCC Standardization Organization halal accreditation in December 2020**.
- This is a precedent for Ethiopian HCB recognition by Gulf accreditors.

## 9. Precedents for OIC HCB Recognition in the Livestock Sector

1. **Eatsafe Certification PLC (Ethiopia)** — accredited by Ethiopian National Accreditation Office; received GCC halal accreditation December 2020.
2. **ORIASC-HCB (Ethiopia)** — accredited by EIAC and SFDA Halal Center; currently certifies Ethiopian agro-food products for Gulf export.
3. **Saudi SFDA portal (halal.gov.sa)** — foreign HCBs must be recognized to issue HFC/HSC for meat/poultry imports. Historically, certificates from Islamic centers approved by the Muslim World League were accepted; now SFDA-designated HCBs are required.
4. **ORIASC chairman** spoke at the 1st Global Halal Industry Summit 2024, indicating active international engagement and credibility building.
