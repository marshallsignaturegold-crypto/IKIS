## 1. What is an Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR)?

An Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) is a digital certificate representing ownership of physical gold stored in a SEBI-registered vault. EGRs were notified as **"securities"** under Section 2(h)(iia) of the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956, via Gazette Notification S.O. 5401(E) dated December 24, 2021. [T1]

EGRs enable:
- **Trading** on stock exchanges (BSE, NSE) like any other security
- **Demat holding** through NSDL/CDSL
- **Physical redemption** by converting EGRs back to gold bars/coins (subject to charges and 3% GST)
- **Standardized purity**: gold must meet India Good Delivery Standard or LBMA Good Delivery standard

The EGR ecosystem was approved by SEBI Board on September 28, 2021, and operationalized through the **Gold Exchange** (or EGR segment) framework. [T1: SEBI Master Circular June 24, 2024]

## 2. Who Can Register?

### 2.1 Vault Manager
A Vault Manager is a SEBI-registered intermediary providing vaulting services for gold deposited for creation of EGRs. [T1]

**Eligibility criteria** (Regulation 4 of VM Regulations, 2021):
- Must be a **body corporate incorporated in India**
- **Minimum net worth of Rs. 50 crore** (as defined under Companies Act, 2013)
- Must have **necessary infrastructure**: adequate office space, vaults for safe deposit/storage/withdrawal, and computer systems
- Must have a **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)** for all recognized vaults
- Must have **competent personnel** with required experience and ability

**Note:** SEBI amended the Custodian Regulations in September 2025, raising the general custodian net worth requirement from Rs. 50 crore to **Rs. 75 crore** (with a 3-year transition for existing custodians). It is unclear whether this amendment has been explicitly applied to Vault Managers under the VM Regulations. **This is a P1 gap.** [T1: SEBI Custodian Amendment 2025]

### 2.2 Depositories
Only **NSDL** and **CDSL** are currently designated to hold EGRs in demat form and provide the common interface for Vault Managers. No new depository registration pathway is open. [T1]

### 2.3 Trading Members
Stock brokers registered with SEBI and holding exchange membership on BSE/NSE can trade in the EGR segment. No separate EGR-specific registration is required, but brokers must comply with the exchange's EGR segment rules and SEBI's risk management framework. [T2]

## 3. Capital Requirements, Net Worth, and Infrastructure

### 3.1 Vault Manager
| Requirement | Detail | Source |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Minimum Net Worth | Rs. 50 crore (Rs. 75 crore if Custodian Amendment applies) | T1: VM Regulations 2021; T1: Custodian Amendment 2025 |
| Fixed Sum Deposit (FSD) | Rs. 10 lakh to NSDL or CDSL (maintained at all times) | T1: SOG for VM & Depositories |
| Application Fee | Rs. 1 lakh (non-refundable) | T1: VM Regulations, Second Schedule |
| Registration Fee | Rs. 10 lakh | T1: VM Regulations, Second Schedule |
| Renewal Fee | Rs. 10 lakh every 3 years | T1: VM Regulations, Second Schedule |
| Infrastructure | Office space, vaults, IT systems, real-time gold tracking | T1: VM Regulations 2021 |

### 3.2 Infrastructure Requirements for Vaults (Minimum)
- **Direct control**: Vaults must be under absolute control of the VM; leased properties require a minimum 3-year lease with no third-party interference
- **Connectivity**: Well-connected by rail/road; sufficient parking; accessible by armoured vehicles
- **Strong rooms**: Adequate size and strength; protection from fire, theft, burglary
- **Segregation**: EGR gold must be stored in separate racks, clearly demarcated from other business
- **Security**: Adequate armed/unarmed personnel; armed guards must hold valid gun licenses
- **Real-time tracking**: Systems to track gold from deposit to withdrawal
- **Source**: T1: SEBI SOG for Vault Managers & Depositories

## 4. Documents Required for Registration

1. **Form A** of the First Schedule (Application Form)
2. **Net Worth certificate** (not older than 3 months)
3. **Audited financial statements** for last two financial years
4. **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)** for all recognized vaults covering:
   - Deposit and withdrawal procedures
   - Creation and extinguishment of EGRs
   - Security protocols
   - IT systems and data reconciliation
5. **Infrastructure details** (office space, vaults, IT systems)
6. **Personnel details** (experience, qualifications)
7. **Proof of incorporation** and corporate structure
8. **Application fee** of Rs. 1 lakh

**Source**: T1: VM Regulations 2021; T2: TaxGuru summaries

## 6. Ongoing Compliance Requirements

### 6.1 Net Worth
- Must maintain minimum net worth **at all times** during the validity of registration

### 6.2 Quarterly Compliance
- **Compliance Officer** must issue a quarterly declaration to the Depository within **7 working days** from the end of each quarter, confirming compliance with all applicable Acts, rules, regulations, and circulars

### 6.3 Reconciliation and Physical Verification
- Vault Manager and Depositories must **regularly reconcile** EGR data with physical gold
- Depositories must conduct **physical verification** of gold at least **once a fortnight**
- **Full annual inspection** of Vault Manager and vaults by Depositories

### 6.4 Records Retention
- All records and documents must be preserved for a **minimum of 5 financial years**

### 6.5 SEBI Inspection
- SEBI may appoint inspecting officers with at least **10 days' notice**
- SEBI may appoint auditors to inspect books and gold deposits; VM bears the cost

### 6.6 Segregation
- If the VM carries on other activities, the EGR vaulting business must be **separate and segregated**, with dedicated space for EGR gold

**Source**: T1: VM Regulations 2021; T1: SOG for VM & Depositories
