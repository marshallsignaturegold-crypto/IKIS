# Stream 1.3.1 — Somaliland-Berbera Logistics

**Research Date:** 2025-07-10

---

## 1. Berbera Port Capacity

- **Phase 1 (Completed June 2021):**
  - Capacity increased from **150,000 TEU** to **500,000 TEU annually**
  - Facilities: **400m quay**, **17m draft**, **3 STS gantry cranes**, **8 RTGs**
- **Phase 2 (Planned):**
  - Triggered at **75% Phase 1 utilization**
  - Quay extended to **1,000m**
  - STS cranes increased to **10 total**
  - Capacity expanded to **2 million TEU annually**
  - Enables simultaneous handling of multiple large container vessels
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** The National News; India Shipping News; Zawya; DP World press release

---

## 2. Road Infrastructure to Ethiopia

- **Total Distance:** **937 km** (241 km Somaliland, 696 km Ethiopia)
- **Somaliland Side:**
  - Rebuilt **250 km highway**
  - **22.5 km Hargeisa Bypass**
  - Funded by **Abu Dhabi Fund for Development** and **UK DFID**
- **Maintenance Issues:**
  - Severe seasonal flooding in **late 2025** collapsed a key bridge
  - UAE contractor **NBHH** resumed rebuilding in **early 2026**
- **Trade Share:** By early 2026, transit volumes through Berbera accounted for **22% of Ethiopia's total maritime trade**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Berbera Corridor Pre-Feasibility Study; Maritimafrica; The Loadstar; Saxafi Media

---

## 3. Cold Chain Facilities

- **Berbera Special Economic Zone:** Dedicated cold chain handling and storage areas
- **Solar-Powered Refrigeration:** Units and cold storage in Berbera and coastal communities
- **Berbera Fish Market:** New solar-powered facility with built-in cold rooms completed **2024** (TradeMark Africa partnership)
- **Airport:** Berbera International Airport has cargo facilities for **frozen and chilled perishable cargo**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** EU-Som ITBP Trade Barriers Report; Somaliland Standard; TradeMark Africa

---

## 4. Customs Efficiency

- **Processing:** **24-hour electronic processing** at Berbera Port
- **Inspection Rate:** Average container inspection rate ~**8%**
- **ECTN Requirement:** All import shipments require Electronic Cargo Tracking Note at least **5 days** prior to vessel departure
- **One-Stop Service Centre:** Handles consolidated logistics documentation
- **Assessment:** Generally **fast-tracked** compared to traditional regional bottlenecks, but specific clearance-time metrics (days per container) were not found.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** DP World press release; 56ok.com; ECTN Somaliland FAQ

---

## 5. Shipping Routes to Major Markets

- **Jebel Ali – Berbera Service (DP World + Summit Shipping, launched Oct 2025):**
  - Rotation: **Dubai – Aden – Djibouti – Berbera**
  - Frequency: **9-day rotation**
  - Vessels: Two **713 TEU** feeder vessels
- **RGS Service (PIL + Feedertech):**
  - Rotation: **Mundra (India) – Dubai – Djibouti – Jeddah – Berbera**
  - Frequency: **3-week rotation**
  - Vessels: Three **1,800 TEU** vessels
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Maritimafrica; The Loadstar

---

## 6. Freight Costs from Berbera

- **FCL Indicative Rates (highly variable due to geopolitical conditions):**
  - Mundra → Berbera: ~**$7,306**
  - Berbera → Khor Fakkan: **$8,131 – $8,470**
  - Berbera → Jebel Ali: **$14,877 – $15,497**
- **Insurance Mitigation:** Somaliland Coast Guard provides maritime escorts to reduce war risk insurance premiums
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Sources:** Cogoport rate platforms; Saxafi Media (security/insurance context)

---

## 7. Port Fees and Charges

- **Terminal Handling Charges (THC):**
  - Imports / local cargo: **$482 (20ft)** / **$859 (40ft)** — non-cigarettes
  - Transit cargo: **$330 (20ft)** / **$650 (40ft)**
- **Storage (Container Yard):**
  - Free first **8 days**
  - Days 9–14: **$2/TEU**
  - Day 29 onwards: **$10.50/TEU**
- **Marine Services:**
  - Berthing/unberthing (up to 3,000 GRT): **$400**
  - Pilotage (up to 3,000 GRT): **$300**
  - Pilotage (larger vessels): **$0.12/RTG**
  - General port service charge: **$90 per ship**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** DP World Berbera Tariff Book (Jan 2022); 56ok.com

---

## 8. DP World Concession Structure

- **Awarded:** May 2016
- **Term:** **30 years** with optional **10-year extension**
- **Investment:** Total planned up to **$442 million**
- **Shareholding:**
  - DP World: **51%** (controlling)
  - Somaliland: **30%**
  - Ethiopia: **19%** (failed to acquire share in 2022)
- **Government Revenue:**
  - **$5 million annually** from 2020
  - **10%** of port handling services revenue
- **Tax Holiday:**
  - **100%** corporate tax for first 10 years
  - **50%** for next 5 years
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** BBC News; DIIS RVI Briefing; HowWeMadeItInAfrica; SomalilandBiz; Hiiraan Online

---

## 9. Berbera Economic Zone (BEZ) Status

- **Opened:** **March 2023**
- **Model:** Based on **Dubai's Jebel Ali Free Zone (Jafza)**
- **Master Plan:** Over **1,200 hectares**
- **Registration:** One-stop-shop via **Jafza-linked system**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandInvest.com; DP World; Jafza; Saxafi Media

---

## 10. Free Zone Incentives

- **0% corporate tax** for **50 years**
- **100% foreign ownership**
- **100% capital and profit repatriation**
- **Duty-free storage** of goods
- **Exemption from import/export permits**
- **Ability to employ foreign labor**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandInvest.com; DP World; Jafza

---

## 11. Labor Costs in Somaliland

- **Unskilled Casual Labor:**
  - Standard: ~**$54/month** or **$6.15/day**
  - Berbera (heat/hardship premium): ~**$10/day**
- **Semi-Skilled:**
  - Standard: ~**$92/month**
- **Skilled / Specialized (Berbera):**
  - ~**$15/day** or **$120/month** standard
- **In-Port Stevedoring / Porter Unions (Gelle):**
  - **$3.50 per metric ton**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** LCA LogCluster — Somalia Manual Labor Costs

---

## 12. Utility Costs

- **Electricity:**
  - Historically up to **$1.00/kWh** due to private monopoly operators and high grid losses
- **Water:**
  - Highly scarce; 200-liter tank price rose to **17,000 SL SH** (~$4.00 in rural areas) as of early 2026 due to diesel costs
- **Fuel:**
  - Volatile; petrol averaged **8,200–8,400 SL SH** (~$0.81/liter) in early 2026
  - Government established technical committee **April 2026** to explore cheaper fuel for industrial users
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Somaliland Chronicle; Gallaydh.com; WFP; Dawan Africa

---

## 13. Security Situation

- **Piracy:** No successful piracy attacks in Somaliland territorial waters in **over a decade**
- **Insurance Risk:** Somaliland waters remain classified as **high-risk** by the Joint War Committee (JWC) due to lack of international recognition
- **Mitigation:**
  - Somaliland Coast Guard provides **24-hour escorts**
  - **Berbera Secure Approach Protocol (BSAP)** introduced early 2026
  - Establishes a **Secure Maritime Zone** to provide compliance certificates for vessel insurers
- **Land Security:** Stable along the Berbera Corridor; cargo moves normally despite regional conflicts on the Ethiopian side
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Saxafi Media; The National News
