## 1. PARIVESH Portal Overview

**PARIVESH** (Pro-Active and Responsive facilitation by Interactive, Virtuous, and Environmental Single-Window Hub) is a web-based, role-based workflow application launched by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) in **August 2018**. It handles online submission and monitoring of proposals for:
- **Environmental Clearance (EC)**
- **Forest Clearance (FC)**
- **Wildlife Clearance (WL)**
- **Coastal Regulatory Zone (CRZ) Clearance**

**Current Version:** PARIVESH 2.0 — enhanced with GIS, data analytics, and new modules. [T1]

## 2. EIA Categories and SEZ Applicability

### 2.1 Category A
- **Scope:** Projects/activities with significant spatial extent of potential impacts and potential impacts on human health and natural/man-made resources.
- **Authority:** Prior EC from **Central Government (MoEFCC)** based on recommendations of **Expert Appraisal Committee (EAC)**.
- **SEZ Context:** Under EIA Notification 2006 Schedule Item 7(c), industrial estates/parks/SEZs with area **> 500 ha** and housing at least one Category A or B industry are **Category A**. A 50 sq km (5,000 ha) SEZ housing such industries would fall under Category A. [T1: EIA Notification 2006, para 4; OM April 4, 2016]

### 2.2 Category B
- **Scope:** Projects/activities with comparatively smaller impacts.
- **Authority:** Prior EC from **State/UT Environment Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA)** based on recommendations of **State Expert Appraisal Committee (SEAC)**.
- **Sub-categories:**
  - **B1:** Requires further environmental studies and preparation of an **EIA report** for appraisal.
  - **B2:** Does **not** require an EIA report.
- **SEZ Context:** If the 50 sq km SEZ does **not** house Category A/B industries, or if its area is smaller, it may be Category B. The B1/B2 determination is made by SEAC during **scoping**. [T1: EIA Notification 2006, para 4(iii)]

### 2.3 General Conditions (GC)
If a Category B project fulfills General Conditions (e.g., proximity to sensitive areas like national parks, sanctuaries, critically polluted areas), it is considered at the **Central level as Category A**. [T1: EIA Notification 2006, para 4(iii)]

### 2.4 SEZ-Specific Thresholds
Per the OM dated April 4, 2016:
- Industrial estates/SEZs > 500 ha with at least one Category A/B industry → **Category A**
- Industrial estates/SEZs > 500 ha without any Category A/B industry → **Category B**
- Industrial estates < 500 ha without Category A/B industry → **No prior EC required** (unless building/construction projects exceed 20,000 sq.m. or development area > 50 ha, in which case Item 8(a)/8(b) applies)
- **For a 50 sq km (5,000 ha) SEZ:** This far exceeds the 500 ha threshold. If it houses Category A or B industries, it is **Category A** and requires MoEFCC clearance with full EIA. [T1: OM April 4, 2016]

## 3. EIA Requirements by Category

| Category | EIA Required? | Process | Source |
|----------|---------------|---------|--------|
| **A** | Yes | Scoping by EAC → ToR → EIA/EMP → Public Consultation → Appraisal → EC | T1: EIA Notification 2006 |
| **B1** | Yes | Scoping by SEAC → ToR → EIA/EMP → Public Consultation → Appraisal → EC | T1: EIA Notification 2006 |
| **B2** | No | Form 1/1A → Appraisal without EIA → EC (if no public consultation needed) | T1: EIA Notification 2006 |

**For a 50 sq km SEZ:** Likely **Category A** → full EIA mandatory.

## 4. Forms, Documents, and Reports Required

### 4.1 Application Forms
| Form | Purpose | Applicability |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **Form 1** | Main application for prior EC | All categories (A, B) |
| **Form 1A** | Supplementary form for expansion/modernization/change in product mix | If applicable |
| **Form 1M** | For mining of minor minerals ≤ 5 ha (Category B2) | B2 mining only |
| **Pre-Feasibility Report** | To be submitted along with Form 1/1A | All categories |
| **Conceptual Plan** | For construction projects (Item 8 of Schedule) instead of pre-feasibility report | Construction/Township |
| **EIA Report + EMP** | Environmental Impact Assessment and Management Plan | Category A and B1 |
| **Summary EIA** | For website publicity during public consultation | Category A and B1 |

### 4.2 Additional Documents
- **ToR (Terms of Reference)** — determined by EAC/SEAC after scoping
- **Public Hearing Proceedings** — conducted by SPCB/UTPCC
- **NOC from SPCB/UTPCC** — as required
- **Site clearance** (for specific projects like mining, thermal power, hydro) — MoEFCC/State decision within 30 days

**Source:** T1: EIA Notification 2006, para 6; Stage (2) Scoping

## 5. Timeline

### 5.1 Key Statutory Timelines
| Stage | Timeline | Source |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **Scoping / ToR determination** | No fixed statutory timeline; typically 30–60 days | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (2) |
| **Public hearing request to SPCB** | Applicant requests via simple letter | T1: Schedule IV |
| **SPCB forwards proceedings** | Within **45 days** of request | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3)(iii) |
| **Public hearing completion** | Within **45 days** of request; if SPCB fails, another agency gets further **45 days** | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3)(iii)-(iv) |
| **Website publicity (Summary EIA)** | Within **7 days** of written request for public hearing | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3)(vi) |
| **Appraisal completion** | EAC/SEAC completes within **60 days** of receipt of final EIA/EMP | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3)(iii) |
| **Final decision after appraisal** | Competent authority decides within **15 days** of recommendations | T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3)(iii) |
| **Total estimated** | **6–12 months** for Category A; **4–8 months** for Category B | T2: Industry practice |

### 5.2 PARIVESH-Specific Workflow Timelines (SPCB Comments)
- **SPCB raises demand note:** Within 7 days of receiving request [T1: OM Jan 14, 2025]
- **SPCB verifies payment and uploads comments:** Within 15–30 days of receipt of payment [T1: OM Jan 14, 2025]
- **Note:** If comments not uploaded within 15 days, system moves proposal to "Raise Demand Note beyond Timeline" bucket [T1: OM Jan 14, 2025]

## 6. Fees

**EIA Notification 2006 does not prescribe a uniform national fee structure.** Fees vary by:
- **State/UT** (SEIAA fee schedules)
- **Project type and size**
- **SPCB/UTPCC processing charges**

Typical ranges (indicative, not statutory):
- **EC application fee:** Nominal to Rs. 1–5 lakh at Central level; state-level fees may be 0.1%–0.5% of project cost with min/max caps
- **Public hearing expenses:** Borne by applicant (newspaper advertisements, venue, panel costs)
- **SPCB NOC fees:** As per state pollution control board schedules
- **EIA consultant fees:** Market rates (typically Rs. 5–50 lakh depending on project complexity)

**Action:** Contact MoEFCC or concerned SEIAA for current fee schedule. [T2: Industry practice; T3: General web]

## 7. Public Hearing Process

### 7.1 Applicability
- **Category A and B1:** Mandatory public consultation (public hearing + written responses)
- **Category B2:** May not require public hearing depending on state-level determination
- **SEZ exemptions:** Historically, units in EPZs/SEZs were exempt from public hearing under the 2001 notification. However, the **OM dated April 4, 2016** clarified that exemption applies only to **small-scale industrial units** in industrial estates that **existed as of the EIA Notification 2006 date** and had obtained EC. Large projects (thermal power, cement, steel) **cannot** be exempted even if in SEZs. [T1: OM April 4, 2016]

### 7.2 Process Steps
1. **Applicant requests** public hearing from Member Secretary, SPCB/UTPCC via simple letter
2. **SPCB publishes notice** in at least two newspapers (one vernacular) with date, time, place
3. **Suggestions invited** within 30 days of publication
4. **Panel composition:**
   - SPCB/UTPCC representative
   - District Collector or nominee
   - State Government representative
   - State Environment Department representative
   - Up to 3 local body representatives
   - Up to 3 senior citizens nominated by District Collector
5. **Proceedings forwarded** to regulatory authority within 45 days
6. **Website publicity:** Summary EIA and application placed on SPCB/regulatory authority website within 7 days of request

### 7.3 For a 50 sq km SEZ
Given the scale, a **public hearing is very likely required** at the SEZ level during the master EC process. Individual units within the SEZ may be exempt if the SEZ has obtained prior EC and the unit is small-scale, but major industrial units will require their own public hearing. [T1: OM April 4, 2016]

**Source:** T1: EIA Notification 2006, Stage (3); Schedule IV (Public Hearing Procedure)

## 8. Conditions Typically Imposed on SEZ Projects

### 8.1 Standard EC Conditions (MoEFCC OM Jan 4, 2019)
- Abide by all EIA/EMP commitments and public hearing commitments
- Submit **six-monthly compliance reports** on PARIVESH portal
- Submit **annual environmental statement (Form-V)** to SPCB
- Publicize EC in **two local newspapers** (one vernacular) within 7 days
- Display EC on project website **permanently**
- Submit EC copies to local bodies for **30-day public display**
- Inform MoEFCC Regional Office of financial closure, land development commencement, and production start

### 8.2 SEZ-Specific Technical Conditions
- Wastewater treatment and zero liquid discharge (ZLD) requirements
- Common effluent treatment plant (CETP) if applicable
- Air pollution control (ESP, bag filters, scrubbers)
- Hazardous waste management authorization
- **Green belt development** (typically 33% of total area)
- Rainwater harvesting and groundwater monitoring
- Traffic management and road infrastructure
- CSR and local community development programs

**Source:** T1: OM Jan 4, 2019; T2: Industry practice

## 9. Compliance Monitoring Post-Approval

| Monitoring Type | Requirement | Frequency | Source |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| **Six-monthly compliance report** | Status of stipulated environmental conditions | Every 6 months | T1: OM Jan 4, 2019 |
| **Annual environmental statement (Form-V)** | To SPCB as per EP Rules, 1986 | Annual | T1: OM Jan 4, 2019 |
| **Certified Compliance Report (CCR)** | For expansion proposals — from Regional Office on compliance status of existing EC conditions | As needed for expansion | T1: OM Jan 25, 2026 |
| **Regional Office inspections** | Physical verification and compliance checks | Periodic / Risk-based | T1: EIA Notification 2006 |
| **Non-compliance action** | Revocation of EC, penalties, closure directions | As warranted | T1: EIA Notification 2006 |

### CCR Module (New)
- A dedicated CCR module is now on PARIVESH
- Hybrid/manual processing allowed until **Feb 28, 2026**
- From **Mar 1, 2026**, all CCR applications must be submitted and processed **online only**
- Workflow: PP submits request → RO head forwards to official → Site visit → Generate CCR → RO head approves → E-sign and publish

**Source:** T1: OM Jan 25, 2026

## 10. PARIVESH 2.0 Workflow Status

### Current Modules (as of early 2026)
1. **Registration / e-KYC / KYA**
2. **New application submission** (Form 1, 1A, 1M, attachments)
3. **ToR processing** (Central for Cat A; SEAC for Cat B)
4. **SPCB comments workflow** (live from Oct 24, 2025)
5. **EC processing** (EAC/SEAC appraisal, agenda, MoM)
6. **EC generation** (16-digit EC ID, e-sign, cover page)
7. **CCR module** (for expansion proposals)
8. **Forest Clearance, Wildlife Clearance, CRZ** (parallel tracks)

### EC Identification Number Format
Example: `EC21A018AR599999`
- `EC` = Environmental Clearance
- `21` = Year (2021)
- `A` = Category A
- `018` = Sector code
- `AR` = State code (Arunachal Pradesh)
- `5` = Checksum
- `99999` = Random number

**Source:** T1: OM Oct 2021 (User Manual for EC Cover Page Generation)
