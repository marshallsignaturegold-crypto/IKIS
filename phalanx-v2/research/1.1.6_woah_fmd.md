# Stream 1.1.6 — Ethiopia WOAH FMD-Free Zone

**Research Date:** 2025-07-10

---

## 1. Current FMD Status in Ethiopia

- **Status:** FMD is endemic and widely prevalent across Ethiopia with multiple circulating serotypes: **O, A, SAT 1, and SAT 2**.
- **Official Recognition:** Ethiopia does **not** currently hold an officially recognized FMD-free country or zone status from WOAH.
- **Progressive Pathway:** The country is working toward the **Progressive Control Pathway (PCP-FMD)** and intends to establish official Disease-Free Zones (DFZs) in key pastoral/export livestock-sourcing areas: **Borana, South Omo, Fafan, Ogaden, and Afar**.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** BMC Veterinary Research 2024; WOAH Africa Regional Representation; PMC review article

---

## 2. Seroprevalence in Bale Zone (20.8%)

- **Finding:** A comprehensive cross-sectional study (published Nov 2024) in export livestock-sourcing districts of **Bale Zone** found an overall FMD NSP antibody seroprevalence of **20.8%** (95% CI: 18.3–23.5).
- **District Variation:**
  - Seweyna: **35.2%**
  - Dinsho: **13.0%**
- **Serotype Exposure (among positive samples):**
  - SAT 1: **71%**
  - Serotype O: **43%**
  - SAT 2: **38%**
  - Serotype A: **30%**
  - Multiple serotypes: **15–40%**
- **Assessment:** This is the **most recent published data** for Bale Zone as of 2024. No more recent data was found.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** BMC Veterinary Research 2024; DOI reference

---

## 3. WOAH Requirements for FMD-Free Zone Recognition

Per **Chapter 8.8 of the Terrestrial Animal Health Code**, requirements include:

1. Regular and prompt animal disease reporting record
2. Documented proof of effective veterinary supervision and biosecurity
3. Strict animal movement controls and permanent animal identification
4. **Prohibition of FMD vaccination** within the zone
5. Clearly defined boundaries separating the zone from infected areas (protection zones / natural barriers)
6. Diagnostic testing at approved laboratories aligned with the WOAH Terrestrial Manual

- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11; WOAH Chapter 8.8 PDF

---

## 4. Application Process and Forms

- **Form:** Detailed questionnaire laid out in **Chapter 1.11 of the Terrestrial Animal Health Code**.
- **Package must include:**
  1. Formal letter signed by the national Delegate
  2. One-page executive summary
  3. Core dossier (~50 pages)
  4. Contact details of technical staff
  5. Proof of application fee payment
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11; WOAH Application SOP

---

## 5. 12-Month Surveillance Requirement

Documented evidence must show that for **at least 12 months**:

1. No clinical case of FMD or evidence of infection in unvaccinated animals
2. No FMD vaccination carried out
3. Active and passive surveillance per Terrestrial Code Articles 8.8.40–8.8.42 fully operational

- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11; WOAH ad hoc Group FMD Report 2023

---

## 6. Evaluation Criteria and Timeline

- **Deadline:** Applications must be submitted **at least 2 months** prior to the ad hoc Group evaluation meeting (typically October).
- **Process:**
  1. Preliminary screening by Status Department
  2. Detailed evaluation by ad hoc Group
  3. Recommendation to Scientific Commission for Animal Diseases
  4. Final recognition by World Assembly (following May)
- **Total Timeline:** ~**12–18 months** from submission to potential recognition
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH ad hoc Group FMD Report 2024; SEACFMD EpiNet May 2025

---

## 7. Emergency Preparedness Plan

The dossier must detail:

1. Clear action plan for outbreak control and eradication (stamping-out, modified stamping-out, emergency zoning, movement controls, emergency vaccination)
2. Justification of corrective actions
3. Early warning system and diagnostic capabilities to confirm suspected cases rapidly

- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11; WOAH ad hoc Group FMD Report 2023

---

## 8. Geo-Referenced Maps

- Boundaries must be clearly defined
- Application must include **digitalized, geo-referenced maps** plus precise written textual description of geographical limits and any protection zones
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11

---

## 9. Border Inspection Protocols

Must document system preventing FMD virus entry, including:

- Established border and zone veterinary inspection protocols
- Veterinary control posts
- Animal quarantine stations (Ethiopia operates stations in **Jijiga, Mile, Metema**)
- Strict regulatory controls on import/transit of live animals and commodities

- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Sources:** WOAH Chapter 1.11; IJVSR Review Article

---

## 10. FMD Detection During Evaluation

- If FMD detected during evaluation or in an already recognized zone, status is **immediately suspended** by the Director General (effective from the date of non-compliance).
- Members have **up to 2 years** to submit documentation proving successful outbreak management.
- Otherwise, status is **withdrawn** and a **new application** must be submitted.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Suspension SOP; Frontiers in Veterinary Science 2022

---

## 11. African Precedents

- **Botswana, Namibia, and Zimbabwe** have established WOAH-recognized FMD-free zones where vaccination is not practiced.
- However, these zones face high vulnerability: Botswana had multiple FMD-free zone statuses **suspended in early 2026** following outbreak notifications.
- **Lesson:** Achievable but fragile; requires sustained biosecurity and surveillance.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WRLFMD Botswana; WOAH Resolution 2026

---

## 12. Application Costs

- **Standard first-time fee:** 9,000 Euros
- **LDC discount (Ethiopia qualifies):** **50% = 4,500 Euros**
- **Re-application or new zone additions:** 2,250 Euros (LDC rate)
- **Excluded:** Travel and operational costs of expert field missions
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** WOAH Application SOP / Resolution No. 16
