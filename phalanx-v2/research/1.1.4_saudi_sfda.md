# Stream 1.1.4 — Saudi SFDA / MEWA Halal Protocol

**Research Date:** 2025-01-21  
**Confidence Level:** High (based on SFDA publications, MEWA announcements, USDA FAIRS reports, and industry guidelines)

---

## 1. Saudi Halal Center Platform (Mandatory Oct 2024)

The Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA) operates the **Saudi Halal Center (SHC)** via the mandatory online portal **halal.gov.sa**. As of **1 October 2024**, all facilities exporting halal products to Saudi Arabia must register on this platform to obtain:

- **Halal Facility Certificate (HFC)** — annual facility-level certification
- **Halal Shipment Certificate (HSC)** — per-consignment certification

**Process:**
1. Submit Halal certification request with company name and activity to Halal Center email.
2. Halal team sends requirements and application fee note.
3. Submit documents and proof of payment; back office reviews eligibility.
4. For eligible applicants, a contract including audit fees is sent for signature.
5. Audit team visits facility to verify compliance with Halal standards and metrology.
6. Halal Decision Committee issues certificate for approved applicants.

## 3. HACCP Requirements for Meat/Livestock Processing

- **Standard:** Gulf Standard **GSO 21/1984** — "Health conditions in food factories and their workers"
- **Scope:** All abattoirs, poultry slaughterhouses, and food factories preparing meat must fully implement **HACCP** at all production stages.
- **Certification:** Must be obtained from an **accredited certification body** recognized by SFDA.

---

## 4. Facility Certification Requirements

### Licensing Steps
1. Environmental permit from the National Center for Environmental Compliance Monitoring.
2. Establish and equip facility per SFDA technical guides and licensing entity requirements (Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs, Industrial/Economic Cities Authority, or Royal Commission).
3. Obtain HACCP certificate from accredited body.
4. Conduct laboratory testing in SFDA-approved private laboratory.
5. Register in unified electronic system **"Ghad"** and submit operating license request.
6. Obtain final operating license approval from SFDA.

### Facility Design & Equipment
- Area must be spacious and proportional to SFDA-approved construction plan.
- Include production halls, warehouses, staff quarters, laboratory, maintenance, and administrative offices.
- Equipment at least **2 meters** from walls; adjacent equipment at least **4 meters** apart.
- Equipment raised off ground for cleaning; made of corrosion-resistant, non-absorbent materials.
- Temperature-controlled handling and storage with monitoring and recording.
- Adequate lighting and ventilation; prevent airflow from contaminated to clean areas.
- Adequate flush lavatories, hand wash basins with hot/cold water and soap, separate from food washing.

### Staffing Requirements
- All workers must have **valid health certificates** from competent authority.
- **Periodic medical exams** at least annually; pre-resumption checks after vacations abroad.
- Workers with contaminated wounds or contagious diseases **excluded** until treated.
- First aid available on site with trained responders.
- Quality department with qualified personnel for self-monitoring, inspection, and audits.
- Preferably implement a food safety system (e.g., ISO 22000 / FSSC 22000).

### Inspection
- SFDA Designated Officer conducts **on-site visit** before license issuance.
- License valid for **one year**; can be suspended or withdrawn for violations.
- Facility must display license visibly.

## 5. Labeling Requirements

- **Standard:** Gulf Standard **GSO 9** — "Labels of packed foodstuffs"
- **Language:** Arabic and English
- **Mandatory elements:** Product name, net weight, country of origin, nutritional facts, allergen declaration, production/expiry dates, storage conditions, manufacturer/distributor name and address.
- **Halal mark rules:** Prepackaged food labels must NOT include any halal claims, symbols, logos, or marks unless authenticating evidence is provided from accredited bodies **designated by the Saudi Halal Center**.
- **Carcass labeling:** For carcasses and wholesale meat, a durable label containing primary GSO data must be securely affixed via wire to one end of the carcass.

## 6. Import Requirements for Livestock from Ethiopia / Somaliland

### Authority
**Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture (MEWA)** — Animal and Plant Quarantine Department.

### Pre-Shipment Requirements
- **Import permit** from MEWA required before shipments leave the exporting country.
- **Health certificate** from recognized authority in country of origin, **duly authenticated by Saudi embassy** in that country.
- **Direct route:** Must ship directly from country of origin to KSA without anchorage or call to any banned/infected port or country.
- **Pre-export quarantine:** Livestock must undergo strict pre-export quarantine at certified facilities (e.g., Berbera Port for Somaliland exports).
- **Disease inspection:** Inspected for epidemic diseases (Rift Valley Fever, Foot and Mouth Disease, etc.); necessary vaccinations required.

### On-Arrival Requirements
- **Veterinary inspection** mandatory at any Saudi port.
- **Whole shipment rejected** if infected with diseases in MEWA Table 1 (e.g., FMD, RVF, rabies, BSE).
- **Quarantine allowed** if infection rate ≤ 10% of random sample for diseases in MEWA Table 2. If > 10%, shipment refused entry.

### Ban Status
- **Somaliland:** Ban lifted **17 March 2022**; animals quarantined up to two weeks at Berbera port before export.
- **Ethiopia:** Conflicting reports. MEWA reported imports from Ethiopia in **April 2020**, but *The Reporter Ethiopia* (March 2022) stated the year-long ban on Ethiopian livestock remained in place. This suggests **intermittent or re-imposed restrictions**.
- **Historical bans:** Saudi Arabia banned live animal imports from Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Sudan, Kenya, and Djibouti at the end of the 1990s.

## 7. Transit and Storage Requirements (Cold Chain)

### Frozen Meat
- Kept continuously frozen at temperature **not exceeding -18°C** until reaching consumer.
- Shipment must arrive **within 4 months of slaughter**.

### Chilled Meat
- Vacuum-packed / CO2-packed meat stored at **-0.5 ± 1°C**.
- Maximum shelf-life: **90 days**.

### Container & Transit Rules
- Shipped in **closed, sealed transshipment containers waxed with red wax or lead**.
- Containers **must not be opened** or exposed to harsh weather conditions at transit stations.
- **No repacking, cutting, canning, or packaging permitted in third countries.**
- Viscera shipped separately from meat containers; accompanied by health certificate.

## 8. Certification Failure & Appeal Process

### Rejection Authority
SFDA Border Inspection Posts (BIPs) and Executive Management of Food Import Control (EDIFC).

### Common Rejection Reasons
- Non-compliance with GSO or Saudi regulations
- Missing or invalid Halal certificate
- Missing health certificate or improper authentication
- Failure of laboratory analysis
- Temperature violations for frozen/chilled shipments

### Rejected Consignments
Must be **re-exported or destroyed under SFDA supervision**.

### Appeal Process
1. **Timeline:** File written appeal within **60 days** of refusal decision.
2. **Submission:** Written appeal to **EDIFC at SFDA headquarters**, with certified letter from Chamber of Commerce and complete shipment documents.
3. **Review:** EDIFC forwards to SFDA special committee which studies shipment documentation and BIP test results.
4. **Outcome:** If BIP action found in error, EDIFC overturns and instructs clearance. If compliant with rules, rejection stands.
5. **Importer obligations:**
   - Bear costs of any new laboratory tests if re-testing approved.
   - Responsible for value of goods if shipment remains at port during appeal.
   - For frozen/chilled shipments, provide temperature reader from rejection until final decision.
6. **Repeat appeals:** Cannot be repeated for same customs declaration to same SFDA branch.

### Clearance Timeline
Containers can be cleared in **less than five working days** if all documents are in order and products meet Saudi/Gulf specifications.

## 9. Precedents for Ethiopian / Somaliland Livestock Exports to Saudi Arabia

- **April 2020:** MEWA announced 27,827 head of cattle (sheep, cows, camels) from Djibouti, **Ethiopia**, and Romania arrived in KSA.
- **April 2020:** 3,050 sheep from **Ethiopia** arrived at Al-Ahsa Regional Airport; passed physical and lab tests.
- **March 2022:** Somaliland government confirmed lifting of 11-year livestock export ban to Saudi Arabia; Djibouti and Somalia also included.
- **March 2022:** The Reporter Ethiopia reported that Saudi Arabia's year-long ban on Ethiopian livestock imports remained in place, suggesting **intermittent or re-imposed restrictions**.
