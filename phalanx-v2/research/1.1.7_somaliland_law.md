# Stream 1.1.7 — Somaliland Investment Law

**Research Date:** 2025-07-10

---

## 1. Somaliland Investment Law (2010/2020/2021)

- **Current Law:** **Somaliland Investment Act (No. 99/2021)** — supersedes the previous Foreign Investment Law (No. 29/2004/2008).
- **Scope:** Applies to wholly foreign-owned, joint-venture, and wholly domestic investments.
- **Objectives:** Promote, manage, and facilitate investments while addressing poverty and unemployment.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandLaw.com PDF; Invest in Somaliland 2025 (Gov); Investment Guide PDF

---

## 2. Incentives for Foreign Investors

- **Tax Holiday:** 100% tax holiday on business profits for **3–5 years** (depending on project scale and sector)
- **Customs Duty Exemptions:** 100% on imported capital goods, raw materials, machinery, and equipment
- **Profit Repatriation:** 100% permitted
- **Foreign Ownership:** 100% permitted
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Somaliland Foreign Investment Law; Invest in Somaliland 2025; MOIID

---

## 3. Somaliland Investment Promotion Office (MOIID)

- **Name:** Ministry of Investment and Industrial Development (MOIID), established **2017**
- **Function:** Main regulatory body for investment
- **Services:** Operates a dedicated **One-Stop Investment Center** to coordinate and streamline registration, licensing, and approval processes for domestic and foreign investors
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** GovSomaliland.org; Invest in Somaliland 2025; MOIID website

---

## 4. Concession Framework

- **Granting Authority:** Government of Somaliland (typically via Cabinet / Ministry of Investment)
- **Major Precedent — DP World Berbera Port (2016):**
  - **Duration:** 30-year concession with **optional 10-year extension**
  - **Stakeholding:** DP World 51% (controlling), Somaliland 30%, Ethiopia 19% (Ethiopia **failed to acquire** its share in 2022)
  - **Annual Fee:** $5 million annually from 2020 to Somaliland government
  - **Revenue Share:** 10% of port handling services revenue to Somaliland
  - **Tax Holiday:** 100% corporate tax for first 10 years, 50% for next 5 years
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandBiz; RVI Briefing 2019; Goobjoog; BBC News

---

## 5. Foreign Investment Protections

- **Guarantees:** Against nationalization and expropriation without due process and **prompt fair market-value compensation**
- **Dispute Resolution:**
  - Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
  - Domestic courts
  - International arbitration
- **Historical Framework:** Arbitration boards formed by representatives from both parties and a neutral chairperson or the President of the Supreme Court
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Somaliland Investment Act 2021; Foreign Investment Law 2004; Invest in Somaliland 2025

---

## 6. Tax Regime for Foreign Investors

- **Corporate Tax:** **12.3%** (business income tax, inclusive of administrative and stamp duties)
- **Inland Taxes:** Personal payroll taxes, withholding taxes, and **Goods and Services Tax (GST)**
- **Revenue Note:** Customs duties and import/export levies at ports comprise **over 70%** of total government revenues
- **Note:** No specific VAT rate confirmed in sources; GST appears to be the consumption tax instrument
- **Confidence:** Medium (headline rate is high-confidence; detailed GST rate is a gap)
- **Sources:** Somaliland National Tax Policy 2021-25; Ministry of Finance

---

## 7. Labor Law

- **Governing Law:** Private Sector Employees Law (No. 31/2004, amended 2010 and 2020)
- **SEZ Labor Quotas:**
  - **100%** of unskilled labor must be Somaliland nationals
  - **Priority** for nationals in semi-skilled labor; expectation of at least **70%** local workforce participation unless special permit granted
- **Minimum Wage:** Not confirmed in sources
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandLaw.com Labour Law; SEZ Law 2021

---

## 8. Land Tenure System

- **Ownership:** Land is **public property** commonly owned by the nation; the state acts as manager
- **Foreign Ownership:** Foreigners **cannot own land freehold** but are granted restricted rights of occupancy or **long-term leaseholds**
- **Lease Terms:** Government provides long-term leases on industrial and commercial land at preferential rates; **25-year lease terms** are common in agricultural and economic zones
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Somaliland Investment Brochure 2025; National Land Policy Scoping; OpenLandContracts

---

## 9. Environmental Requirements

- **Governing Law:** Somaliland Environmental Management Act
- **EIA Requirement:** Comprehensive **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)** by authorized experts before starting major undertakings
- **Clearance:** Proponents must obtain environmental clearance certificate from the Ministry
- **Incentives:** Customs and excise waivers on imported capital goods designed to prevent environmental degradation
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** UNEP / Somaliland Environmental Management Act PDF

---

## 10. Political Status and Investment Impact

- **Major Milestone (Dec 26, 2025):** **Israel became the first country** to officially recognize Somaliland as an independent state.
- **Reaction:** Condemned by Somalia, UN Security Council, and Arab states; supported by the United States.
- **Impact:** Significant but **not yet broad international recognition**. Most countries and international organizations still do not recognize Somaliland. This creates legal and diplomatic risk for investors, though the DP World precedent shows major projects can proceed.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** Times of Israel; Al Jazeera; UN Press

---

## 11. Foreign Concession Precedents

- **Primary Precedent:** DP World Berbera Port (2016) — the only major, well-documented foreign concession
- **Other Sectors:** Smaller precedents exist in telecom and energy but are less documented
- **Concession Durations:** 30+10 years (port)
- **Fee Structures:** Annual fixed fee + revenue share (negotiated project-by-project)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandBiz; RVI Briefing 2019; BBC News

---

## 12. Typical Concession Durations, Fees, Revenue Sharing

- **Duration:** 30 years standard, with 10-year extension option (DP World precedent)
- **Annual Fixed Fee:** $5 million (DP World example)
- **Revenue Share:** 10% of handling services revenue to government (DP World example)
- **Tax Holiday:** 100% for 10 years, 50% for next 5 years (DP World example)
- **Note:** These are **negotiated on a project-by-project basis** and serve as reference points, not fixed tariffs.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Sources:** SomalilandBiz; RVI Briefing 2019
