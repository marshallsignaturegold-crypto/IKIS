## 1. Executive Summary

**Bihar Industrial Investment Promotion Package 2025 (BIIPP-2025 / BIPPP-2025)** is the state's most ambitious industrial policy, announced by the Bihar Cabinet on **August 26, 2025**, chaired by Chief Minister Nitish Kumar. It offers free land, interest subvention up to ₹40 crore, SGST reimbursement up to 300% of project cost, capital subsidies up to 30%, and export incentives up to ₹40 lakh/year for 14 years. The policy is valid for applications submitted before **March 31, 2026**.

**Key numbers:**
- Target: **1 crore jobs** in 5 years
- Land in pipeline: **15,000 acres** for industrial use
- Land already acquired by BIADA: **8,000 acres** since inception; **14,600 acres** approved for 32 industrial areas in the past year
- FinTech City at Fatuha: **242 acres**, **₹408.81 crore** sanctioned

The policy is the successor to the **Bihar Industrial Investment Promotion Policy, 2016** and is described by officials as a "2.0 version" with stronger land and financial incentives. [T2: ETGovernment interview with Additional Chief Secretary Mihir Kumar Singh, Sept 2025]

## 3. BIIPP-2025 Application Process

### 3.1 Eligibility Criteria
| Parameter | Requirement | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Entity type** | Companies, LLPs, partnerships, proprietorships (industrial units) | T2: Economic Times |
| **Investment threshold (free land)** | ₹100 crore + 1,000 jobs (10 acres); ₹1,000 crore (25 acres); Fortune 500 (10 acres) | T2: Business Standard |
| **Application deadline** | **March 31, 2026** | T2: Business Standard |
| **Sector focus** | Textiles, apparel, food processing, agro-based, engineering goods, electronics assembly, IT/ITeS, MSMEs, renewable energy, manufacturing | T2: ETGovernment |
| **Land requirement** | If land is needed, must be arranged by purchase or lease of at least 30 years (for sector-specific policies like wood-based); for BIIPP-2025, land is provided by BIADA | T2: Forest Dept policy (for context) |

### 3.2 Three Incentive Models (Choose One)
1. **Interest Subvention + SGST Reimbursement** — For SMEs and debt-heavy projects needing cheaper debt.
2. **SGST Reimbursement (up to 300% of project cost over 14 years)** — For high sales-volume companies with high GST outflow (e.g., cement, beverages).
3. **Capital Subsidy (30%)** — For capital-intensive industries requiring heavy machinery and infrastructure upfront.

The choice is made at the time of application and is binding. [T2: ETGovernment interview with ACS Mihir Kumar Singh]

### 3.3 Application Steps (Inferred from Public Sources)
1. **Project identification** — Determine investment size, employment target, and sector.
2. **Choose incentive model** — Select among the three models based on project characteristics.
3. **Submit application** — Through the BIADA online portal or designated government portal (exact URL not fully specified in public sources; BIADA portal has "Apply Online" at https://biada1.bihar.gov.in).
4. **BIADA / SIPB processing** — Application reviewed on merit, investment, employment, innovation, and sector alignment. Decisions are time-bound and transparent; investors can join meetings virtually.
5. **Land allotment and incentive disbursement** — Upon approval, land is allotted and incentives released as per milestones.

**Note:** The exact application form, required documents, and detailed process flow are **not fully published** in the public sources reviewed. This is a **P1 gap**. [T2: Business Standard, ETGovernment]

The **Bihar Industrial Area Development Authority (BIADA)** is the nodal agency for land acquisition, development, and allotment. [T1: BIADA portal]

- **Functionality:** "Apply Online (Land & Shed)" is available on the portal.

- Begusarai

- Darbhanga

- Muzaffarpur
- Motipur
- Patna

### 4.4 Process Flow
1. **Online application** for land/shed
2. **BIADA evaluation** — Merit-based assessment considering investment, employment, innovation, and sector
3. **Payment** — Land cost (or confirmation of free land eligibility) and execution of lease/deed
4. **Post-allotment** — Use eCollection Portal for ongoing payments

## 5. Export Obligations

### 5.1 BIIPP-2025 Export Incentive
- **Up to ₹40 lakh/year** for 14 years for export promotion.
- This is an **incentive (subsidy)**, not a legal obligation. There is no mandatory export target under BIIPP-2025 itself.

### 5.2 SEZ Export Obligations (If SEZ Status Is Sought)
If the project is designated as a **Special Economic Zone (SEZ)** under the central **SEZ Act, 2005**, then:
- **Net Foreign Exchange Earning (NFE):** SEZ units must achieve positive NFE within **5 years** of commencement of production.
  - NFE = Foreign Exchange earned − Foreign Exchange spent (excluding capital goods and raw material imports).
- **Penalty for non-compliance:** Failure to achieve positive NFE may result in loss of SEZ status, withdrawal of benefits, and liability to pay duties/taxes on imported goods as if imported into the Domestic Tariff Area (DTA).
- **Developer obligations:** Complete infrastructure within specified timelines (typically 10 years for multi-product SEZs, 5 years for sector-specific SEZs). Maintain the SEZ as a fenced area with customs control.

**Important:** BIIPP-2025 does **not** automatically confer SEZ status. SEZ designation requires a separate application to the **Ministry of Commerce & Industry (SEZ Board of Approval)** under the SEZ Act, 2005. [T1: General SEZ Act knowledge; T2: BIIPP-2025 announcements do not mention SEZ status]

## 7. Existing SEZs in Bihar

### 7.1 Current Status
**Bihar does not currently have a large, fully operational multi-product SEZ under the central SEZ Act, 2005.** The state has been focusing on **Special Industrial Zones (SIZs)** and dedicated industrial parks rather than formal SEZs. The following zones exist or are planned:

| Zone | Location | Area | Status | Source |
|------|----------|------|--------|--------|
| **FinTech City at Fatuha** | Patna district, adjacent to MMLP | 242 acres | Land acquisition phase; cabinet sanctioned Aug 2025 | T1: Bihar Cabinet; T2: Business Standard |
| **Special Industrial Zones (SIZs)** | Planned in almost all districts | Various | Planning phase; new industrial policy at final stage | T2: Economic Times (April 2025) |
| **IT Parks** | Bihta (Patna), Rajgir | Not specified | Land notified; development status unclear | T1: Bihar Industrial Policy 2016 |
| **BIMCGL** | Gaya | Not specified | Referenced on BIADA portal; detailed status not available | T1: BIADA portal |

### 7.2 Key Insight
Investors seeking **SEZ-specific benefits** (duty-free imports, single-window customs clearance, 100% income tax exemption, stamp duty exemptions) must apply for SEZ designation separately under the **SEZ Act, 2005**. BIIPP-2025 is a **state-level industrial policy** that provides land and financial incentives but does **not** confer central SEZ status. [T2: BIIPP-2025 announcements; T1: General SEZ Act knowledge]

## 8. FinTech City at Fatuha

### 8.1 Overview
- **Location:** Fatuha circle, Patna district, Bihar
- **Area:** **242 acres**
- **Investment sanctioned:** **₹408.81 crore** for land acquisition
- **Model:** Modeled on **Gujarat International Finance Tec-City (GIFT City)** between Ahmedabad and Gandhinagar
- **Adjacent infrastructure:** Multi Modal Logistic Park (MMLP) at Fatuha circle
- **Acquiring authority:** Infrastructure Development Authority in Patna
- **Status:** Land acquisition phase; approved by Bihar Cabinet in August 2025

### 8.2 Relation to SEZ
The FinTech City has **not been explicitly designated as an SEZ** under the SEZ Act, 2005 in current announcements. It is a **state-level dedicated zone** for FinTech and digital services. If SEZ status is sought, a separate application to the Ministry of Commerce & Industry (SEZ Board of Approval) would be required.

**Size consideration:** A 242-acre zone is smaller than typical **multi-product SEZs** (which require 1,000+ hectares or 2,500+ acres). However, **sector-specific SEZs** can be smaller (minimum 10 hectares with 100% FDI). FinTech/IT/ITeS could potentially qualify as a sector-specific SEZ if applied for. [T1: Bihar Cabinet; T2: Business Standard, Economic Times]
