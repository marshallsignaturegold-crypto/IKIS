# SEBI EGR (Electronic Gold Receipt) Issuer Registration — BIBC Bihar SEZ

**Filing Deadline:** August 15, 2026  
**Jurisdiction:** India (SEBI — Securities and Exchange Board of India)  
**Framework:** Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) Regulations, 2022 (as amended 2025)  
**Exchange:** India International Bullion Exchange (IIBX), GIFT City, IFSCA  
**Applicant:** Bihar International Bullion Corporation (BIBC) — SEZ Unit, Bihar  
**Refinery Capacity:** 25 TPD (tonnes per day) gold dore → 99.5%+ LBMA Good Delivery Bars  

---

## 1. REGULATORY CONTEXT: TOKEN MIGRATION TO EGRs

**Critical Finding:** SEBI has NO regulatory framework for tokenized bullion, blockchain-based bullion scrip, or crypto-asset backed by physical gold. The EGR framework (introduced 2022, expanded 2025) is the ONLY legal pathway for digital representation of physical gold in India.

| Feature | BIBC Token (DEPRECATED) | SEBI EGR (MANDATORY) |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Legal basis | None (unregulated) | SEBI EGR Regulations, 2022 |
| Regulatory body | None | SEBI + IFSCA |
| Exchange | Unregulated DEX | IIBX (GIFT City) |
| Investor protection | None | SEBI Investor Protection Fund |
| Settlement | T+0 (crypto) | T+1 (regulated) |
| Custody | Self-custody (risky) | SEBI-registered vault (BIBC) |
| Tax treatment | Unclear (potentially 30% crypto tax) | 12.5% LTCG (if held >36 months) |
| Foreign investment | Prohibited | 100% FDI permitted under IFSCA |
| Tokenization premium | 5% (assumed) | 0% (spot price) |

**Migration Impact:** Remove 5% token premium from BIBC financial model. EGRs trade at spot gold price with 0.5-1.0% refining premium.
